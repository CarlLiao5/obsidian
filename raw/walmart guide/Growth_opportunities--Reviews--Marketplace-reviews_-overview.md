Overview

Reviews left on your Walmart Marketplace listings are regulated by the Federal Trade Commission (FTC) and must comply with applicable laws and [Marketplace guidelines](https://marketplacelearn.walmart.com/guides/Policies%20&%20standards/Product%20listings/Walmart-product-review-sampling-guidelines). In this guide, you’ll learn how ratings and reviews are generated and how you can manage them.

## How does it work?

If a customer has opted to receive emails from Walmart, they’ll receive an email seven days after delivery or pickup of a Marketplace item with a request to leave a review for the product and for the Marketplace seller. If the customer doesn’t open this email, Walmart sends a second email 28 days later. Customers who haven’t opted in to receive these emails won’t be asked to leave a review but still have the option to leave one in their purchase history in their Walmart.com account.

The customer’s feedback is collected and displayed on the [**Ratings and Reviews**](https://seller.walmart.com/partner-analytics/ratings-and-reviews/seller-reviews) page in Seller Center, where you can contact the customer, initiate a refund or track their order. You can use the **Item reviews** tab or **Seller reviews** tab to filter through specific topics or reviews. To review insights summarized at the item level, select the three horizontal dots in the **Actions** column and select _View all item reviews_.

The **Ratings and Reviews** page only displays feedback from customers who purchased your offer on Walmart.com. Syndicated reviews and item reviews for other sellers’ offers are not displayed.

## Frequently asked questions

* * *

Can I email customers to request feedback on items they have purchased?

No. Soliciting feedback, offering a gift card in exchange for a review or including any promotional inserts in packages is strictly prohibited and may result in account suspension or termination. Additionally, soliciting feedback from customers is prohibited unless it’s through a Walmart-approved reviews [provider](https://marketplacelearn.walmart.com/guides/Listing%20optimization/Reviews/Marketplace-reviews:-review-syndication).

* * *

Can I send customers a sample item for review?

Yes, by working with one of Walmart’s third-party approved sampling vendors:

- [Field Agent](https://plumshop.com/p/walmart-ratings-reviews)
- [Wholescale](https://wholescale.com/walmart)
- [The Brand Power Company](https://www.thebrandpowercompany.com/contact-us)
- [PowerReviews](https://www.powerreviews.com/walmart/)
- [Bazaarvoice](https://marketplace.walmart.com/bazaarvoice/)


For additional information about these tools and review samples, review the [Walmart product review sampling guidelines](https://marketplacelearn.walmart.com/guides/Policies%20&%20standards/Product%20listings/Walmart-product-review-sampling-guidelines?locale=en-US).

* * *

Why are my reviews missing?

When reviews aren't displaying on Walmart.com it is usually the result of a product family issue or syndication issue:

- Product families enable the sharing of reviews across items that have variants such as size, color, fabric, etc. Product families must be set up and maintained by the merchandising team. If you have questions, select the _Help_ button in the [Seller Center](https://seller.walmart.com/home) menu bar to contact Support or contact your merchant for assistance.
- Syndication facilitates the sharing of reviews from a supplier or brand website to Walmart.com. Items must be set up with an item ID and UPC in both the supplier’s catalog and the Walmart catalog. If your syndicated reviews aren't displaying, check your catalog to ensure that these necessary data points are present.

* * *

Why do the columns SKU, Fulfilled and Order ID sometimes show two dashes ("--") instead of a value?

This is a known issue due to historical data limits since our system only links data for reviews and orders from September 2023 onwards. This issue can't be corrected through a support ticket. We're working to address this by improving our internal linking capabilities.

* * *

How long does it take for a new seller or product review to post on Walmart.com?

New reviews will appear on the listing within five to seven business days. If a review isn't posted within that time, it may have violated one of the review guidelines.

* * *

Can customer reviews help with search engine optimization (SEO) and conversion?

Yes. Reviews may help you boost brand awareness and drive customer confidence, leading to better SEO results and conversions.

* * *

I want to remove a review from my listing. What should I do?

To request the removal of a review, visit [_Request to remove a review_](https://marketplacelearn.walmart.com/guides/Listing%20optimization/Troubleshooting/Request-to-remove-a-review).

* * *

Can a customer leave a seller review if their order was cancelled?

No.

* * *

Can a customer leave a seller review if they returned their items?

Yes.

* * *

How can I increase reviews for my items?

There are many ways to increase your Marketplace reviews:

- You can encourage your customers to submit more reviews with [Review Accelerator](https://marketplacelearn.walmart.com/guides/review-accelerator-overview).


- You can work with an approved third-party Solution Provider to conduct product sampling and generate authentic customer reviews.


- You can import reviews from your external website by [syndicating them to Walmart.com](https://marketplacelearn.walmart.com/guides/Listing%20optimization/Reviews/Marketplace-reviews:-review-syndication).


* * *

Can I browse reviews that have been rejected?

No. Only reviews that have passed moderation are made available.

* * *

Why do some reviews feature a strikethrough?

Reviews on items sold by sellers related to fulfillment issues caused by Walmart aren’t factored into the overall rating. Such reviews will be marked with a strikethrough and a message generally stating that Walmart fulfilled the order, and the fulfillment issues weren’t the fault of the seller.

* * *

How is the overall star rating calculated for an item?

Walmart calculates an overall rating for most items using a proprietary algorithm that considers reviewer customers’ star ratings along with various factors including recency of a review and community feedback. For items that don’t have enough ratings and reviews, the overall star rating is calculated using a simple average.

* * *

Are incentivized reviews considered in my rating?

Yes. An overall item rating may include ratings from incentivized reviews. Reviews are considered incentivized if the customer received free samples, discounts, payments or other compensation in exchange for their honest rating or review, whether positive or negative.

* * *

How does Walmart handle my reviews where the customer mentioned an issue where Walmart Fulfillment Services (WFS) is responsible?

We apply a strikethrough to these reviews under special circumstances. If WFS is at fault for a customer's complaint, such as late delivery, expired perishable items or damaged exterior packaging prepared by WFS, the review will remain on the site with a strikethrough and a statement explaining that the fulfillment issue was not the fault of the Seller.

These reviews won’t be removed because WFS was used. Carrier complaints such as not following delivery instructions or issues stemming from product quality or seller-prepared packaging do not qualify for a strikethrough or for removal. For more details on how to correctly prepare, pack and label a shipment, review this [guide](https://marketplacelearn.walmart.com/guides/Walmart%20Fulfillment%20Services%20(WFS)/Shipping%20to%20WFS/WFS-inbound-orders-Prepare-and-pack-shipments).

* * *

Do negative reviews impact my seller performance metrics?

Yes. When your average seller rating falls below 4.0, you may no longer be eligible for certain benefits, such as Pro Seller status. All Marketplace sellers must also maintain a Negative Feedback Rate of less than or equal to 2%.
