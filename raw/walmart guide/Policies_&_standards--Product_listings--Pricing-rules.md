Pricing rules

Last updated on Dec 11, 2025

Reading time: 4 min

* * *

In this guide:

- [Overview](https://marketplacelearn.walmart.com/guides/Policies%20&%20standards/Product%20listings/Pricing-rules#overview)

- [Frequently asked questions](https://marketplacelearn.walmart.com/guides/Policies%20&%20standards/Product%20listings/Pricing-rules#frequently-asked-questions)

Overview

At Walmart, we’ve enacted automated rules that identify and remove offers from Walmart.com that are so highly uncompetitive or unfairly priced they could potentially harm customers and impact the overall shopping experience. Failure to adhere to the pricing rules or other [Walmart Marketplace retailer policies](https://marketplacelearn.walmart.com/guides/Policies%20&%20standards/Account/Walmart-Marketplace-seller-retailer-policies) may result in account suspension and or termination. In this guide, you’ll learn more about these rules.

## What is the policy?

### Pricing rule

The Pricing Rule will automatically unpublish offers from Walmart Marketplace that are priced egregiously higher than prices currently or recently offered for the same item on Walmart.com, a competing website or priced at a level that could potentially be viewed as price gouging, or other unfair or abusive pricing practices. Walmart reviews the offer’s listing price and shipping fee to determine whether an offer is compliant with our Pricing Rule.

If any offers are flagged by this Pricing Rule, Walmart automatically unpublishes the offer in question. If you’ve subscribed to [Seller Center notifications](https://marketplacelearn.walmart.com/guides/Getting%20started/Account%20settings/Manage-notifications-in-Seller-Center), you’ll be notified by email and in Seller Center. Alternatively, you can check the [**Unpublished Items**](https://seller.walmart.com/catalog/unpublished-items) dashboard or download an [item report](https://marketplacelearn.walmart.com/guides/Catalog%20management/Reporting/Generate-an-item-report) in Seller Center to identify which items have been affected. The reason code will be listed as **Reasonable Price Not Satisfied** in the report.

Notes:

Market conditions change frequently. We encourage you to monitor prices on an ongoing basis to ensure you can remain competitive. Alternatively, you can use the [Repricer](https://marketplacelearn.walmart.com/guides/Catalog%20management/Price%20management/Repricer:-overview) to enable automatic price adjustments.

### Egregious Shipping Cost

The Egregious Shipping Cost rule may unpublish your offer or deem it ineligible for the Buy Box if your offer has extremely high shipping fees. Ensure shipping costs remain reasonably priced to remain competitive. If you try to add a shipping cost to your [Shipping Template](https://marketplacelearn.walmart.com/guides/Seller%20Fulfillment%20Services/Shipping%20Templates/Shipping-templates:-overview) that exceeds the allowed limit, the upload will be rejected and your account may be suspended.

Notes:

You’re limited to 10 shipping cost edits per day for each Shipping Template. If you exceed this threshold, your Shipping Template will be locked for 24 hours. Wait 24 hours before proceeding to edit the existing template.

### Buy Box Ineligibility

Walmart’s Buy Box features an offer based on factors like competitive pricing, shipping speed, shipping cost, inventory in-stock rates, high-quality listing content and post-purchase customer experience. In certain cases where the offer is priced substantially higher than prices currently or recently offered on Walmart.com or on competing sites, but not at a level requiring automatic unpublishing, we’ll remove the _Add to Cart_ button, as well as the offer details such as price and shipping from the item page. Customers can still purchase the item via the **More Seller Options** transaction flow.

This policy protects customers and Marketplace sellers. If your item is flagged by this rule, you can generate an [Item Report](https://marketplacelearn.walmart.com/guides/Catalog%20management/Reporting/Generate-an-item-report) in Seller Center to identify affected items or if you’ve subscribed to [Seller Center notifications](https://marketplacelearn.walmart.com/guides/Getting%20started/Account%20settings/Manage-notifications-in-Seller-Center), you’ll be notified by email and in Seller Center. If an item is not eligible for Buy Box, the Buy Box eligible column will be displayed as “No” in the Item Report.

## Frequently asked questions

* * *

My offer was unpublished but it’s listed by another seller on Walmart Marketplace for the same or a higher price. Why was my listing unpublished, but not other listings?

If you believe your item has been incorrectly unpublished, select the _Help_ button in the [Seller Center](https://seller.walmart.com/home) menu bar to contact Support _._ Provide the following information:

- Item ID

- Current Price

- Feed ID in which that price was sent

- The status Change Reason found in the **Item Report**

- A screenshot of the Buy Box for this item (if you have it and it’s relevant to your dispute)


* * *

I believe my item was incorrectly matched, which resulted in market conditions being inaccurately calculated. What should I do?

Review [_Submit an external price match_](https://marketplacelearn.walmart.com/guides/Catalog%20management/Troubleshooting/Submit-an-external-price-match) for guidance.

* * *

My offer was unpublished due to the Pricing Rule. How can I republish my offer?

If prices change on Walmart.com or monitored competing websites and the item now adheres to the pricing rules, Walmart will automatically republish the offer, typically within 48 hours. You can also proactively [update your price](https://marketplacelearn.walmart.com/guides/Catalog%20management/Price%20management/How-to-Update-Price:-Overview). If your offer remains unpublished after you've submitted a more competitive price, continue to adjust the price until your item no longer violates the Pricing Rule.

For any unpublished item, you can also find a **Reference price** on the [**Unpublished Items**](https://seller.walmart.com/catalog/unpublished-items) dashboard. You may use this reference price to help you update your listing to a more competitive price and get your item republished. Walmart calculates the reference price for each item using multiple external signals, such as the match price from competitor websites, historical selling price, Buy Box winner price, average offer price and Walmart e-commerce and store price.

* * *

My offer was unpublished due to a pricing error. What should I do?

In certain cases, Walmart may automatically unpublish offers that have been priced too low, to protect you from unintended losses and customers from experiencing cancellations. The reason code listed for such items is **Pricing Error**. In such a case, use the **Reference price** or other provided price signals as a reasonable benchmark or reference to increase price until it no longer violates the Pricing Rule. When the price is adhering to the pricing rules, Walmart will automatically republish the offer, which typically takes up to 48 hours.

If you intended to set a price that appears to be unintentionally low compared to the usual selling price, you may submit relevant documentation to demonstrate that the price is intentional and complies with Walmart’s Pricing Rules. To do this, select the _Help_ button in the [Seller Center](https://seller.walmart.com/home) menu bar to contact Support. Walmart will review such documentation at its sole discretion and may, but is not obligated to, reinstate the offer if it determines that the price does not violate its policies.

* * *

How do I reinstate my Buy Box eligibility?

[Update your price](https://marketplacelearn.walmart.com/guides/Catalog%20management/Price%20management/How-to-Update-Price:-Overview) to be more competitive. After you’ve updated the price, allow up to four hours to reinstate your eligibility.

* * *

Can I include my shipping fee in the item price itself?

While you’re allowed to include your shipping fee in the item price, including it may cause the price to become egregiously high compared to prices offered for same item on Walmart.com or external competing websites. In such cases, your offer may be flagged as a violation of our Pricing Rules and, therefore, unpublished or ineligible for the Buy Box. Update the shipping fees in your [Shipping Template](https://marketplacelearn.walmart.com/guides/Shipping%20&%20fulfillment/Shipping%20Templates/Shipping-templates:-manage-settings) for both your item price and the shipping fee to be competitive and avoid a Pricing Rule violation.

* * *

My offer was unpublished due to the Egregious Shipping Cost rule. How can I republish my offer?

Update the shipping fee listed in your [Shipping Template](https://marketplacelearn.walmart.com/guides/Shipping%20&%20fulfillment/Shipping%20Templates/Shipping-templates:-manage-settings) to be more competitive. Your offer is measured by the shipping price relative to item price.When the shipping fee for an unpublished offer falls back within an acceptable range, Walmart will automatically republish the offer, which typically takes up to 48 hours.

* * *

What do I do if my items remain unpublished?

If more than 48 hours have passed and you believe your offer remains incorrectly unpublished, select the _Help_ button in the [Seller Center](https://seller.walmart.com/home) menu bar to contact Support. Once you’ve confirmed the correct price and provided any necessary documentation, Walmart will review your request and may republish your offer if the price is adhering to the Pricing Rules. Walmart has pricing policies to protect both sellers and customers, so it's essential to ensure that your prices are accurate and comply with our Pricing Rules.

* * *

I was suspended due to Pricing Rules violations. Can I appeal this decision?

Yes. For instructions on how to appeal a suspension due to Pricing Rules violations, check out [_Appeal an Account Suspension or Suppression_](https://marketplacelearn.walmart.com/guides/Policies%20&%20standards/Troubleshooting/Appeal-an-account-suspension).

* * *

Why is my Resold item ineligible for the Buy Box?

All Resold items are subject to Buy Box ineligibility rules. To ensure a fair and transparent shopping experience for everyone, Walmart may also make Resold ( **Pre-owned**, **Restored** and **Open Box**) items ineligible for the Buy Box if their price exceeds that of a new item on the same product page. This policy helps protect customers from overpaying and maintains the integrity of Walmart Marketplace. Exceptions may apply for certain categories, such as collectibles or some luxury brands.
