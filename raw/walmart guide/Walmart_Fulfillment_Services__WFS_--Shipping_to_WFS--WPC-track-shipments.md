Walmart Preferred Carrier: Track shipments

Last updated on Jul 16, 2025

Reading time: 7 min

* * *

Overview

Once a Walmart preferred carrier has [picked up](https://marketplacelearn.walmart.com/guides/Walmart%20Fulfillment%20Services%20(WFS)/Shipping%20to%20WFS/WPC-prepare-pickup) your shipment, you can view delivery updates directly in Seller Center. In this guide, learn how to track a shipment and item receiving.

![](https://azure-na-images.contentstack.com/v3/assets/blta7903c6b840b702d/blt311a697778dc4a34/6877df3eee343026caaae082/WFS_Preferred-carrier-journey_Infographic.jpg?branch=us_mplearn)

## How to track a shipment

1. Log into Seller Center and go to the [**Shipping Plans**](https://seller.walmart.com/wfsLite/sendinventory) page.
2. Search for the plan or shipment ID, then open the dropdown.
3. Select _Track_ for a quick view of the shipment's progress.

The carrier will send status updates on the way to the Walmart facility. Estimated delivery is an estimate of when the shipment may arrive, based on the pickup date and average transit time. Actual delivery is when the carrier reported dropping off the shipment at the destination. This may be affected by the carrier’s operating hours or unexpected events, like severe weather.

If you ship hundreds of master cases through parcel, you'll get 1 master tracking number. This links all cases in the shipments and makes it easier to track all cases at once.

![](https://azure-na-images.contentstack.com/v3/assets/blta7903c6b840b702d/bltf5b0330f53082658/68754978ef20eff8ecebfd39/Send_domestic_track_carrier.png?branch=us_mplearn)

## How to track receiving

After a shipment has arrived, switch to the _Track items_ tab for a quick view of fill rates. For more details, you can select _View progress_. Items that have been properly packed and labeled will generally be checked in within 2 business days. However, items that require unplanned prep work, arrive during peak months (October 1–December 31) or other circumstances may take up to 10 days to process.

![](https://azure-na-images.contentstack.com/v3/assets/blta7903c6b840b702d/bltac622757cf68217c/68754978ee34306105aaad21/Send_domestic_track_items.png?branch=us_mplearn)

## Frequently asked questions

* * *

Why does the carrier website say the shipment was delivered, but the Shipping Plans page doesn't?

The carrier likely delivered the shipment to the facility’s yard, but we haven’t scanned and started receiving it yet. Please check back after 2 business days. If receiving still hasn't started, contact Support by selecting the _Help_ button in the [Seller Center](https://seller.walmart.com/home) menu bar.

* * *

What if I don’t see tracking details in Seller Center?

In rare cases, updates may not appear due to technical issues or because the carrier isn't integrated with our system. If this happens, please go to the carrier website to track the shipment.

* * *

What if there’s a discrepancy between my shipped and received units?

If receiving is complete and there’s a shortage, you can [create a dispute](https://marketplacelearn.walmart.com/guides/Walmart%20Fulfillment%20Services%20(WFS)/Troubleshooting/Troubleshoot-WFS-receiving-issues:-open-a-dispute-claim) for us to investigate. Claims must be filed **between 10 and 50 days** from the time of delivery.

* * *

How do I view final shipping fees?

You can view final charges on the [**Payments**](https://seller.walmart.com/partner-analytics/payments/statements/period) page of Seller Center after each payment cycle. For more detail, you can also download the [Inbound transportation report](https://seller.walmart.com/wfsLite/reports).
