Payment activities

Overview

When reviewing your [payment statement](https://marketplacelearn.walmart.com/guides/Taxes%20&%20payments/Payments/Payment-statements-and-transactions), you may encounter various payment activities that may delay the disbursement of your funds. Some of these activities include account-level **Payment reserves**, **Payment holds** or **Payment suspensions**. There are a number of reasons why you may encounter one of these payment activities and in this guide, you’ll learn more about them.

Notes:

This material has been prepared for informational purposes only and is not intended to provide and should not be relied on for legal advice. If you should have any questions about this information, you should consult your own legal advisors.

## How does it work?

- **Payment reserves:** It is a normal part of selling on Walmart Marketplace to see an account-level reserve in your statement summary. Reserves indicate a fixed amount of money set aside to cover expected refunds, chargebacks or other risks. Note that payment reserves will appear on the Statements page as one line item, but your reserve total may comprise multiple reserves for different purposes. If you are importing with [Walmart Cross Border,](https://marketplace.walmart.com/walmart-cross-border/) you may see a payment reserve set aside to cover your expected costs for shipments in progress. For more information, check out this [guide](https://marketplacelearn.walmart.com/guides/Walmart%20Fulfillment%20Services%20(WFS)/WFS%20programs%20&%20services/walmart-cross-border-imports-shipping-charges-billing-and-payment).

- **Payment holds:** All Marketplace orders are eligible for payment holds seven or more days after the order has been shipped.  Payment holds indicate a delay on payments for a specified number of calendar days. The payment hold timeframe begins after the order has shipped.



Once the payment hold timeframe has ended, the funds will be processed in the next scheduled settlement cycle date.



Depending on the payment hold timeframe that has been applied, it can take up to a month or more to receive funds after an order was shipped. This is because settlement cycles occur bi-weekly on alternate Fridays at midnight Pacific Standard Time (PST).



If you’re a new seller to Walmart Marketplace, you’ll experience a [new seller payment hold](https://marketplacelearn.walmart.com/guides/Taxes%20&%20payments/Payments/new-seller-payment-hold-policy) until the requirements outlined in our policy are met.

- **Payment suspensions:** Payments on your account can be temporarily suspended for various reasons. Once suspended, you won’t receive any funds until the suspension is lifted. Suspensions can vary in length and typically last until a review of your account is completed.

- **Final settlement payouts:** A final settlement payout is the final account balance to be paid out for funds owed to a seller after their account is set to be closed or terminated. This includes the net amount of sales proceeds remaining after deductions for refunds, chargebacks and any other amounts owed are resolved. [Walmart’s final settlement payout](https://marketplacelearn.walmart.com/guides/final-settlement-payout-policy) eligibility decisions are based on your adherence to the [Comprehensive Walmart Marketplace Program Retailer Agreement](https://azure-na-assets.contentstack.com/v3/assets/blta7903c6b840b702d/blte9f8c035c2517009/Comprehensive%20Walmart%20Marketplace%20Retailer%20Agreement%20-%207.20.2024.pdf?branch=us_mplearn), [Walmart Marketplace Seller Retailer Policies](https://marketplacelearn.walmart.com/guides/Policies%20&%20standards/Account/Walmart-Marketplace-seller-retailer-policies) and [Seller Performance Standards](https://marketplacelearn.walmart.com/guides/Policies%20&%20standards/Performance/Seller-performance-standards).

## Additional guidelines

There are a number of reasons why you may encounter one or more of these payment activities. If you’ve encountered one of these payment activities, your account will go into review. Upon completing a risk assessment, Walmart will either release payments and return you to a normal payment schedule or take additional steps, which may include a reevaluation of your account status. High-level reasons to encounter a payment activity can be related to your:

- Compliance
- Customer orders
- Payment service provider
- Performance or risk reviews
- Shipping
- Anticipated invoices
- Unusual transactions
- Legal

Notes:

Your payments will be suspended if there's an ongoing legal issue or case on your seller account. Once all your legal cases are resolved and if there are no other payment activities on your account, your payment will be released on your next payment cycle.

## Frequently asked questions

* * *

How long does it take for payment holds to be lifted for new sellers?

Review the [New seller payment hold policy](https://marketplacelearn.walmart.com/guides/Taxes%20&%20payments/Payments/new-seller-payment-hold-policy) for more information.

* * *

I have a question about a payment activity. Where can I get help?

U.S. and international sellers with questions on payment holds should select the _Help_ button in the [Seller Center](https://seller.walmart.com/home) menu bar to contact Support.

* * *

What's considered a legal issue?

Legal issues can include a Temporary Restraining Order (TRO), a litigation case, CA Proposition 65 or a Uniform Commercial Code (UCC) Lien or other similar issues.

* * *

What’s a Temporary Restraining Order (TRO)?

A TRO is a legal order issued by a court to temporarily prohibit certain actions or behavior. This could include issues such as selling counterfeit products, violating pricing policies or engaging in fraudulent activities. The TRO will temporarily restrict you from receiving payments; most of the TROs are sealed.

* * *

What’s a litigation case?

A litigation case refers to third-party legal actions or disputes filed in court against you that may arise and relate to your products. These disputes can involve a variety of issues such as breach of contract, intellectual property infringement, unfair competition, false advertising, or violations of consumer protection laws.

* * *

What’s CA Proposition 65?

[Proposition 65](https://marketplacelearn.walmart.com/guides/Policies%20&%20standards/Prohibited%20products%20&%20brands/Proposition-65) prohibits California businesses from knowingly discharging significant amounts of listed chemicals into sources of drinking water. Proposition 65 requires California to publish a list of chemicals known to cause cancer, birth defects or other reproductive harm.

* * *

What’s a UCC Lien?

A Uniform Commercial Code filing, or UCC filing, is a form of notice that lenders use when securing a borrower’s loan or credit with an asset or group of assets. This enables lenders to seize the listed property as a way of recouping funds in the case of borrower default.
