Seller Center Inbox: Message management

Reading time: 3 min

Overview

If a customer or a Walmart Customer Care agent has a question about an order, their inquiry is sent to your Seller Center [**Inbox**](https://seller.walmart.com/seller-communication/Customer) or your designated customer care email address. You’ll also receive updates from Walmart, including information on policy changes and new product launches. In this guide, we’ll cover how to navigate your **Inbox** and respond to customer messages.

Notes:

Responses to customers must comply with Walmart’s [Customer Care Policy](https://marketplacelearn.stg.walmart.com/guides/Order%20management/Customer%20care/Customer-Care-policy), as well as any other [retailer policies](https://marketplacelearn.walmart.com/guides/Policies%20&%20standards/Account/Walmart-Marketplace-seller-retailer-policies). Failure to adhere to these guidelines when responding to inquiries could put your account at risk of suspension or termination.

## Manage your messages

### Step 1 – Get started

Navigate to your [**Inbox**](https://seller.walmart.com/seller-communication/Customer) in Seller Center by selecting the envelope icon in the top menu bar. Messages are organized into two tabs: **Customer** and **Walmart**. The number displayed next to each tab indicates the number of unread messages from the last 15 days.

### Step 2 – Review messages

An alert appears at the top of your **Inbox** if you have any customer messages that require your immediate attention. You can review, flag and mark messages in your Inbox that need further attention. Select the tab to view the corresponding message type:

- **Customer**: Customer-related messages include order details to help you investigate and quickly resolve inquiries. Select the message from the message list to view the full customer message, along with their address and detailed order information. Order details aren’t included if the customer message is pre-transaction.

Notes:

You’re required to reply to customer messages within 48 hours.

- **Walmart:** These messages provide information about policy updates, product launches and other key initiatives. Time-sensitive messages are indicated by an alert symbol next to the date. Walmart messages are for one-way communication only and can’t be responded to.

Pro tip

You can filter **Customer** messages by type (flagged, response needed, customer care or spam) or by status (all, unread, read or failed). You can only filter **Walmart** messages by status (all, unread, read or flagged).

### Step 3 – Respond to messages

As stated in the [Customer Care Policy](https://marketplacelearn.walmart.com/guides/Order%20management/Customer%20care/Customer-Care-policy),customer messages require a timely response. Follow our [response guidelines](https://azure-na-assets.contentstack.com/v3/assets/blta7903c6b840b702d/blt91cf8d87bb9f8442/Guide-for-responding-customer-messages.pdf?branch=us_mplearn) to manually enter a response or choose from a pre-defined message template (if applicable). If needed, you can also attach supporting documentation. You can include up to five attachments in your email reply. Each attachment must be 5 MB or smaller to ensure successful delivery.

Under certain circumstances, a response may not be required. If this applies, select the _No response needed_ link.

After you've finished writing your message, select _Send Reply_.

## Frequently asked questions

* * *

Can I send auto-reply messages to customers?

Walmart doesn’t consider auto-reply messages to be high-value communication. You must reply personally to customers. You can use [templates](https://marketplacelearn.walmart.com/guides/Create-a-new-customer-email-template) to build your responses as needed.

* * *

How do I create a message template?

Review [_Create a new customer email template_](https://marketplacelearn.walmart.com/guides/Order%20management/Customer%20care/Create-a-new-customer-email-template) for more information.

* * *

Can I offer alternative solutions in place of a return request?

When a customer requests a return, you’re prohibited from negotiating alternative solutions, unless explicitly authorized through Walmart’s [Partial Keep It Rules](https://marketplacelearn.walmart.com/guides/Order%20management/Returns%20&%20refunds/Add-a-Keep-It-Rule) or other pre-authorized resolutions. If you’d like to offer a customer the option to keep an item at a discounted price, you must set the discount threshold using the designated Partial Keep It Rules before the time of purchase.

* * *

Will I be penalized for selecting the “No response needed” option instead of replying to a customer message?

No. Choosing _No response needed_ doesn't impact your [Seller Response Rate](https://marketplacelearn.walmart.com/guides/Policies%20&%20standards/Performance/Seller-performance-standards).
