Guide

No haggling policy

Last updated on Dec 10, 2025

Reading time: 1 min

* * *

In this guide:

- [Overview](https://marketplacelearn.walmart.com/guides/Policies%20&%20standards/Orders%20&%20returns/no-haggling-policy#overview)
- [What is the policy?](https://marketplacelearn.walmart.com/guides/Policies%20&%20standards/Orders%20&%20returns/no-haggling-policy#what)
- [Additional guidelines](https://marketplacelearn.walmart.com/guides/Policies%20&%20standards/Orders%20&%20returns/no-haggling-policy#additional)

* * *

Overview

For a fair and easy returns experience for our customers, haggling, which is the negotiation of returns and refunds, isn’t allowed. In this guide, you’ll learn more about the policy.

## What is the policy?

When a customer requests a return, you’re prohibited from negotiating alternative solutions, unless explicitly authorized through Walmart's [Partial Keep It Rules](https://marketplacelearn.walmart.com/guides/Order%20management/Returns%20&%20refunds/Add-a-Keep-It-Rule) or [other pre-approved resolutions](https://marketplacelearn.walmart.com/guides/Order%20management/Returns%20&%20refunds/returns-policy). If you’d like to offer a customer the option to keep an item at a discounted price, you must set the discount threshold using the designated Partial Keep It Rules before the time of purchase. If a customer declines the partial refund, you must process a return request.

Notes:

Walmart reserves the right to suspend or terminate sellers who don’t adhere to Partial Keep It Rules and try to haggle with customers.

## Additional guidelines

This table outlines haggling policy allowances and restrictions.

| Allowed | Prohibited |
| --- | --- |
| - Addressing customer concerns before a return is initiated by offering solutions aligned with Walmart’s policies, as long as you don’t pressure customers into waiving their right to a return or refund.<br>- Processing a partial refund if a Partial Keep It Rule was created before the time of purchase. <br>- Engaging with customers to process a returns exemption. | - Offering a partial refund in the absence of a Partial Keep It Rule. <br>- Offering alternative items as replacements. <br>- Offering store credit or other incentives to avoid a return. |
