Tax classifications and documentation

Overview

If you intend on selling on the U.S. Marketplace, you’re required to provide a business tax ID or business license number, and supporting documentation to confirm your company’s credentials. Keep reading below to find out your tax classification and what documentation is required for your country of incorporation.

## How does it work?

### W-9

If you're a U.S.-based seller with an Employer Identification Number (EIN) and your country of incorporation is in the United States, your tax classification is W-9. We may ask for additional documentation, such as:

- An IRS-issued EIN verification letter (IRS LTR 147C) or IRS-issued SS4 confirmation letter (IRS LTR CP 575).
- A state-issued business license, registration certificate, articles of incorporation or a Certificate of Good Standing.

If you don't have a U.S. Business Tax ID, you can apply for one [here](https://www.irs.gov/businesses/small-businesses-self-employed/apply-for-an-employer-identification-number-ein-online).

Pro tip

If you don’t have a U.S. state registration number, you can get it from your state's Secretary of State website (or a similar business filing division).

### W-8ECI

If you have an Employer Identification Number (EIN) and your country of incorporation isn’t the United States, your tax classification is W-8ECI. For supported Countries of Incorporation (COIs), you can onboard by providing your COI tax ID details on the business verification tile. For certain COIs, we don’t accept COI tax ID and you may have to provide a U.S. tax ID to set up your account.

### W-8BEN-E

If you don't have an EIN and your country of incorporation is not the United States, your tax classification is W-8BEN-E. We support this tax classification for sellers from China, Hong Kong, the United Kingdom of Great Britain and Northern Ireland (U.K.), Japan, Canada, Mexico, India, Singapore, South Korea, Taiwan, Germany, Vietnam, Thailand, Chile and Turkey.

### W-8BEN

We only support this tax classification if you’re a sole proprietor from Mexico or India, i.e., an individual who owns and operates an unincorporated business by themselves. If you're a sole proprietor from Mexico, you can only onboard to the Mexico market.

## Frequently asked questions

* * *

I accidentally provided incorrect business information and my business verification failed. Can I re-submit?

Yes. As a reminder, your business details must be entered exactly as they appear in your IRS records or other government issued documents. If your application still appears as "Failed" after you resubmit, Walmart has determined your business does not currently meet Walmart Marketplace requirements.

* * *

How long does the business verification take?

Business verification can vary from a few minutes to a few business days.

* * *

Why is the business verification title saying “Failed”?

This typically occurs when Walmart is unable to verify the information you provided. Review your business information and, if you find incorrect information or have questions, select the _Help_ button in the [Seller Center](https://seller.walmart.com/home) menu bar to contact Support. Make sure to mention that you need to correct the business information on your application in the case description.

* * *

I need to update my tax details. How do I do that?

To update and manage your tax details, visit [_Update tax information in Seller Center_](https://marketplacelearn.walmart.com/guides/Getting%20started/Account%20settings/Update-tax-information-in-Seller-Center).

* * *

How often do I need to renew my documentation?

W-9s don’t expire. W-8ECI and W-8BEN-E documentation needs to be renewed every three years.

* * *

How can I add a market?

Select the U.S. flag icon on the homepage in Seller Center and choose the market you wish to begin selling in. Follow the on-screen instructions to complete account set up for your new market. For a step-by-step tutorial, review [_Global onboarding: Set up an additional market_.](https://marketplacelearn.walmart.com/guides/Getting%20started/Onboarding/global-onboarding-set-up-an-additional-market)
