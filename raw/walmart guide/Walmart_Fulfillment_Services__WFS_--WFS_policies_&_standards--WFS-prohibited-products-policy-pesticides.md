Overview

Each prohibited product category has unique requirements that should be followed, or Walmart will automatically unpublish your items. To read the full policy, please read the [Walmart Fulfillment Services Prohibited Products Policy](https://marketplacelearn.walmart.com/guides/Walmart%20Fulfillment%20Services%20(WFS)/WFS%20item%20setup/wfs-prohibited-products-policy) and the [Prohibited Products Policy for Marketplace Sellers](https://marketplacelearn.walmart.com/guides/Policies%20&%20standards/Prohibited%20products%20&%20brands/Prohibited-products-policy:-overview).

Notes:

The contents of this document are subject to change without notice, so please check this page often. Sellers are responsible for ensuring that their items comply with this policy and any future amended or updated versions.

## What is the policy?

Pesticide is defined by Walmart to include any item of merchandise which is advertised and/or labeled with direct or implied **claims to kill, repel or prevent the growth of any living organism** (e.g., antimicrobial, antibacterial, antifungal).

Pesticides and articles treated with pesticides are regulated by the Environmental Protection Agency (EPA) and must comply with the Federal Insecticide, Fungicide, and Rodenticide Act (FIFRA), including all registration and labeling requirements. EPA registered pesticides, 25(b) minimum risk pesticides, pesticide devices and treated articles with pesticide claims are **currently ineligible for WFS**.

- **Pesticides:** A pesticide is a substance or mixture of substances intended to prevent, destroy, repel, mitigate, or attract any pest.

- **Pesticide devices:** A pesticide device is any instrument or contrivance intended for trapping, destroying, repelling, or mitigating any pest.

- **Treated articles with pesticide claims:** A treated article is an item treated with an antimicrobial pesticide to protect the item itself that makes pesticide claims.


Pests include, but are not limited to:

- Microbes (bacteria, viruses, etc.)

- Rodents (rats, mice squirrels,  etc.)

- Mold / Mildew

- Insects

- Fungus

- Weeds


Additional information on pesticides and pesticide devices can be found in our [What Is a Pesticide](https://gecrm.my.salesforce.com/sfc/p/#61000000ZKTc/a/4M0000005TcH/aYWLRpxO2cKxUBeU4QZEVASB1eOd033Oo8fjj7w8t.k) guide. In addition to the WFS guidelines above, all sellers must adhere to the [Marketplace Hazardous Items](https://marketplacelearn.walmart.com/guides/prohibited-products-policy-hazardous-items) policy.

## Policy basics

|     |     |
| --- | --- |
| Prohibited | All pesticides. Examples include:<br>- Herbicides<br>  <br>- Insecticides<br>  <br>- Fungicides<br>  <br>- Repellants<br>  <br>All pesticide devices. Examples include:<br>- Air purifiers designed or marketed to sanitize or remove pests such as microbes, bacteria or mold<br>  <br>- Insect traps<br>  <br>- UV lights designed or marketed to kill, remove, repel or sanitize pests<br>  <br>Treated articles with pesticide claims<br>- Cutting boards, kitchen sponges, cat litter, toothbrushes, toys etc. with claims such as “Antimicrobial properties are built-in to inhibit the growth of bacteria that may affect this product” |
| Allowed with restrictions | If pesticide claims are not present:<br>- Household cleaners<br>  <br>- Cleaning wipes for surfaces |
| Allowed | Devices that are not regulated by the EPA, including, but not limited to:<br>- Fly swatters<br>  <br>- Fish traps<br>  <br>- Traps for vertebrate animals including rats and mice<br>  <br>- Pesticide application equipment that's sold separately from the pesticide itself<br>  <br>See Related guides for more information on antimicrobial and drug products. |
