Overview

As a Marketplace seller, you pay a Referral Fee for each product you sell on Walmart Marketplace. The Referral Fee is determined by the contract category (also known as the product type) of your item.

## What is a Referral Fee?

The **Referral Fee** is a percentage of the gross sales of each product sold. The Referral Fee is determined by the product type that Walmart assigns to your items during the item setup process.

## What is a contract category?

Because Referral Fees vary by category, it’s important to know which contract category your items are assigned. A contract category (or product type) corresponds to the type of items sold on Marketplace.

## Where can I find my Referral Fee?

Navigate to your [**Statements**](https://seller.walmart.com/payments/statements) page. The contract category column displays the contract category for each of your items. The commission rate column displays the Referral Fee percentage assigned to your products.

## Referral Fee schedule

Total sales price includes item price as well as shipping and handling, gift wrap and other charges. Lower Referral Fees may be applicable to some of your items (depending on the total sales price), if you sell products in the following categories:

- Baby
- Beauty
- Electronics Accessories
- Grocery
- Health & Personal Care

The Walmart Marketplace Referral Fee schedule for contract categories is listed below.
