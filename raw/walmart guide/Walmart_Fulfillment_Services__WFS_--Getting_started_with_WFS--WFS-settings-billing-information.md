WFS settings: Add billing information

Overview

To use Walmart Fulfillment Services (WFS), you’re required to add a billing method in Seller Center. This allows you to automatically pay any outstanding WFS charges — including fulfillment fees, storage fees and customer refunds — at the end of your Marketplace settlement cycle. You can use a business credit card or a US bank account through ACH. In this guide, you’ll learn how to add or update your billing method in Seller Center.

## How does it work?

Notes:

You must have Admin or Read and Write access to manage billing. If you don’t have access, reach out to your account administrator for help.

We need your billing information to automate payments for value-added services, customer refunds and chargebacks. We’ll only charge you if your sales revenue doesn’t cover the fees. We do not charge any processing fees. Check your charges at the end of each settlement cycle on the [Payments](https://seller.walmart.com/payments/statements/period) page, or download the [Settlement Report](https://seller.walmart.com/wfs/reports) for more detail.

For credit cards, we accept Visa, Mastercard, American Express, Discover, Japan Credit Bureau (JCB) and Union Pay. The credit card should be a business credit card that is not used for personal, family or household purposes. We accept bank accounts from all US banks running on the Automated Clearing House (ACH) network, such as Citibank and Bank of America.

## Add billing

Setting up automated payments is a one-time process. Here’s how:

1. Log into Seller Center and navigate to the [**Billing Services**](https://seller.walmart.com/settings/financial-settings/billing-services) page.
2. Select _Add billing method_, then choose a credit card or ACH bank account.

3. Enter the required information for the method and submit.


### Verify bank account

If you choose to connect a bank account, we’ll use microdeposits to safely verify it. You’ll receive two deposits of $0.01–$0.99. When the deposits reach your account, confirm the amounts by selecting Verify account.

Account verification usually takes 2–3 business days. After verification is complete, you can set the account as your default billing method. The microdeposits will be withdrawn from your account typically within 3 business days.

## Update billing

If you need to make changes, select the pencil icon next to the billing method. You must always have at least one billing method on file. If you only have a single billing method, you won’t be able to delete it until you add an alternative billing method first.

## Frequently asked questions

* * *

Is the billing method the same as the payment method?

No, they’re different. The payment method is for receiving sales revenue and adjustments from Walmart Marketplace via payment service providers. Billing method is solely for paying service fees and outstanding dues to Walmart Marketplace.

* * *

Why can't I submit a billing method?

Only administrators can add or edit information in the Billing Services page. If you can't access it, you'll need to reach out to your account's administrator. If you are an administrator and the page fails to load, try incognito or private mode in your browser. If you still can't submit billing information, select the _Help_ button in the Seller Center menu bar to contact Support.

* * *

Can I pay service charges with more than one billing method?

No. Walmart will only charge the default billing method listed on your account. You can change your default method at any time.

* * *

How frequently am I billed?

Your billing cycle will match your current settlement cycle. For example, if your settlement cycle is every 2 weeks, then your billing cycle will also be every 2 weeks.

* * *

What should I do if my bank account is rejected?

If you’re using a valid bank account, check that the routing and account numbers are correct. If you continue to experience issues, please contact your bank.

Notes

Please do not request microdeposits multiple times. Only do so if there are errors in the information.

* * *

What should I do if my bill payment failed?

If your payment failed, check your billing method to make sure that there are sufficient funds and no active holds. If you continue to experience issues, please contact your bank or credit card provider.

* * *

What should I do if I haven't received the microdeposits or if the account verification failed?

This can happen if you've placed restrictions on your bank account or your bank account doesn’t accept ACH deposits or credits. If your bank confirms the transaction was blocked, you can do the following:

- **Bank confirms blocked transaction due to restriction:** Request the restriction be removed or an exception be made. Return to Seller Center and try to verify your account again.

- **Provide the following ACH IDs if asked:** PAYPALMTCU, PaypalBTCU, PAYPALMTBU, WFMSBRNT01 and 2205717726

- **Bank doesn’t accept ACH deposits:** Start the process again from _Use different bank account_.

* * *

I entered the microdeposits. Why do I still see a Verify account option next to my bank account in Seller Center?

Verification usually takes 2 to 3 business days. If you’ve entered the microdeposits, no further action is needed from you. Once verification is complete, you can set the account as your default billing method.
