Business information policy

Overview

To sell on Walmart.com, you must provide accurate and current business information (e.g., a valid business name and phone number). In accordance with applicable law, certain Marketplace sellers must verify their business information every year. In this guide, you’ll learn more about the Business Information Policy and how to verify your business information.

Notes:

Walmart may display your business information provided on product listings and/or seller pages on Walmart.com.

## What is the policy?

In accordance with applicable law, such as the Federal INFORM Consumers Act and similar state laws, Walmart may reach out to you to verify your business information. You may be required to verify your business information on file in Seller Center, by email or by phone. You must respond to each inquiry from Walmart in a timely manner. Failure to do so may result in consequences, up to and including suspension of your Marketplace account.

If you use a third-party seller to supply an item that is purchased on Walmart.com, you must inform Walmart that you intend to use a third-party seller to supply your items and provide Walmart and/or consumers with the name and contact information of the third-party seller. To do this, send an email to [SubSeller@walmart.com](mailto:SubSeller@walmart.com) with the subject "Third Party Seller Notice." Then, provide Walmart and/or consumers with the name and contact information of the third-party seller.

## Seller Certification

### Step 1 – Get started

Navigate to the [**Seller Certification**](https://seller.walmart.com/settings/administrator-options/seller-certification) page in Seller Center. If you have administrator access, select the _Account settings_ menu and choose _Seller Certification_ under the Administrator Options section.

### Step 2 – Review and update information

Review the information on the [**Seller Certification**](https://seller.walmart.com/settings/administrator-options/seller-certification) page. To update your business information, select the _Edit Business Profile_ tab. To update your customer service contacts, navigate to the **Customer service contact** section and select Change contact. If you’re completing the certification for the first time, enter your customer service phone number and email address carefully to avoid verification delays.

![2503_Business-info-policy_screenshot.png](https://azure-na-images.contentstack.com/v3/assets/blta7903c6b840b702d/bltbbb887984c9fac46/67ed5e7b226f4c7b4f07d2cc/2503_Business-info-policy_screenshot.png?branch=us_mplearn)

### Step 3 – Select phone verification method (U.S./China sellers only)

Walmart can verify your number through a one-time passcode (OTP) delivered by text message. Landline and Voice Over Internet Protocol (VOIP) numbers can’t receive an OTP.

If you don’t receive the OTP or you can’t verify your number through this method, select _Complete phone verification later_ and Walmart will contact you by phone.

![2410_8229_Screenshot-2.png](https://azure-na-images.contentstack.com/v3/assets/blta7903c6b840b702d/blt8bf864e7c300afd2/67291376adab677bf9f8c9ba/2410_8229_Screenshot-2.png?branch=us_mplearn)

### Step 4 – Submit

Check your information to make sure everything is correct. Then, select _Submit Certification_. Visit the [**Seller Certification**](https://seller.walmart.com/settings/administrator-options/seller-certification) page in Seller Center to view your certification status, check for updates and make any changes. If we can’t verify your information, we’ll notify you through Seller Center and by email.

## Frequently asked questions

* * *

Will I be notified prior to Walmart imposing a consequence?

Walmart will inform you of a pending consequence and will inform you of the time to respond prior to the consequence taking effect. Walmart will communicate with you using Seller Center and using the information on file. Be sure to regularly log in to your Seller Center account and if your contact information changes, be sure to update your Seller Center profile to ensure messages reach you.

* * *

How will I know if I need to submit information for certification?

An email will be sent to your primary business contact asking you to certify your information. You’ll also see a prompt when you log in to Seller Center that will let you certify your information and make any changes if needed.

* * *

What if I receive an email from an unknown address requesting that I verify my seller account information?

Never respond to unknown email addresses as they may be a phishing attempt. Remain alert to bad actors attempting to obtain your business information. If you are in doubt as to whether a request for information or verification is coming from Walmart, select the _Help_ button in the [Seller Center](https://seller.walmart.com/home) menu bar to contact Support.

* * *

Do I need to certify my business information?

All third-party Marketplace sellers who have made $5,000 or more in gross revenue and have sold 200 or more new or unused consumer products on Walmart Marketplace, must certify their business information annually.

* * *

How long does it take for Walmart to verify my information?

It may take seven to 10 business days for Walmart to verify your business information.

* * *

Will my business information be visible to Walmart customers?

Your business contact information and business name are visible on your seller page on Walmart.com.

* * *

If I have been previously penalized because of non-certification, how long does it take to reinstate my account post-verification?

Once your business information has been submitted, it may take at least 24 hours to reinstate your account.  It may take longer if additional verification is required.

* * *

I’ve already completed the annual certification, but I need to update my business information. Where can this be done?

To make changes to your business address, legal name and tax related information, you’ll need to submit changes via your [**Tax Profile**](https://seller.walmart.com/settings/partner-profile/tax-profile) page in Seller Center. To make changes to your customer service contact, you’ll need to submit them via the [**Manage contacts**](https://seller.walmart.com/settings/partner-profile/manage-contacts) dashboard in Seller Center.

* * *

I need more help. What should I do?

If you have questions, select the _Help_ button in the [Seller Center](https://seller.walmart.com/home) menu bar to contact Support.
