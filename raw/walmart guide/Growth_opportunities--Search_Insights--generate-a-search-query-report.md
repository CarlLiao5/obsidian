Search Insights: Generate a search query report

Overview

The **Search Query Report** within [**Search Insights**](https://seller.walmart.com/analytics/search-insights/) in Seller Centerhelps you identify growth opportunities by providing keywords customers use to find products similar to yours. In this guide, you’ll learn how to generate the report and use insights to optimize your product listings.

## Generate report

### Step 1 — Get started

Navigate to [**Search Insights**](https://seller.walmart.com/analytics/search-insights/) in Seller Center. Go to the _Search query reports_ tab. Search for an item in your Catalog and enter a date range. You can choose a month or a calendar week.

### Step 2 — Download the report

Next, select _Generate report_ to download the report.

![](https://azure-na-images.contentstack.com/v3/assets/blta7903c6b840b702d/bltb3bf1fd7cadf25b1/66cca05f9f118079e551fd43/12177_gif1.gif?branch=us_mplearn)

### Search query report metrics

The Search Query Report gives you an in-depth view of the following:

|     |     |
| --- | --- |
| - **Added to cart share:** This indicates the percentage of ATC received by the item compared to all other items impressed for their query. It also represents the likelihood of the item being added to the cart after appearing in search results. <br>  <br>- **Click Share:** Click shareindicates the percentage of clicks an item receives compared to all other items impressed for that query.  <br>- **Frequency Rank:** The frequency rank indicates how popular a query is. (Ex. Rank 500 indicates that the phrase is the 500th most popular query on Walmart in the selected time range). | - **Impressions Share:** This indicates the percentage of impressions received by the item compared to all other items impressed for that query. <br>- **Number of Competing Products:** This represents the number of items that appeared in search results for which the associated query was searched. Helps you identify the competing products displayed to customers. <br>  <br>- **Number One Clicked Product:** The top clicked product after this query phrase was searched.  <br>  <br>- **Query Score:** This represents the rank of the search query based on traffic and sales to this item. Lower rank indicates higher traffic share. This score helps you identify the top performing queries for an item. |

Notes:

Data is only from search results and doesn’t include data from other product discovery experiences visible on Walmart platforms.

## Additional guidelines

To have the best possible performance on search, we recommend focusing on implementing [Listing Quality recommendations](https://marketplacelearn.walmart.com/guides/Listing%20optimization/Items%20and%20inventory/Listing-quality-and-rewards-dashboard) and verifying your assigned product type is correct.

## Frequently asked questions

* * *

How’s the search query score assigned for a particular query?

The search query score is based on how much traffic your item has gotten from a particular query, in comparison with all other items displayed for that search query.

* * *

What are some of the benefits of using the Search Query Report?

The search query report is a valuable tool to help you find growth opportunities on [Walmart.com](https://www.walmart.com/) search.  You can use the query report to understand which queries your item performs well for today, compared to what’s popular with customers. In addition, you can learn what your share of traffic and sales is for popular search queries.

With an understanding of the queries customers typically use to find products like yours, you can enhance your listing content to be more relevant to your target customers. Additionally, you can use sponsored search to promote items on search.

* * *

Why is there no data for my report despite having sales?

This report covers data only from search results on [Walmart.com](https://www.walmart.com/) and mobile apps. and does not include other product discovery experiences. The item may not have history on onsite search in the time range that you have selected.

* * *

Why is the data here different from my item sales performance?

This report covers data only from search results on [Walmart.com](https://www.walmart.com/) and mobile apps. Sales driven by traffic and clicks from other channels or personalized product experiences on the website aren't part of the report.

* * *

Why do I have a high Search Query score but a low impression, click and ATC share for a query?

Search Query score is based on an individual item’s performance for an associated query. It helps you understand which query brings the most traffic and sales for an item. However, it doesn’t consider the performance of competing items, which is reflected in the share metrics.
