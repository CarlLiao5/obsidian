WFS trade item configuration

Overview

When you set up a Walmart-fulfilled item, we ask for trade item configuration. That means the quantity, weight and dimensions of an item, including any packaging. In this guide, learn how to measure an item and fill in trade item configuration.

## Why does trade item configuration matter?

We need item dimensions, including packaging, to properly store your item at a fulfillment center. For example, dimensions help us decide if items can fit in totes or shelves.

Item dimensions, along with weight, also determine your fees for Walmart Fulfillment Services (WFS). We measure items during receiving, and mismatched dimensions may affect the final charges. While we do not provide images of our measurements, you can download the [Storage report](https://seller.walmart.com/wfsLite/reports) to compare dimensions.

## How to measure an item

The dimensions and weight for your item must include packaging.

- **Width:** Measure the shortest side in inches, including packaging
- **Depth:** Measure the longest side in inches, including packaging
- **Height:** Measure the remaining side in inches, including packaging
- **Weight:** Weigh the item in pounds, including packaging

Notes:

Don’t want to measure your item? Try [searching items](https://marketplacelearn.walmart.com/guides/Item%20setup/Item%20setup%20methods/Add-a-new-item:-Search-the-Walmart-catalog) that are currently listed on Walmart.com. If there’s a match, you can reuse the dimensions and weight from approved listings.

## Add trade item configuration

There are [many ways](https://marketplacelearn.walmart.com/guides/Item%20setup/Item%20setup%20methods/Item-setup-methods:-Overview) to add items to your catalog. No matter the method, Walmart-fulfilled items will have a section for trade items. Follow these steps to add trade item configuration in either Seller Center or a downloadable template.

### Seller Center

If you’re [adding](https://marketplacelearn.walmart.com/guides/Item%20setup/Item%20setup/Add-items-in-bulk:-full-setup) or [converting](https://marketplacelearn.walmart.com/guides/Walmart%20Fulfillment%20Services%20(WFS)/WFS%20item%20setup/Convert-seller-fulfilled-items-to-WFS) a single item, follow these steps.

1. Fill in the **Required to list** section. Make sure to choose _Walmart Fulfilled_ as your fulfillment option.
2. Scroll to the **Required for Walmart’s supply chain** section. Add quantity, weight and dimensions in the _Each_ section.

![](https://azure-na-images.contentstack.com/v3/assets/blta7903c6b840b702d/blt20826f9205106d8f/6877cbe5ef20ef7cabec30a0/Single_UI_trade_item_config.png?branch=us_mplearn)

### Template

If you’re [adding](https://marketplacelearn.walmart.com/guides/Item%20setup/Item%20setup/Add-items-in-bulk:-full-setup) or [converting](https://marketplacelearn.walmart.com/guides/Walmart%20Fulfillment%20Services%20(WFS)/WFS%20item%20setup/Convert-seller-fulfilled-items-to-WFS) multiple items at a time, you’ll fill in a template. In the Trade Item Configurations tab, enter the country of origin, dimensions and weight for each item.

![](https://azure-na-images.contentstack.com/v3/assets/blta7903c6b840b702d/bltc642e0f034b108f1/6877cbaae72f253da0902ce9/Trade_item_config_tab.png?branch=us_mplearn)

## Frequently asked questions

* * *

What dimensions and weight are Walmart Fulfillment Services using to charge me?

Download the [Storage report](https://seller.walmart.com/wfsLite/reports) to check dimensions and weight in our system. If you believe the measurements are incorrect, contact Support by selecting the _Help_ button in the Seller Center menu bar.
