Overview

To receive payouts from Walmart Marketplace, you must set up an account with a third-party payout provider or through the [Marketplace Wallet](https://marketplacelearn.walmart.com/guides/marketplace-wallet-apply-and-enroll). In this guide, you’ll learn more about your payout options and you’ll get answers to common questions about the process.

## How does it work?

When you first join Walmart Marketplace, you’ll have the opportunity to set up your payout settings to receive your net sales payouts through [the Marketplace Wallet](https://marketplacelearn.walmart.com/guides/Taxes%20&%20payments/Settings/marketplace-wallet-apply-and-enroll) or a third-party provider.

The Marketplace Wallet, powered by our bank provider, is a deposit account embedded in Walmart’s platform that allows you to receive and transfer your payouts in Seller Center. Once enrolled in the Marketplace Wallet program, your payouts are first deposited into your Marketplace Wallet account. You can then link an approved U.S. business bank account registered under the same business name on your Marketplace Wallet account, and your financial controller can transfer your payouts to your linked U.S. business bank account through ACH with no hidden fees.

If you choose to receive payouts through your preferred payout provider, it may result in additional fees that Walmart won’t cover. Contact your payout provider directly for more information. Below are the payout providers you can choose from, depending on your country of incorporation:

| Country of incorporation | Payout provider |

| United States | [Marketplace Wallet](https://seller.walmart.com/settings/financial-settings/wallet-account-settings), [Hyperwallet](https://www.hyperwallet.com/), [Payoneer,](https://www.payoneer.com/) [PingPong](https://www.international.pingpongx.com/smb/ecommerce-sellers) |
| China and Hong Kong | [Payoneer](https://www.payoneer.com/), [PingPong](https://usa.pingpongx.com/) , [LianLian](https://www.lianlianglobal.com/), [Airwallex](https://www.airwallex.com/), [WorldFirst](https://www.worldfirst.com/uk/), [NetEase](https://marketplace.walmart.com/netease/?_gl=1*xkde6m*_gcl_au*MTE4MjgyNjc0NS4xNzIyOTczMjAz*_ga*MTI1MzgzNjc4MS4xNzIyOTcyOTM3*_ga_1LB22TM2MG*MTcyMzA0NDgzNy44LjEuMTcyMzA0Njg3Mi42MC4wLjA.) |

Notes:

Make sure your payout details and contact information are always accurate and up to date with Walmart and your chosen payout provider. Walmart isn't responsible for delays or losses resulting from incorrect information provided during your account setup, or during subsequent changes.

## Additional guidelines

- Your payout frequency is determined when your Walmart Marketplace account is approved. Generally, payout occurs biweekly (every 14 days) unless alternate arrangements are made.
- New sellers are subject to a payment hold, also known as a rolling delay of payments, for each order fulfilled within an initial period, as outlined [here](https://marketplacelearn.walmart.com/guides/Taxes%20&%20payments/Payments/Payment-activities). In most cases, payments for orders will be received in the payout cycle following the date that they’ve shipped (about 28 days after order ships).


- Additional performance factors—such as high return or chargeback rates, or selling counterfeit or illegal items—may lead to payment suspension, delays or permanent non-payment.


Notes:

Your default settlement cycle cut-off depends on your payment cycle which can be found under **Payment Information** on the [**Statements**](https://seller.walmart.com/payments/statements/period) page in Seller Center. All shipment notices received after the cutoff will be settled in the following payment cycle.

## Frequently asked questions

* * *

Where can I view my payout frequency?

Your payout frequency is determined by your agreement with Walmart. Check either your Walmart Marketplace Retailer Agreement or the dates on your previous [payment reports](https://seller.walmart.com/payments/statements/period) to view your payout cycle.

* * *

How do I change or make updates to the payout provider I use?

If you use a third-party payout provider and need to make updates to your account or if you already receive payments through a third-party payout provider and want to use the Marketplace Wallet, select the _Help_ button in the [Seller Center](https://seller.walmart.com/home) menu bar to contact Support.

* * *

Can I have more than one payout provider account set up at one time?

No. You can only set one provider at a time.

* * *

Why do I see a $1.50 withdrawal fee while withdrawing funds from my Payoneer account?

If you signed up for a Payoneer account through Payoneer directly instead of through the link in Walmart Seller Center, you may incur a $1.50 fee upon the withdrawal of funds via your Payoneer MyAccount. Contact Payoneer directly to learn how you can avoid or be refunded this Payoneer fee, as they arise from Payoneer’s policies, not Walmart’s.

* * *

How is my payout calculated?

The calculation for your payout includes:

- Shipped order notices received during your payment cycle.


- Refunded orders processed by you or Walmart Customer Care.


- Order adjustments (credits or debits), when applicable.


- Any outstanding negative balance from a previous payout cycle.


- Any fees from Walmart or its affiliates for services or other charges during the payout cycle.


* * *

How is the Marketplace Wallet different from third-party payment processors?

The Marketplace Wallet, powered by our bank provider, is integrated with Walmart’s Marketplace so you receive dedicated support without needing to maintain separate accounts. The wallet offers FDIC\* insurance to deposited funds to protect against any failure from JPMorgan Chase and has no hidden fees for ACH transfers to a linked bank account.

\*The Marketplace Wallet powered by J.P. Morgan is a deposit account insured by the FDIC up to $250,000.00 per depositor at JPMorgan Chase Bank, N.A., for each account ownership category.  The availability of FDIC deposit insurance coverage for the deposit account protects against the failure of JPMorgan Chase Bank, N.A., and not of Walmart, Inc.

* * *

How can I verify microdeposits when using Marketplace Wallet?

If you receive a microdeposit, you'll need to verify those amounts in Seller Center. If the amounts entered match the amount deposited, the verification is successful, and your external bank account will be linked to your Marketplace Wallet account. Ensure you’re correctly entering your bank account number and bank routing number correctly.
