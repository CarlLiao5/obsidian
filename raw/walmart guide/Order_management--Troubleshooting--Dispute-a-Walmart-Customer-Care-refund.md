Dispute a customer return in Seller Center

Last updated on Sep 9, 2025

Reading time: 3 min

* * *

- [Frequently asked questions](https://marketplacelearn.walmart.com/guides/Order%20management/Troubleshooting/Dispute-a-Walmart-Customer-Care-refund#frequently-asked-questions)

Overview

Once you receive a Walmart Customer Care or a customer return, you’ll have 48 hours to evaluate it. After evaluating the returned item, you may be eligible to file a dispute if you determine an unsuccessful or erroneous return has taken place. In this guide, you’ll learn how to dispute a return.

Notes:

If you didn’t refund the customer or initiate a dispute within 48 hours of receiving the return, Walmart will automatically refund the customer. However, you still have up to 45 days from the date of the return to submit your dispute.

Pro tip

Before you submit your dispute, review these common [customer return scenarios](https://marketplacelearn.walmart.com/guides/Order%20management/Troubleshooting/troubleshoot-a-customer-return) and the Walmart [dispute standards](https://marketplacelearn.walmart.com/guides/Policies%20&%20standards/Orders%20&%20returns/Dispute-standards) to determine if the return can be disputed.

## File a dispute

### Step 1 – Get started

Navigate to the [**Returns**](https://seller.walmart.com/orders/returns) dashboard in Seller Center. You can search for a return by the purchase order number or the return merchandise authorization (RMA) number. Select the _arrow_ beside the return you’re looking for to see more details.

### Step 2 – Initiate the dispute

Initiate a dispute by selecting the _Dispute this return_ button, which will open the dispute wizard. If the dispute button doesn’t appear on the [**Returns**](https://seller.walmart.com/orders/returns) dashboard, it’s because the refund has not been issued, the dispute window has passed for that return, or the return has already been disputed.

- **Standard returns**: If an order has more than one of the same item, you can submit a dispute for one of the items or the entire order. Select the purchase order number, then select _Dispute_ _this return_. Choose a dispute reason from the dropdown that best describes your issue. Certain dispute reasons will prompt you to select a subcategory. Once you’ve selected one, choose _Continue._

- **High-value returns:** Items considered over $100 each. This includes all luxury items, such as fine art, loose gems and gemstones, jewelry and watches above $300 and collectibles (e.g., coins, stamps, memorabilia and precious metals).

- **Walmart customer care returns:** For refunds outside the return window, you may still qualify by providing proof that you’ve followed the [Customer Care policy](https://marketplacelearn.walmart.com/guides/Order%20management/Customer%20care/Customer-Care-policy). Select the purchase order number, then select _Dispute this return._ Choose _Item was returned outside of the return window_ as the reason, then select _Next_. Upload supporting documents, then choose _Submit dispute._


Notes:

Choosing the wrong dispute type can delay the review process and negatively impact the resolution.

### Step 3 – Upload documentation

Upload supporting photos or documentation when you create your dispute. Documentation, including an image of the return label with tracking and images of the product showing the issue is required for the following dispute reasons:

- I received an incorrect return


- Item has missing parts/accessories


- Item is in unacceptable condition


- I sent the customer a replacement


When ready, select the _Submit dispute_ button. You’ll receive email confirmation with the case number. Typically, it can take up to 72 hours for your dispute to be reviewed and resolved, however, in some instances the timeframe could be longer.

Notes:

Walmart will review all refund-related email communications between you, the customer and the Walmart Customer Care team to help determine if the [Customer Care policy](https://marketplacelearn.walmart.com/guides/Order%20management/Customer%20care/Customer-Care-policy) was followed.

## Frequently asked questions

* * *

What if I don’t evaluate the return within 48 hours?

If you didn’t refund the customer or initiate a dispute within 48 hours of receiving the return, Walmart will automatically refund the customer. However, you still have up to 45 days from the date of the return to submit your dispute.

* * *

What happens to the customer refund after I submit the dispute?

As soon as you submit the dispute, the customer refund will be put on hold for 72 hours until Walmart provides a decision for your request. If the request is approved, the customer will not be refunded, and their return will be canceled.  If the request is denied, Walmart will automatically refund the customer.

* * *

If my dispute is denied, can I appeal the decision?

Yes. If you're not satisfied with the resolution of your dispute provided by Walmart, you can submit an appeal. You can only appeal a decision once, and it must be submitted within 30 days of receiving the denial or decision.

To submit an appeal, select the _Help_ button in the [Seller Center](https://seller.walmart.com/home) menu bar to contact Support.

* * *

Can I submit a dispute beyond the 45-day window?

Disputes filed outside the 45-day period will be denied, and that denial will be final.

* * *

Can I track the status of my dispute?

Yes, you can track the status of a dispute through the [**Disputes**](http://seller.walmart.com/orders/disputes) dashboard within Seller Center.

* * *

Are orders refunded by Walmart eligible for reimbursement?

Yes, if your order is refunded by Walmart, it may be eligible for reimbursement.

* * *

What returns are eligible for a dispute?

Review [this guide](https://marketplacelearn.walmart.com/guides/Policies%20&%20standards/Orders%20&%20returns/Dispute-standards) to learn more about which returns are eligible for a dispute.
