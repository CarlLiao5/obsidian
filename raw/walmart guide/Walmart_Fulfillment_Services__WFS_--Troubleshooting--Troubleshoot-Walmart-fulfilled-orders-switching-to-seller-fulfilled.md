Troubleshoot Walmart-fulfilled orders switching to seller-fulfilled

Last updated on Jul 14, 2025

Reading time: 1 min

* * *

In this guide:

- [Overview](https://marketplacelearn.walmart.com/guides/Walmart%20Fulfillment%20Services%20(WFS)/Troubleshooting/Troubleshoot-Walmart-fulfilled-orders-switching-to-seller-fulfilled#Overview)
- [3 ways to prevent a fulfillment switch](https://marketplacelearn.walmart.com/guides/Walmart%20Fulfillment%20Services%20(WFS)/Troubleshooting/Troubleshoot-Walmart-fulfilled-orders-switching-to-seller-fulfilled#3waystopreventafulfillment%20switch)

* * *

Overview

As a Walmart seller, there are a few fulfillment statuses. On the [**Catalog**](https://seller.walmart.com/items-and-inventory/manage-items) page, you'll see:

- **Seller fulfilled:** Items that you manage, pack and ship to customers.

- **Walmart fulfilled (WFS):** Items that Walmart stores, picks, packs and ships to customers on your behalf.

- **Seller fulfilled (WFS eligible)**: Items that can be fulfilled by either you or Walmart. These items aren't currently fulfilled by Walmart, but will be eligible once there's inventory at a fulfillment center.


We’ll prioritize your WFS inventory\* to fulfill orders, then switch to any remaining inventory you have outside of WFS once that inventory sells out. At that time you are responsible for fulfilling any customer orders received. If you have items you don’t want to fulfill customer orders for yourself, please set your seller-fulfilled inventory for those items to 0.

Failing to acknowledge and ship seller-fulfilled orders, or canceling them too often will lower your performance metrics. Your Marketplace account may be suspended or terminated if you don’t meet the [Seller Performance Standards](https://marketplacelearn.walmart.com/guides/Policies%20&%20standards/Performance/Seller-performance-standards).

_\*Subject to Section 21(a) of the_[_Retailer Agreement_](https://gecrm.my.salesforce.com/sfc/p/#61000000ZKTc/a/8Y000000lEpa/XSul9sMO5WoOnBUTEHjSb9gcC7ug4.LmlRc_gsXanY8) _, it is the seller’s responsibility to maintain adequate levels of inventory and deliver the same to WFS facilities._

## 3 ways to prevent a fulfillment switch

The most important thing you can do is keep inventory levels – for seller-fulfilled and Walmart-fulfilled — [accurate in Seller Center](https://marketplacelearn.walmart.com/guides/Getting%20started/Getting%20ready%20to%20sell/How-to-update-seller-fulfilled-inventory:-overview).

### Check inventory for seller-fulfilled items

While your items are on the way to WFS, make sure your seller-fulfilled inventory is [regularly updated](https://marketplacelearn.walmart.com/guides/Catalog%20management/Seller-fulfilled%20inventory%20management/Update-seller-fulfilled-inventory-in-bulk-in-Seller-Center). You may get customer orders that you’ll need to fulfill in the meantime. If you don’t have any units for a seller-fulfilled item, set your inventory to 0. This will show it as out-of-stock on Walmart.com so customers can’t place orders.

### Keep WFS inventory in stock

Regularly check the [**Inventory**](https://seller.walmart.com/wfsLite/manage-inventory) page for Walmart-fulfilled items. You can filter your catalog by at-risk and out-of-stock items. To restock items, create a [shipping plan](https://marketplacelearn.walmart.com/guides/Walmart%20Fulfillment%20Services%20(WFS)/Shipping%20to%20WFS/WFS-inbound-orders:-Send-inventory) and ship inventory to your assigned Walmart facility.

### Review customer orders

See the [**Orders**](https://seller.walmart.com/order-management/details?orderGroups=Unshipped) page for unshipped customer orders. Use the _Fulfilled by_ filter to find any unexpected, seller-fulfilled orders.
