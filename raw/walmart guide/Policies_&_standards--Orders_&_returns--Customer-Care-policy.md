Customer Care Policy

Last updated on Dec 9, 2025

Reading time: 3 min

Overview

As a Walmart Marketplace seller, it’s your responsibility to respond to order inquiries from customers and Walmart Customer Care. Before you respond, we suggest you familiarize yourself with this policy to ensure your responses and response times meet Marketplace standards. Failure to adhere to the Customer Care Policy or any other [Walmart Marketplace retailer policies](https://marketplacelearn.walmart.com/guides/Getting%20started/Onboarding/Walmart-Marketplace-retailer-policies) may result in account suspension or termination.

## What is the policy?

When you receive a customer or Walmart Customer Care inquiry by email, phone or in your Seller Center Inbox, you’re required to send a high-quality response within **48 hours** of receipt including weekends and Additional Days Off or Operational Outage periods. If you receive a Better Business Bureau or Attorney General Complaint, you must provide a high-quality response within the **first hour** of receipt.

Pro tip

The percentage of inquiries responded to within 48 hours of receipt is defined as your [Seller Response Rate](https://marketplacelearn.walmart.com/guides/Policies%20&%20standards/Performance/Seller-performance-standards). You’re required to maintain a rate of >95%.

If you don’t respond within 48 hours, Walmart reserves the right to cancel orders that haven’t been shipped, and/or refund shipped orders to satisfy a customer, impose seller category restrictions or suspend accounts for failure to adequately and timely respond to customers or Walmart Customer Care.

Notes:

You can’t dispute order cancellations or refunds due to a failure to respond to customers within 48 hours.

## Additional guidelines

| **Required** | **Prohibited** |
| --- | --- |
| - For transactions in the U.S., customer support must be provided in English. <br>- Voicemail must be available when a live agent cannot answer.<br>- There must be a greeting before customers are put into a waiting queue. <br>- If your call center or toll-free number monitors or records its customer service calls, you must provide customers with notice of such recording or monitoring. <br>- You must represent yourself as a separate entity from Walmart.com in providing your own customer service or support to customers who purchase from Walmart.com.  <br>- You must provide [accurate contact information](https://marketplacelearn.walmart.com/guides/Getting%20started/Account%20settings/Manage-company-information-in-Seller-Center). <br>- Check your spam folder and whitelist Walmart addresses to ensure all customer inquiries reach you. | - Including marketing/promotional materials or links to any website other than Walmart.com. <br>- Inaccurate or invalid contact information directed to another business or company. <br>- Automated responses that require multiple follow-ups.<br>- Misleading or overpromising. <br>- Initiating contact via phone or any other method outside of your Seller Center Inbox. If a customer contacts you by phone using your information on the Walmart platform, you may help. <br>- Including advertisements in greetings and voicemail messages.<br>- Using Google Voice or any internet telephone service as your customer service number. <br>- Using private residential phone numbers. <br>- Unsolicited text messages, emails or telephone calls to customers, including for marketing communications. <br>- Abusive or threatening language to customers, Walmart, or other Marketplace sellers. <br>- Including the term "Walmart" in an email address used for your customer service contact.<br>- Haggling with customers to avoid a return or refund. |

Pro tip

Walmart sends automatic emails to customers about their order status at every stage, so you don’t need to send additional order or product status updates unless the customer reaches out to you directly to inquire.

## Frequently asked questions

* * *

What should I do if a customer reaches out to me before they’ve made a purchase?

You may respond to customers' requests for information about a product both before and after a sale. You may also receive customer communication forwarded by Walmart teams. For more information, review [_Respond to customer messages in Seller Center_](https://marketplacelearn.walmart.com/guides/Order%20management/Customer%20care/Respond-to-customer-messages-in-Seller-Center) _._

* * *

I am unable to cancel or refund a customer order. What should I do?

If an order can’t be canceled or refunded, you’re still responsible for responding to customer inquiries and explaining why the customer can’t receive a cancellation or refund.

* * *

Can I use an automated answering system to respond to customers?

You’re required to offer toll-free telephone support to service Walmart orders. Walmart prefers that live agents are available to answer Walmart Marketplace questions.

* * *

Why did Walmart Customer Care provide a resolution to the customer?

Walmart may in its discretion take customer service actions for the benefit of customers, including but not limited to canceling orders, issuing refunds, processing returns or making other customer service adjustments.

* * *

Can I dispute a Walmart Customer Care refund?

If eligible, you may be able to dispute a refund that was provided by Walmart Customer Care. Review [_Dispute standards_](https://marketplacelearn.walmart.com/guides/Policies%20&%20standards/Orders%20&%20returns/Dispute-standards) for more information.
