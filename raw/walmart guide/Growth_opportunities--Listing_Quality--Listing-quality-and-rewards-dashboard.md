Overview

Your [Listing Quality](https://seller.walmart.com/growth/listing-quality) score provides insight into your items’ performance. Quality listings drive higher conversion rates and boost product visibility, allowing you to unlock growth opportunities such as the Pro Seller badge. In this guide, you’ll learn how your score is calculated.

## How does it work?

Listing Quality is a score that measures your items’ quality. Your [Listing Quality](https://seller.walmart.com/growth/listing-quality) score is made up of five component categories. These categories cover the attributes that make up a product detail page on Walmart.com. Learn more about the categories below:

| Component | Description |
| --- | --- |
| **Content quality** | Measures the efficacy of your listing’s [item name, item description, key features and images](https://marketplacelearn.walmart.com/guides/Item%20setup/Item%20content,%20imagery,%20and%20media/Product-Detail-Page:-overview). |
| **Price competitiveness** | - Compares your price against similar products on external marketplaces.<br>- You can also use the [**Pricing insights**](https://seller.walmart.com/pricing/insights) dashboard to learn more about where your offer stands. |
| **Shipping** | - Measures your promised delivery speed across ZIP codes.<br>- Items that weigh more than 50 pounds or are listed at $10 or less aren't included in your Listing Quality score calculations. |
| **Published & in stock** | Shows how often your item is available to customers and is based on the last seven days. |
| **Ratings & reviews** | Measures an item based on the number of customer reviews and its average rating. |

Notes:

You’ll have a catalog-level Listing Quality score and an item-level Listing Quality score for each individual listing. The catalog-level score is an average of your item-level scores.

## Additional guidelines

Learn more about how you can edit your items in the table below:

| Feature | Get started |
| --- | --- |
| **Apply Listing Quality suggestions to individual listing components** | Apply the Listing Quality suggestions to improve individual component scores (e.g., Content quality, Price competitiveness, Shipping, Published & in stock and Ratings & Reviews). To get started, visit [_Listing Quality: Fix issues_](https://marketplacelearn.walmart.com/guides/listing-quality-fix-issues) _._ |
| **Apply content updates to multiple items at once** | Improve your Content quality score by updating your [item’s name, item description, key features and images](https://marketplacelearn.walmart.com/guides/Item%20setup/Item%20content,%20imagery,%20and%20media/Product-Detail-Page:-overview). To get started, visit [_Listing Quality:_ _Bulk Attribute Editor_](https://marketplacelearn.walmart.com/guides/Listing%20optimization/Items%20and%20inventory/Bulk-attribute-editor). |

## Frequently asked questions

* * *

Why do some of my Listing Quality components show N/A?

If your Listing Quality component shows _N/A_ at the catalog- or item-level, that component is excluded from the overall score calculation.

* * *

How do I report an incorrect product type?

If you believe your item has been assigned to the incorrect product type, visit [_Report incorrect product type_](https://marketplacelearn.walmart.com/guides/Listing%20optimization/Troubleshooting/Report-incorrect-product-type) for more information.

* * *

Why am I not able to submit changes to my item’s content?

To ensure a high-quality shopping experience, Walmart selects the best content for each item attribute, giving priority to content provided directly by [brand owners and their authorized resellers](https://marketplacelearn.walmart.com/guides/Getting%20started/Brand%20Portal/brand-portal-overview). This means that if you don't have brand owner or authorized reseller privileges, you won’t be able to update some content fields, including blank fields.

For more access to an item’s content, you can [register as a brand owner or become an authorized reseller](https://marketplacelearn.walmart.com/guides/Getting%20started/Brand%20Portal/Register-your-brand-on-Walmart-Marketplace). Because brand owners and authorized resellers have priority, there are times when you won’t be able to decide what content is published on Walmart.com. If you’re not able to update your content, follow our [troubleshooting steps](https://marketplacelearn.walmart.com/guides/Getting%20started/Getting%20ready%20to%20sell/troubleshooting-content-errors).

* * *

I made updates to my item’s content, but they aren’t appearing on Walmart.com. Why?

To ensure a high-quality shopping experience, Walmart selects the best content for each item attribute, giving priority to content provided directly by [brand owners and their authorized resellers](https://marketplacelearn.walmart.com/guides/Getting%20started/Brand%20Portal/brand-portal-overview). If you need more access to an item’s content, you can [register as a brand owner or become an authorized reseller](https://marketplacelearn.walmart.com/guides/Getting%20started/Brand%20Portal/Register-your-brand-on-Walmart-Marketplace).

Because brand owners and authorized resellers have priority, there are times when you won’t be able to decide what content is published on Walmart.com. If you’re not able to update your content, follow our [troubleshooting steps](https://marketplacelearn.walmart.com/guides/Getting%20started/Getting%20ready%20to%20sell/troubleshooting-content-errors).
