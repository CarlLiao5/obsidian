Duplicate listings policy

Last updated on Dec 9, 2025

Reading time: 2 min

* * *

In this guide:

- [Overview](https://marketplacelearn.walmart.com/guides/Policies%20&%20standards/Product%20listings/duplicate-listings-policy#overview)
- [What is the policy?](https://marketplacelearn.walmart.com/guides/Policies%20&%20standards/Product%20listings/duplicate-listings-policy#Policy)
- [Frequently asked questions](https://marketplacelearn.walmart.com/guides/Policies%20&%20standards/Product%20listings/duplicate-listings-policy#frequently-asked-questions)

* * *

Overview

If a Marketplace item is offered by multiple sellers and it shares the same [product identifier](https://marketplacelearn.walmart.com/guides/Item%20setup/Item%20setup/Choose-a-product-identifier) (or product ID), the items will be combined into a single listing to help create a better navigation experience for customers. If you submit an incorrect product ID for an item that is already sold on Walmart.com, it may result in duplicate listings. To help prevent duplicate listings, learn more about the policy below.

## What is the policy?

Creating duplicate listings is a violation of Walmart policy and duplicate listings will be unpublished from Walmart.com. Items in unpublished status for more than 90 days will be retired from Walmart’s catalog. Once retired, the item will not be searchable or available in the Walmart catalog.

Failure to adhere to Marketplace policies may result in the removal of listings, account suspension or account termination.

## Frequently asked questions

* * *

How can I find my duplicate listings?

To locate your duplicate listings, navigate to the **Unpublished** tab of your [**Catalog**](https://seller.walmart.com/catalog/list-items) in Seller Center **.** Configure the table to include duplicate listings by selecting the **Filters** button. Next, select **Status change reason** section and select the checkbox for **Potential duplicate item**, then select _Apply_.

* * *

How can I correct a duplicate listing?

You can correct a duplicate listing by  [updating the item’s product ID](https://marketplacelearn.walmart.com/guides/Catalog%20management/Item%20management/Update-a-product-ID) and resubmitting the updated item data in Seller Center or through Walmart Marketplace APIs. If you are having trouble finding the correct product ID, contact the manufacturer or visit the manufacturer’s site.

* * *

Why aren’t duplicate listings allowed?

Duplicate listings clutter search and browse results on Walmart.com and make it harder for customers to find the product they want. Also, search engine optimization (SEO) is dependent on accurate product IDs. If an item is not set up with the correct product ID, it may not perform as well in Google searches.

* * *

How can I avoid creating a duplicate listing?

Pay attention when assigning product identifiers to your items. Some items with different variant attributes such as quantity, size, color, or flavor should have different product IDs (e.g., a pack of six boxes of tissues should have a different product ID than a pack of four boxes). If you confirm that a product ID does not exist for your specific variant, you can purchase a new one. Don’t use different product IDs to produce separate listings for the same item for your supply chain or other internal needs.

* * *

I can’t find a barcode on the item’s packaging. What should I do?

Search the manufacturer’s site or other online sources for product ID verification. If you still can’t find it, contact the manufacturer directly to confirm the correct product ID.

* * *

Should I purchase product IDs for my items?

Yes. If you are the manufacturer or a brand owner, you should always purchase product IDs for your items. Before purchasing a product ID, check the item’s packaging to verify if your item already has a product ID.

* * *

I believe my item listing was removed in error. What should I do?

If you believe an item was unpublished incorrectly or if you have questions, select the _Help_ button in the [Seller Center](https://seller.walmart.com/home) menu bar to contact Support.

* * *

What happens if my Duplicate listings are not actioned on?

Listings that have remained unpublished for at least 90 days will be retired by Walmart.
