Lag time exemption policy

Last updated on Mar 18, 2026

Reading time: 2 min

* * *

In this guide:

- [Overview](https://marketplacelearn.walmart.com/guides/Policies%20&%20standards/Shipping%20&%20fulfillment/Submit-a-lag-time-exemption-request#overview)
- [What is the policy?](https://marketplacelearn.walmart.com/guides/Policies%20&%20standards/Shipping%20&%20fulfillment/Submit-a-lag-time-exemption-request#what)
- [Additional guidelines](https://marketplacelearn.walmart.com/guides/Policies%20&%20standards/Shipping%20&%20fulfillment/Submit-a-lag-time-exemption-request#additional)
- [Frequently asked questions](https://marketplacelearn.walmart.com/guides/Policies%20&%20standards/Shipping%20&%20fulfillment/Submit-a-lag-time-exemption-request#frequently-asked-questions)

* * *

Overview

Lag time is the number of operational days it takes for you to ship an order after it’s been placed by a customer. Walmart Marketplace requires sellers to ship orders within two operational days after receiving an order. In this guide, you’ll learn how to request an exemption to exceed that timeframe if needed.

Pro tip

Walmart recommends setting a [fulfillment lag time](https://marketplacelearn.walmart.com/guides/Seller%20Fulfillment%20Services/Shipping%20methods/Shipping-and-fulfillment-policy) of 0 days to help improve your customer conversion rates and your eligibility for expedited delivery programs.

## What is the policy?

For made-to-order and print-on-demand items that are made in your facility, the lag time exemption request must not exceed five operational days. For all other items, including items shipped by freight or less than truckload (LTL), the lag time exemption request must not exceed 10 operational days.

If you’re a new Walmart Marketplace seller, you may be eligible to apply for a temporary lag time exemption with a set expiration date. Eligibility is determined by Walmart. If approved, you’ll receive an email notification before your exemption expires. After the expiration, any items with expired exemptions will automatically have their lag time reset to one day.

If an order comes in after your fulfillment center’s cutoff time, Walmart will count the following operational day as day one. Additional Days Off aren’t counted as operational days.

Notes:

Operational days are the active days specified in your fulfillment center settings, defaulting to Monday through Friday while allowing for adjustments.

Lag time exemption limits are subject to change at the discretion of Walmart. Walmart reserves the right to update the seller’s lag time limits as well as individual item lag time settings. Without limiting this discretion, Walmart may reject your request if the information is incomplete or misrepresents your business or the assortment offered on Walmart Marketplace.

Common lag time rejection reasons also include:

- You sourced the item from a third-party vendor (a vendor whose inventory levels and processing times are not contractually controlled by you).
- You’re not able to ship the item with same-day or next-day shipping due to inefficiencies in your fulfillment process.
- The item is on preorder or backorder.
- The item is to be personalized.


## Additional guidelines

To submit a lag time exemption request, select the _Help_ button in the [Seller Center](https://seller.walmart.com/home) menu bar to contact Support. Choose _Contact support_ and enter the phrase: **submit lag time exemption request**. Select _Next_ and then select _Confirm_. Then, select the _Get in touch with us_ link and provide your answers to the prompts.

If you’ve already set up your item prior to your request being approved, Walmart recommends that you temporarily [unpublish the item](https://marketplacelearn.walmart.com/guides/Catalog%20management/Item%20management/Retire-items-in-Seller-Center) or zero out your inventory to ensure that customers can’t place an order while the exemption request is being reviewed.

Pro tip

You can request more than one category when submitting your lag time request. To request multiple categories, you must submit a qualifying SKU example for each category. To confirm which lag time request category your SKU belongs to, download an item report from the **Reports** page in Seller Center and check the **Lag time category** column.

## Frequently asked questions

* * *

How long does it take for the lag time to show up in my orders after I submit a lag time exemption request?

The time it takes Walmart to approve or deny a lag time exemption request can vary, but it typically takes around two to three operational days for the request to be reviewed and a decision to be made. In some cases, it may take longer if additional information is needed or if there is a high volume of requests being processed.

* * *

Can I update my items’ lag times in bulk?

Yes, you can submit lag time exemption requests in bulk. You can use [_Update fulfillment lag time in Seller Center_](https://marketplacelearn.walmart.com/guides/Shipping%20&%20fulfillment/Fulfillment%20settings/Update-fulfillment-lag-time-in-Seller-Center) to apply the lag time to your items once your request is approved.

* * *

Can I apply for a standard lag time exemption after my temporary lag time expires?

Yes. You can apply for a standard lag time exemption after your temporary lag time exemption expires; however, eligibility will depend on freight criteria or made-to-order qualifications.
