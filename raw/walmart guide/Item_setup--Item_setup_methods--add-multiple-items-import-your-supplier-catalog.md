Add multiple items: Import your supplier catalog

Last updated on May 19, 2025

Reading time: 2 min

* * *

In this guide:

- [Frequently asked questions](https://marketplacelearn.walmart.com/guides/Item%20setup/Item%20setup%20methods/add-multiple-items-import-your-supplier-catalog#frequently-asked-questions)

* * *

Overview

If you’re a Walmart Drop Ship Vendor (DSV) or Warehouse supplier with a Supplier One account, you can seamlessly import your entire catalog to Walmart Marketplace. In this guide, you’ll learn how to import your Supplier One items into your Marketplace catalog in Seller Center.

Notes:

You must have a Supplier One account to use this item setup method.

## Import your supplier catalog

### Step 1 – Get started

Navigate to your [**Catalog**](https://seller.walmart.com/catalog/list-items) in Seller Center and select the _Add items_ button. In the **Import items** section, select _Import items_.

![2505_add-multiple-items_import-supplier-catalog_screenshot-1.png](https://azure-na-images.contentstack.com/v3/assets/blta7903c6b840b702d/blt953ff3c3c06edea6/682b4d057419015ddef85918/2505_add-multiple-items_import-supplier-catalog_screenshot-1.png?branch=us_mplearn)

### Step 2 – Download and complete the spreadsheet

On the **Import items** page, navigate to the **Import your Supplier One catalog** section. You have two options to import your assortment. You can import a spreadsheet of your assortment that is fulfilled through Walmart Fulfillment Services (WFS) or that you self-fulfill.

Once you download the spreadsheet of your choice, open the spreadsheet and review your items. For seller-fulfilled items, you must enter a SKU and price for each item.  For [Walmart-fulfilled items](https://marketplacelearn.walmart.com/guides/Walmart%20Fulfillment%20Services%20(WFS)/WFS%20item%20setup/WFS-item-spec-sheet-setup), you’ll need to fill out more detailed information, such as the item’s country of origin and whether it contains potentially hazardous material. You can remove any items that you don’t want to import.

![](https://azure-na-images.contentstack.com/v3/assets/blta7903c6b840b702d/blt3456c7567c64656a/682b4d05204ceba39212089d/2505_add-multiple-items_import-supplier-catalog_screenshot-2.png?branch=us_mplearn)

### Step 3 – Upload spreadsheet

When you’re ready, return to the [**Import items**](https://seller.walmart.com/catalog/add-items/importer) page and select the _Upload spreadsheet_ button. Then, upload the spreadsheet. If the system detects any errors, you’ll receive a notification with information on how to fix them.

After submitting your items, you can track their status from the [**Activity Feed**](https://seller.walmart.com/catalog/activity-feed) in Seller Center. Updates may take up to 24 hours. Any data errors that are detected during the updates will require extra processing time.

## Frequently asked questions

* * *

How does the Activity Feed work?

Whenever you edit an existing item or add a new item to your catalog, Seller Center will generate a Feed ID that can be used to track item setup and maintenance. Then, you’ll use the Feed ID and the [**Activity Feed**](https://seller.walmart.com/catalog/activity-feed) to [track the progress of your upload](https://marketplacelearn.walmart.com/guides/Item%20setup/Item%20setup/Track-item-setup-or-maintenance-activity-in-Seller-Center).

* * *

What should I do if I receive an error when I upload the spreadsheet?

If an error occurs, you can troubleshoot it. Learn more about how to fix common [Walmart template errors](https://marketplacelearn.walmart.com/guides/Item%20setup/Troubleshooting/troubleshoot-item-setup-errors) and [Walmart Fulfillment Services (WFS) item errors](https://marketplacelearn.walmart.com/guides/Walmart%20Fulfillment%20Services%20(WFS)/Troubleshooting/Troubleshoot-item-feed-errors).

* * *

I’m not interested in importing items yet. Are there other ways to build my catalog in Seller Center?

Yes. You can add new items to your Marketplace catalog and manage existing items anytime from your [**Catalog**](https://seller.walmart.com/catalog/list-items) in Seller Center. [Learn more about how to set up items](https://marketplacelearn.walmart.com/guides/Getting%20started/Getting%20ready%20to%20sell/Item-setup-methods:-Overview).

* * *

How do I assign inventory to an item?

It depends on how you’re fulfilling the item. For seller-fulfilled items, you can [choose how you want to update your inventory](https://marketplacelearn.walmart.com/guides/Getting%20started/Getting%20ready%20to%20sell/Item-setup-methods:-Overview). For Walmart-fulfilled items, the first step is to [create an inbound order in Seller Center](https://marketplacelearn.walmart.com/guides/Walmart%20Fulfillment%20Services%20(WFS)/Shipping%20to%20WFS/WFS-inbound-orders:-Send-inventory).

* * *

Can I import items into my Marketplace catalog even if I don’t have the item in stock?

Yes. However, an item must be in stock and available for customers to purchase before the item’s listing can be published on Walmart.com.

* * *

I still have questions about importing my items. Who can I contact?

If you still need help, email Walmart at [walmart\_mp\_item@walmart.com](mailto:walmart_mp_item@walmart.com). Your email must include:

- Your Drop Ship Vendor (DSV) or Warehouse supplier ID


- Your Marketplace seller ID


- The Feed ID


- The number of items affected


- Any error messages that you’ve received


- A copy of your Full Setup template


- Whether the items are Walmart-fulfilled or seller-fulfilled
