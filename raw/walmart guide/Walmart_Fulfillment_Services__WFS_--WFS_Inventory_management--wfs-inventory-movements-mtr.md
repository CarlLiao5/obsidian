WFS inventory movements (MTR)

Overview

When you use Walmart Fulfillment Services (WFS), we need to strategically position inventory across our network of fulfillment centers to quickly deliver to customers. This sometimes means we need to move your stored inventory from one fulfillment center to another, per the Retailer Agreement. This is called a Merchandise Transfer Request (MTR). In this guide, learn why MTRs happen and how they affect your available inventory.

## Reasons and benefits

There are several reasons we may need to move your inventory from one fulfillment center into another.

### Operational

We may move your inventory to optimize our operations. This includes, but isn’t limited to:

- A fulfillment center opening or closing

- Improving 2-day ground transportation in the network


### Business

To proactively meet customer demand and give your items the best chance to sell, we may also move your inventory to:

- Unlock faster shipping to customers

- Spread your inventory across the network so they’re closer to customers


## Check transferred units

While inventory is being transferred, those units will be temporarily unavailable for customer orders. To see how many units are affected, check the _Unavailable to sell_ column on the [**Inventory**](https://marketplacelearn.walmart.com/guides/Walmart%20Fulfillment%20Services%20(WFS)/WFS%20Inventory%20management/seller.walmart.com/wfs/inventory) page. Just select the number of units to view a breakdown.

![](https://azure-na-images.contentstack.com/v3/assets/blta7903c6b840b702d/bltd472c87bf3e580c8/6659c6fbb5316f000dc726ca/Screenshot_2024-05-31_at_6.40.29_AM.png?branch=us_mplearn)

If you prefer to view multiple items at once, download the [Inventory Reconciliation Report](https://seller.walmart.com/wfs/reports). You can choose a certain time period or even which GTINs. In the report, look for the _Transferred_ column to find how many units of each item are being transferred. Use the _Received_ column to identify any units that have been received at the fulfillment center.

## Turnaround time

From picking to receiving, you can expect the entire process to take about 14 business days, depending on the distance between the original and new fulfillment center. If your inventory has been unavailable for more than 30 days, please contact Support by selecting the _Help_ button in the [Seller Center](https://seller.walmart.com/home) menu bar.

## Frequently asked questions

* * *

What does an MTR cost for me?

MTRs don’t cost anything. It’s an internal decision made by WFS after your inventory is already stored with us.

* * *

Can I prevent an MTR from happening?

No. We make these decisions occasionally based on business or operational reasons. These inventory movements are made at Walmart’s sole discretion and are strategically made to serve you better.

* * *

Will my inventory remain in stock during an MTR?

We’ll do our best to avoid items going out of stock, but we cannot guarantee this will always be the case. In most cases, we only transfer part of your inventory at a time to keep some available for customers.

* * *

How can I track the progress of an MTR?

Unfortunately, you can’t track MTRs in detail. However, you can go to the [**Inventory**](https://seller.walmart.com/wfs/inventory) page and check the _Unavailable to sell_ column. This will show the number of units with movement in progress. Once those units are received, the count will be 0. Alternatively, you can download the [Inventory Reconciliation Report](https://seller.walmart.com/wfs/reports) to compare transferred and received units per item.

* * *

How long does an MTR take to complete?

On average, the end-to-end process takes about 14 business days. However, it may take longer if the original and new fulfillment centers are far apart.
