Overview

To provide a consistent shipping experience for customers, you're required to meet a minimum set of shipping policy requirements. In this guide, you'll learn more about the requirements of the shipping and fulfillment policy, along with additional guidelines.

## What is the policy?

## Additional guidelines

### Expected Delivery Date

The Expected Delivery Date is an item’s projected date of delivery to the customer. It’s calculated based on the order cutoff time, fulfillment lag time, seller-selected operational days and transit time that you’ve specified for the item. This Expected Delivery Date may or may not be the same as the Estimated Delivery Date that is shown to Walmart.com customers.

The Expected Delivery Date that is shown to Walmart.com customers is calculated based on your historical On-time Delivery rate, your carrier’s delivery input and Walmart’s logistical insights. This date also determines the search ranking of your product.

Notes:

You must maintain an On-time Delivery rate of 90% or higher to meet the [Seller Performance Standards](https://marketplacelearn.walmart.com/guides/Policies%20&%20standards/Performance/Seller-performance-standards).

When an item is five days past the Expected Delivery Date and the carrier has reported no movement of the item in the last three days, Walmart considers the item to be in a terminal state. In these instances, Walmart reserves the right to cancel any order that hasn't been delivered within a reasonable period after the Expected Delivery Date, as determined by Walmart at its sole discretion. You're responsible for any associated costs. Repeated failures to meet the Expected Delivery Date may result in selling privilege restrictions.

Notes:

The Expected Delivery Date is assigned by adding the maximum transit time for a given method and region. Order cancellation analysis for past-due Expected Delivery Date orders may be performed daily to provide refunds to customers.

### Fulfillment Lag Time

Fulfillment lag time is an item-level attribute that determines the number of days it takes for you to prepare the item for shipment. Walmart Marketplace requires sellers to ship orders within two operational days after receiving an order (unless you’ve submitted a [lag time exemption request](https://marketplacelearn.walmart.com/guides/Shipping%20&%20fulfillment/Fulfillment%20settings/Submit-a-lag-time-exemption-request)  and received approval from Walmart. Operational days are the active days specified in your fulfillment center settings, defaulting to Monday through Friday while allowing for adjustments. Additional days off are also considered when determining operational days.

Notes:

Lag time exemption limits are subject to change at the discretion of Walmart. Walmart reserves the right to update lag time limits as well as individual item lag time settings.

Lag time exemption approval is discretionary, revocable, limited and depends on the type of product. If you make any changes to your lag time, it may take up to 24 hours to be reflected on Walmart.com. Lag time is also used to calculate the shipping date that is displayed to Walmart.com customers, so you’re responsible for choosing a lag time that accurately reflects your fulfillment capabilities.

### Expected Ship Date

The Expected Ship Date is the date by which you must mark the order as _Shipped_  with valid tracking information. Once you mark the order as _Shipped_ in Seller Center or with APIs _,_ Walmart will send a shipment notification to the customer. If the shipping confirmation is received on or before the Expected Ship Date, the order is considered shipped on time.

Walmart will auto-cancel an order if it’s not marked as shipped and tracking information hasn’t been uploaded by the Expected Ship Date plus four calendar days. If an order has an Expected Ship Date of Monday, the order would be auto-canceled on Friday. If you ship an auto-canceled order, Walmart isn’t obligated to reimburse a seller for orders shipped after the Expected Ship Date.

Notes:

The [Seller Performance Standards](https://marketplacelearn.walmart.com/guides/Policies%20&%20standards/Performance/Seller-performance-standards), require you to maintain a Valid Tracking Rate of 99% or greater.

Repeated failures to meet the Expected Ship Date may negatively impact your performance metrics. If an order is placed by your order cutoff time, it’s expected to ship by the specified date below.

| Fulfillment lag time | Date that the order must be shipped by |
| --- | --- |
| **0 days** | On the same day |
| **1 day** | The next day |
| **2 days** | The day after next |

Notes:

If an order is placed after the order cutoff time, an additional day is added to the fulfillment lag time.

### Transit times

Order transit times determine the number of operational days it takes the carrier to deliver an order to the customer after it's shipped. The transit time requirements are specific to your particular shipping method and supported region.

You’re not required to use any specific carriers or carrier service levels to support the specified transit times within each shipping method.

## Frequently asked questions

* * *

What's the order cutoff time?

The time at which you’ll stop receiving orders each day. If you have multiple fulfillment centers in different time zones, select the time that provides the best [order cutoff time](https://marketplacelearn.walmart.com/guides/Shipping%20&%20fulfillment/Fulfillment%20settings/Fulfillment-Center-operating-schedule) across all your facilities.

* * *

Can I modify my order cutoff time?

Yes. Unless otherwise specified, your fulfillment centers will have a default order cutoff time of 2:00 pm in your local time zone and you’ll be automatically enrolled to support all operational days, Monday through Friday. Failing to configure your order cutoff time could negatively impact your on-time shipping rate.

* * *

What shipping carriers does Walmart support?

Freight carriers are listed [here](https://marketplacelearn.walmart.com/guides/Shipping%20&%20fulfillment/Shipping%20methods/Shipping-methods:-freight-shipping-carriers) and parcel carriers [here](https://marketplacelearn.walmart.com/guides/Shipping%20&%20fulfillment/Shipping%20methods/Shipping-methods:-parcel-shipping-carriers). Consider using large, reputable carriers to fulfill your Walmart orders. If you’re using small or regional carriers not integrated with Walmart, you may not meet the 99% valid tracking requirement.

* * *

How do I edit a tracking number after I’ve submitted it to Walmart?

You can edit the tracking number up to four hours after updating the order to _Shipped_ status. For more information, review [_Update tracking numbers in Seller Center_](https://marketplacelearn.walmart.com/guides/Order%20management/Order%20status/Update-tracking-numbers-in-Seller-Center).

* * *

Can I use stamps or prepaid envelopes to ship orders via USPS?

No. All orders must have a valid tracking number.

* * *

Can I opt out of having Walmart shorten my delivery promise times?

Yes. Navigate to the [**Shipping Info**](https://seller.walmart.com/settings/shipping-profile/shipping-info) page in Seller Center, locate the **Delivery Promise Optimization** section and select _Opt Out_. If you want to avoid late deliveries, you can try [**Simplified Shipping Settings**](https://seller.walmart.com/settings/shipping-profile/simplified-shipping/simplified-carriers), which helps you close the gap between your actual and promised delivery dates.

* * *

Can I opt out of having Walmart extend my delivery promise times?

No. To provide a consistent and accurate shipping experience for Walmart.com customers, Walmart reserves the right to update or extend shipping dates. Walmart may also extend delivery promise times due to weather-related delays, which can impact seller or carrier operations.

However, your delivery promise times may not need to be extended if you consistently meet the **On-time Delivery Rate** seller standards.

* * *

Can I use Amazon Multi Channel Fulfillment (MCF)?

Yes. When using Amazon MCF, you must ship the items in a neutral box and block Amazon Logistics as a carrier to ensure orders aren’t delivered using Amazon-branded vehicles and to comply with Walmart’s shipping and fulfillment policy.
