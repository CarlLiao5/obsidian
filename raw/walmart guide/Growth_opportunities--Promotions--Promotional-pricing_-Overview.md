Promotional pricing: Overview

Last updated on Jun 29, 2025

Reading time: 4 min

* * *

Overview

Promotional pricing is used as a method to provide a temporary price reduction. Promotion guidelines may vary depending on the badge or type of promotion you choose. In this guide, you’ll learn more about these guidelines.

## How does it work?

There are several ways to display your promotions on Walmart.com. For example, you may want to run a promotion during a holiday period, or you may want to reduce the price of an item that you’re discontinuing. There are guidelines that you must follow when applying promotional pricing to your listings. To qualify for a strikethrough, the promotional price must be:

- 10% lower than the [**Comparison price**](https://marketplacelearn.walmart.com/guides/Policies%20&%20standards/Product%20listings/Comparison-Price-rules) for items less than $200.


- 5% lower than the **Comparison price** for items equal to or greater than $200.


Promotions are identified with a **Reduced Price**, **Clearance** or **Price-in-cart** badge on Walmart.com. Learn more about the various promotions in the table below.

| Promotion | Guidelines |
| --- | --- |
| **Reduced price** | This promo type should be used when you’re temporarily reducing your price for a minimum of 14 days up to 90 days (e.g., during special merchandising events on Walmart.com). Your promo price must be at least 10% lower than theComparison price. While meeting the criteria increases the chances of qualifying, Walmart auto-assigns this badge based on various factors and isn’t guaranteed. |
| **Clearance** | This promo type should be used only when your item has either reached the end of its lifecycle or is being liquidated. Your promo price must be at least 10% lower than the Comparison price. |
| **Price-in-cart** | This promo placement option allows you to display the promotional price in the customer's shopping cart during the checkout process when the item is listed for less than the Minimum Advertised Price (MAP). |

## Additional guidelines

There are two ways you can create and manage a promotion in Seller Center. You can use the [**Promotions**](https://marketplacelearn.walmart.com/guides/Growth%20opportunities/Promotions/promotional-pricing-set-up-via-the-promotions-dashboard) dashboard to apply a price reduction to a group of multiple items, or you can use the [Price and promotion template](https://marketplacelearn.walmart.com/guides/Growth%20opportunities/Promotions/Promotional-pricing:-Set-up-in-Seller-Center) to apply a unique discount to each individual item.

Once your promo is set up, you can run a [Promotions Report](https://seller.walmart.com/items-and-inventory/reports?returnPATH=%2Fitem%2Flist) in Seller Center to browse all active promotions.

Notes:

If you use Walmart APIs, use the [Price and Promotional Price Management API](https://developer.walmart.com/us-marketplace/docs/introduction-to-marketplace-apis) to manage your promotions.

## Frequently asked questions

* * *

How can I set up promotional pricing in Seller Center?

For more information on how to set up promotions in Seller Center, check out [_Promotional pricing: Set up via the Price and promotion template_](https://marketplacelearn.walmart.com/guides/Growth%20opportunities/Promotions/Promotional-pricing:-Set-up-in-Seller-Center).

* * *

How can I view my promotions in Seller Center?

You can view your promotion through the [Promotion Report](https://seller.walmart.com/items-and-inventory/reports?returnPATH=%2Fitem%2Flist&_gl=1*1futnys*_ga*MTM3NTg4NjM2MS4xNjk4MDc2Mjgw*_ga_1LB22TM2MG*MTcxNjkxOTA5MS4yNzMuMS4xNzE2OTE5NTc4LjU1LjAuMA..) and/or **Promotions** dashboard in Seller Center.

* * *

Why isn’t the strikethrough showing for my item?

There could be a few reasons why the strikethrough isn’t showing up for your item. As a reminder, for it to appear the promotional price must be at least 10% lower than the Comparison price for items less than $200, and 5 % lower for items equal to or greater than $200.

* * *

How long does it take for a promotion to publish on Walmart.com?

Promotion updates can take up to four hours to publish. For this reason, you should upload your Price and promotions template in Seller Center at least 24 hours before you want the promo to begin. As a reminder, the start and end times of your promotions are in Universal Time Zone (UTC) and not in your local time zone.

* * *

Can I set up a promotion for individual customers?

No, promotions are set at the item-level. It's not possible to offer deals or discounts to individuals. Offering deals or discounts to individual customers constitutes marketing/solicitation and is prohibited in the Marketplace Retailer Agreement.

* * *

Would my promotion show up in the Promotions dashboard if I created it via the Price and promotion template?

Yes, your promotion created via the Price and promotion template will be captured on the **Promotions** dashboard.
