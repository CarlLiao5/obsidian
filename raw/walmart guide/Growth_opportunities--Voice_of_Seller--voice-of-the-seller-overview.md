Overview

The Voice of the Seller program provides the Walmart Marketplace team with opportunities for growth and insight into your business goals, needs and preferences to enhance our platform and improve your engagement and performance.

In this guide, you’ll learn about the benefits of the program and how to participate in various activities.

## How does it work?

Occasionally, you may receive an invitation to participate in various surveys, focus groups, interviews, user testing or listening sessions. Your participation in these activities drives direction on programs, products and experiences to build a better Marketplace together.

Notes:

When you provide or make available suggestions, comments, ideas, improvements or other feedback or materials to Walmart.com, the Marketplace, the Sites, or other subject matter Walmart.com will be free to disclose, reproduce, modify, license, transfer and otherwise distribute, and use and exploit any of the foregoing feedback or materials in any manner in its sole discretion.

## Frequently asked questions

* * *

How do I opt-in to participate in Voice of the Seller feedback activities?

If you’re an active seller, then you’re enrolled by default. Research requests are sent to you via email or as feedback collection forms with Seller Center.

* * *

How often will I be contacted to give feedback?

Our core measurement survey will be sent to you once per quarter unless you’ve previously opted-out of receiving feedback requests. You may receive additional invitations based on the business need for your input.

* * *

Can I opt-out of the feedback activities?

Yes, you can unsubscribe from future activities in the footer of the email invitation. Once you opt-out, you’ll be removed from future requests.

* * *

Will you collect my personal information be shared?

Occasionally, we might ask for or collect personal information (ex. name and preferred email address), but only if we need to schedule follow-up sessions. However, you should still remain vigilant for any scam emails that may impersonate Walmart. Walmart will never ask you for any sensitive information and you shouldn’t share any passwords, financial information, or other personal identifiable information.
