Credit Card Chargeback policy

Last updated on Dec 11, 2025

Reading time: 2 min

* * *

In this guide:

Overview

A credit card chargeback, also known as a charge dispute or credit card reversal, occurs after a customer makes a purchase on Walmart.com and disputes the charges with their financial institution. Typically, customers will dispute the charge because they don’t recognize the charge on their statement, their card was stolen, they didn’t receive the product or the product or service was defective or damaged. Keep reading to learn more about the Walmart Marketplace policy on credit card chargebacks.

Notes:

Credit card chargebacks that you’re responsible for are offset against amounts owed to you.

## What is the policy?

Per the Walmart Marketplace Retailer Agreement, you’re responsible for all credit card chargebacks except for chargebacks that are a result of credit card fraud, for which Walmart is responsible.

Walmart collects all the required and supporting information for customer chargeback claims received from credit card companies. A chargeback claim can take up to 90 days to appear on your statement from the date the charge was disputed with the customer’s financial institution. Some disputes can take longer. Many claims are based on the credit card companies, not Walmart. Therefore, there is no recourse to appeal the decision.

## Frequently asked questions

* * *

How does a credit card chargeback differ from a customer refund?

A customer will contact Walmart or you directly to request a refund. If a customer refuses the charges of their purchase, they’ll contact their credit card company directly to dispute the charge. When this happens, the final decision is made by the financial institution, not Walmart. We can’t reverse chargebacks.

* * *

How will credit card chargebacks be reflected on my Payments Report?

Credit card chargebacks will be reflected in your [Payments Report](https://marketplacelearn.walmart.com/guides/Taxes%20&%20payments/Payments/Payment-statements-and-transactions) as refunds with the reason code _5025: Customer Chargeback_.

* * *

How can I prevent credit card chargebacks?

Walmart encourages you to take the following steps to prevent charge disputes:

- Use signature-required delivery methods for high-value orders and products.

- Describe your products and services in as much detail as possible.

- If your customer service policy doesn’t allow refunds or is otherwise unsatisfactory to customers, you may receive more chargebacks than average. We recommend reviewing your customer service policy and practices for allowing customers to dispute in a manner that meets both the needs of the customer and your business.

- Provide easy access to return and refund policies for customers.

- Don’t change the shipping address provided by Walmart. You’ll be liable for any disputes initiated for orders you send to a different shipping address.

- Provide valid tracking details for all orders.
