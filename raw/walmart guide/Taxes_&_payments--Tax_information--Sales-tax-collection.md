Overview

Based on several state tax laws, Walmart Marketplace is responsible for calculating, collecting, remitting and refunding sales tax on orders that are shipped and delivered to the states listed in this guide.

## How does it work?

Walmart certifies that it is registered to collect sales tax and will remit sales and use tax on the sales of taxable items made through the Walmart Marketplace in the states listed below:

## Additional Guidelines

You’ll continue to receive taxes collected on orders delivered to all other states not listed above, even if other marketplaces in those states are remitting taxes on your behalf. You’re responsible for remitting the taxes to the tax authorities in those states until otherwise notified by Walmart.

For more information on product fees/taxes collection and remittance, see [product fees & tax collection](https://marketplacelearn.walmart.com/guides/Taxes%20&%20payments/Tax%20information/Product-fees-&-tax-collection). For detailed information on what was collected and sent to you, see [payment processing](https://marketplacelearn.walmart.com/guides/Taxes%20&%20payments/Payments/Payment-processing).

Notes:

This guide is solely related to sales and use tax collection and remittance. Walmart and the Walmart Marketplace Program do not provide tax, legal, or accounting services. This material has been prepared for informational purposes only, and is not intended to provide, and should not be relied on for, tax, legal or accounting advice. If you should have any questions about this information, you should consult your own tax, legal and accounting advisors.

## Frequently asked questions

* * *

Where do I go if my items are not being taxed correctly?

To learn how to verify if your item’s product type ID is correct, visit [_Report incorrect product type._](https://marketplacelearn.walmart.com/guides/Listing%20optimization/Troubleshooting/Report-incorrect-product-type)

* * *

I’m still seeing taxes in orders shipping to states where Walmart is responsible for remittance on my settlement report. How do I find out what these are?

Marketplace facilitator laws do not cover all taxes and fees that are collected from the customer. For more information on the seller's responsibility to remit to the appropriate government agency, visit [_Product fees & tax collection_](https://marketplacelearn.walmart.com/guides/Taxes%20&%20payments/Tax%20information/Product-fees-&-tax-collection).

* * *

A sales tax auditor is asking Walmart to complete a form to verify that Walmart is collecting and remitting sales tax under Marketplace facilitator law. How do I get this form filled out by Walmart?

The Walmart Marketplace website is set up so that these forms are not necessary to be completed. We have worked with state agencies to ensure the language on our website is sufficient for their audit purposes. Guide your auditor to this guide and the [_Tax collection and remittance policy addendum_](https://marketplacelearn.walmart.com/guides/Policies%20&%20standards/Account/Tax-collection-and-remittance-policy-addendum) _._
