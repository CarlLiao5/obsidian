Returns Shipping Service (RSS) policy

Last updated on Dec 10, 2025

Reading time: 2 min

* * *

In this guide:

- [Overview](https://marketplacelearn.walmart.com/guides/Policies%20&%20standards/Orders%20&%20returns/Returns-Shipping-Service-RSS-policy#overview)

- [Frequently asked questions](https://marketplacelearn.walmart.com/guides/Policies%20&%20standards/Orders%20&%20returns/Returns-Shipping-Service-RSS-policy#frequently-asked-questions)

Overview

Walmart’s Returns Shipping Service (RSS) is the default label printing option for Marketplace returns and is applied to any item that is returned to a Walmart store. RSS allows you to use special, discounted rates for return shipments of products sold on Walmart.com. Walmart will determine the carrier based on the best rates available.

Notes:

Your business will be listed as the shipper of record.

## What is the policy?

Walmart isn’t liable for any loss or damage to a package shipped using RSS. You’re responsible for filing a claim with FedEx or USPS for any package that was lost or damaged during transit to the return center location you provided. RSS packages that are undeliverable or can’t be returned to your return center by FedEx or USPS may be disposed of or destroyed at Walmart’s discretion without notice.

Per the [Returns policy](https://marketplacelearn.walmart.com/guides/Order%20management/Returns%20&%20refunds/returns-policy), you're responsible for listing a valid and correct return center address. If the address is invalid, your packages may be re-routed to Walmart facilities, and you’ll be responsible for all associated charges. All disputes will be denied. If necessary, Walmart may terminate or suspend your participation in RSS with 30 days prior written notice. If terminated or suspended, you’ll remain responsible for any costs or charges existing or accrued at or prior to the date of suspension or termination.

## Frequently asked questions

* * *

How much does RSS cost?

Return shipping charges are based on the rate card available in [Seller Center,](https://seller.walmart.com/order-management/returns) which is confidential. The applicable rate card is subject to change with ten days' prior written notice by Walmart.  Disclosure or distribution of RSS rates is prohibited under the Walmart Retailer Agreement.

Rates are applied based on the higher of the actual weight or dimensional weight for the package, using the package weight provided by the carrier. Return shipping charges may be adjusted after shipment due to carrier or other service provider adjustment.

* * *

Do I have to use RSS for return shipping?

No. You can also use a [third-party carrier](https://marketplacelearn.walmart.com/guides/Shipping%20&%20fulfillment/Shipping%20methods/Retrieve-credentials-for-your-return-shipping-carrier-account) (FedEx or UPS only).

* * *

I’m using a third-party carrier for returns, but RSS was applied instead. Why?

RSS will be used for initiated returns if:

- The return is initiated in a Walmart store.

- You fail to provide the details of your chosen shipping carrier.

- You provided shipping carrier details, but Walmart is unable to authenticate the carrier credentials, or the account is not fully ready to print labels when a customer return is initiated.

- The shipping carrier fails to generate a requested return label.


* * *

Where can I see the return shipping charges?

Your [**Payments Report**](https://seller.walmart.com/payments/statements/period) in Seller Center will list any return shipping fees you owe to Walmart. This will be part of the financial settlement between you and Walmart.
