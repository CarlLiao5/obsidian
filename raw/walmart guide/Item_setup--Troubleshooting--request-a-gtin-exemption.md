Request a GTIN exemption

Overview

If you sell products that don't have a [product ID](https://marketplacelearn.walmart.com/guides/Policies%20&%20standards/Product%20listings/Choose-a-product-identifier) (e.g., private label or handmade jewelry), you may be eligible to receive authorization to sell these items on Walmart.com by submitting a GTIN exemption request. In this guide, you’ll learn how to submit a request if needed.

## Request a GTIN exemption

### Step 1 – Get started

Navigate to the **Catalog** section in Seller Center and select [_GTIN Exemptions_](https://seller.walmart.com/catalog/gtin-exemptions) _._ If it’s the first time you’re applying for an exemption, select _Get started_. If you have existing exemptions, select the _Apply for GTIN exemptions_ button.

Notes:

You must have admin level access to Seller Center to request an exemption and to view the status of existing submissions.

Pro tip

For product verification, you may provide up to three URLs from your business website or an external marketplace where your store information is located.

### Step 2 – Choose the category and product type

You can either enter your store information using item URLs (optional), or you can select _Next_ to choose your category, product type, brand and reason for exemption. You can select up to 10 categories and 100 product types for each exemption request. Then, select _Next_.

### Step 3 – Select the brand and reason

Use the drop-down menu to search for and select the brand. Then, select the reason for your exemption. If none of the reasons apply, select _Other_ and add your reason in the text field.

If you don’t find the brand, you can select _Add a brand_ from the Brand dropdown and add the brand or your brand name.

Pro tip

To request an exemption for the same brand or reason across all product types in this exemption, select _Update brand & reason._ Search for a brand or add a new one if it isn’t listed. Select the reason, then choose _Submit_.

### Step 4 – Submit

Once you’ve submitted your request, it'll be reviewed by Walmart. On the [**GTIN Exemptions**](https://seller.walmart.com/catalog/gtin-exemptions) page, you can check the status of your request. The request may display one of the following statuses:

| Status | Definition |
| --- | --- |
| **Approved** | After your request is approved, you can set up your items with the same brand and product type approved in the exemption. You must wait four hours after approval to set up your item. |
| **Processing** | Your request is being reviewed by Walmart. Allow up to three business days to receive a response. |
| **Needs info** | Walmart needs additional information from you before processing your request. Check your email for more details and respond to all questions within five days. |
| **Denied** | Your request was denied. You may view the denial reason by hovering over the Denied status. |
| **Closed** | Your request was reviewed and determined that Walmart needs additional information from you to process the request. If Walmart doesn't receive a response from you within five days, the request will be closed. |

Notes:

When you set up your items for exemption, the brand entered must be the exact same name you used during item setup.

## Frequently asked questions

* * *

Can I use this same process for a UPC exemption?

Yes. You can request an exemption for a UPC or GTIN.

* * *

Why was my request denied?

Requests are commonly denied if we’ve identified that you’ve been issued a GTIN previously. If you believe this is incorrect and you have a legitimate exemption need, select the _Help_ button in the [Seller Center](https://seller.walmart.com/home) menu bar to contact Support and submit an appeal.

* * *

I have a GTIN exemption and want to use Walmart Fulfillment Services (WFS). What should I do?

If you have exempted items and want to use WFS, you must set up the items as seller-fulfilled first during item setup and then [convert to WFS](https://marketplacelearn.walmart.com/guides/Walmart%20Fulfillment%20Services%20(WFS)/WFS%20item%20setup/Convert-seller-fulfilled-items-to-WFS).

* * *

I have existing GTIN exemptions that were previously approved at the category level. Is there anything I need to do?

Existing GTIN exemptions that were previously approved at the category level have been migrated to the product type level and will remain approved unless otherwise noted. To view a list of your approved GTIN exemptions by product type, visit the [**GTIN Exemptions**](https://seller.walmart.com/catalog/gtin-exemptions) page in Seller Center.
