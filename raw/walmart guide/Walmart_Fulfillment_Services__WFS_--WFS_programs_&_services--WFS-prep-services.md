Overview

Use Prep Services to outsource inventory preparation for Walmart Fulfillment Services (WFS). It’s a great option if you lack the time, infrastructure or available labor to prepare your Walmart-fulfilled items correctly and consistently. In this guide, learn how to request poly bags and item labels for each unit.

## How does it work?

When you create shipments in Seller Center, you can choose item preparation options for your Walmart-fulfilled items. When your items arrive at your assigned fulfillment center, we’ll complete your requested prep and make the items available on Walmart.com.

We’ll prioritize items with scheduled prep services over items with unplanned prep. We also charge a fee to correct items that require unplanned prep work (e.g., missing labels or incorrect packaging).

## Fees and unplanned prep

By using Prep Services, your items will comply with WFS packing and label requirements, as stated in the [WFS Routing and Packaging Guide](https://azure-na-assets.contentstack.com/v3/assets/blta7903c6b840b702d/blt57da3349569cc081/WFS_Routing_Guide.pdf?branch=us_mplearn). That helps us receive your items more efficiently.

If you prepare your own items, they must meet the same requirements. Items that don’t may be refused or delayed at the fulfillment center. If items require unplanned prep work (e.g., missing labels or incorrect packaging) at the fulfillment center, we’ll charge an extra $0.20 per unit on top of the usual Prep Services fee. Specifically, we’ll:

1. Accept the items and correct them on your behalf.
2. Process the items.
3. Issue the unplanned prep fee to your account.

The unplanned prep fee will cover labor and material costs required to fix your items. We’ll prepare and process the items according to WFS facility timelines, which may take up to 10 business days.

| **Service** | **Description** | **Prep Service fee** | **Unplanned prep fee** |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Poly bagging | We’ll package each sellable unit in a loose, clear bag to protect it during storage and fulfillment. If the original label isn’t scannable after we poly bag, we may have to add new labels to the bag. | $0.60 per unit | $0.80 per unit |
| Item labeling | We’ll place a readable, scannable label on every sellable unit and ensure that each item is properly labeled, received and stored | $0.45 per unit | $0.65 per unit |

## Add a prep service

Use one of these methods to request prep for your items:

### Seller Center

1. Log into Seller Center and go to the [**Shipping Plans**](https://seller.walmart.com/wfsLite/sendinventory) page.
2. Select _Create plan_ and choose items.

3. In the _Packing setup_ column, you’ll need a template for each single SKU item. When you create or edit a template, you can choose whether you want WFS Prep Services applied to the item.

4. Update the number of master cases for each item to see a price estimate.

5. Complete the rest of your shipping plan and submit it.


You won’t be able to modify the prep services settings after you submit your inbound order. If you need to make a change, cancel the order and create a new one.

![](https://azure-na-images.contentstack.com/v3/assets/blta7903c6b840b702d/blt331f1803ef0b87ff/68757c4ef077e269577692ec/Prep_Services_modal.png?branch=us_mplearn)

Notes:

If you don’t see an option to create a packing template, then you can simply add prep under the _Pack and label_ column.

### API

Go to the Developer Portal learn how to add prep services in our [Create Inbound Shipment API](https://developer.walmart.com/us-marketplace/reference/createshipment). Before you ship your items, please print your receiving labels using your usual process. If you requested prep services for any items, "Prep Required" will appear on the receiving labels.

## Frequently asked questions

* * *

How will the WFS Prep Services fees appear on my statement?

You'll see any planned or unplanned prep fees in the [Settlement Report](https://seller.walmart.com/wfsLite/reports), as well as the Marketplace and WFS Report. The transaction type will be "PrepServiceFee," and the reason code column will describe the kind of prep work ("Bag" or "Label"). If it was unplanned prep work, it'll say "UnplannedBag" or "UnplannedLabel."

* * *

What type of items would benefit from poly bagging?

Items that require additional protection from leakage would need to be placed in a bag. Examples include apparel, plush toys, small items (USBs, jewelry, keychains), liquids in plastic bottles, powders and pellets.

* * *

What are the maximum dimensions of an item that would be eligible for poly bagging?

The maximum size of a poly bag is 18" x 24". Most sortable items will work, though we may need to put some items in a box if they won't fit into any of our existing bags. There will be no extra charges in such case.

* * *

Will WFS Prep Services label over existing barcodes on items or packages?

No, we won't label over existing barcodes. Remove or cover any existing scannable barcodes.

* * *

Do we need to send poly bagged items in clear bags?

You can send most items in opaque poly bags or other packaging, as long as they're visibly marked with scannable barcodes and all required hazard warnings. If barcodes aren't visible, the item will either be rebagged or relabeled accordingly and treated as unplanned prep work. Note that valuables like jewelry, watches, and sunglasses must be in tamper-evident sealed packaging. (See the [WFS Routing and Packaging Guide](https://azure-na-assets.contentstack.com/v3/assets/blta7903c6b840b702d/blt57da3349569cc081/WFS_Routing_Guide.pdf?branch=us_mplearn) for more details about valuables.)
