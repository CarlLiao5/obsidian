Manage duplicate listings in Seller Center

Last updated on Oct 22, 2024

Reading time: 3 min

* * *

Overview

If you submit an incorrect product identifier (or product ID) for an item that’s already sold on Walmart.com, it may result in duplicate listings. In this guide, you’ll learn how to manage these potential duplicate listings to ensure that your account remains in good standing.

## Manage duplicates

### Step 1 – Get started

Navigate to the [**Pending review**](https://seller.walmart.com/catalog/pending-review?filters=%257B%2522filters%2522%253A%257B%2522filters%2522%253A%255B%257B%2522field%2522%253A%2522status%2522%252C%2522op%2522%253A%2522equals%2522%252C%2522values%2522%253A%255B%2522ACTION_NEEDED%2522%252C%2522PROHIBITED%2522%252C%2522IN_REVIEW%2522%255D%257D%255D%257D%252C%2522tab%2522%253A%2522wfsCompliance%2522%252C%2522filterTab%2522%253A%2522All%2522%257D) page in Seller Center and select the _Potential duplicates_ tab.

Pro tip

To turn on Duplicates notifications, navigate to **Notifications** _,_ select _Settings_ then scroll down to the Items section. Choose the checkbox next to **Duplicate items identified**.

### Step 2 – Review potential duplicates

Next to the item identified with duplicates, select _Review duplicate._ This redirects you to the Review duplicates page which lists all duplicated items beside your original item.

### Step 3 – Submit decision

Review the items listed to either **Mark as unique** or **Confirm duplicate.** Once you’re satisfied with your selections, choose _Submit._ After your decision has been submitted, the status will be updated on the **Pending review** page.

Notes:

Filter by All to view which items still need action, are currently in review, processing or in error.

## Frequently asked questions

* * *

Why merge duplicates?

Creating duplicate listings is a violation of Walmart Marketplace policy. Failure to comply may result in the removal of listings, account suspension or account termination. To learn more about Walmart’s policy on duplicate listing, including how to avoid them, visit [here.](https://marketplacelearn.walmart.com/guides/Policies%20&%20standards/Product%20listings/duplicate-listings-policy)

* * *

I didn’t receive the email notification. What should I do?

You’ll receive a reminder notification 10 days before your item's unpublished. Take action within 10 days of the notification to avoid further action on your account.

* * *

I didn’t receive any notifications. What should I do?

If you didn’t receive any notifications, ensure that you've opted in to receive notifications for potential duplicate items identified and select the _Help_ button in the [Seller Center](https://seller.walmart.com/home) menu bar to contact Support.

* * *

I believe my item was merged in error. What should I do?

If you believe an item was merged incorrectly or if you’d like to appeal a decision, select the _Help_ button in the [Seller Center](https://seller.walmart.com/home) menu bar to contact Support.

* * *

How can I send notifications to a new user or a user without a Seller Center account?

If you’re an admin user, you can add the new user in Seller Center and they’ll need to subscribe to relevant notifications. To send notifications to a user who doesn’t have a Seller Center account, follow the instructions in [_Managing notifications in Seller Center_](https://marketplacelearn.walmart.com/guides/Getting%20started/Account%20settings/Manage-notifications-in-Seller-Center).
