Overview

You can update prices in Seller Center either individually or in bulk. This guide shows you how to update prices in your [**Catalog**](https://seller.walmart.com/catalog/manage-items) for individual SKUs or a small group of items.

## Update price individually

### Step 1 — Get started

Navigate to your [**Catalog**](https://seller.walmart.com/catalog/list-items) in Seller Center.

### Step 2 — Locate the item to update

Locate the item you need to update, then select its price from the **Current price** column. This is the final price displayed to customers on Walmart.com inclusive of active discounts, promotions, taxes or applied Repricer strategies.

Notes:

You can select multiple items at the same time, but you should only do this if you’re changing them all to the same price, e.g., updating the price of a shirt that you offer in different sizes or colors.

### Step 3 — Change the base price

Enter the new base price of the item. The base price refers to the initial price set by you, before any price adjustments are applied. When you’re ready, select _Apply_.

Notes:

Price changes may take up to four hours to update. If your price violates our [Pricing rules](https://marketplacelearn.walmart.com/guides/Catalog%20management/Price%20management/Pricing-rules), your item will be unpublished. You can update the price again, but it may take up to 48 hours for the item to be republished.
