WFS shipping plans: Schedule delivery appointments

Last updated on Jul 20, 2025

Reading time: 3 min

* * *

In this guide:

- [Overview](https://marketplacelearn.walmart.com/guides/Walmart%20Fulfillment%20Services%20(WFS)/Shipping%20to%20WFS/wfs-inbound-orders-schedule-delivery-appointments#overview)

- [How to schedule delivery](https://marketplacelearn.walmart.com/guides/Walmart%20Fulfillment%20Services%20(WFS)/Shipping%20to%20WFS/wfs-inbound-orders-schedule-delivery-appointments#scheduledelivery)

- [Frequently asked questions](https://marketplacelearn.walmart.com/guides/Walmart%20Fulfillment%20Services%20(WFS)/Shipping%20to%20WFS/wfs-inbound-orders-schedule-delivery-appointments#frequently-asked-questions)

Overview

When you use Walmart Fulfillment Services (WFS), you’ll send inventory to assigned fulfillment centers. If you use [your own carrier](https://marketplacelearn.walmart.com/guides/Walmart%20Fulfillment%20Services%20(WFS)/Shipping%20to%20WFS/WFS-inbound-orders-Choose-a-shipment-method) for less than truckload (LTL) or full truckload (FTL) shipments, the carrier will need to schedule delivery at the fulfillment center. If you’re using a [Walmart preferred carrier](https://marketplacelearn.walmart.com/guides/Walmart%20Fulfillment%20Services%20(WFS)/Shipping%20to%20WFS/WFS-Preferred-Carrier-(WPC)-program), we'll take care of this step for you.

Notes:

You must create a shipping plan _before_ you send your items to a WFS fulfillment center. If not, your inventory will be delayed during check-in or rejected.

## How to schedule delivery

A delivery appointment is required for all LTL and FTL shipments. A delivery appointment is _not_ needed for parcel shipments.

### LTL and FTL shipments

For LTL and FTL shipments, the carrier must schedule a delivery through Walmart’s [Scheduler 2.0](https://retaillink.wal-mart.com/). Carriers must have a Retail Link account to use this service. If they don’t have access, they should complete [this form to request access](https://feedbackally.walmart.com/survey/bEKcifNt4YyQ3B3/). The information must be for the person who will be using the account to schedule. For more on how to get access and schedule a delivery, go to the [Scheduler 2.0 Carrier Guide](https://azure-na-assets.contentstack.com/v3/assets/blta7903c6b840b702d/bltd216871ce88e3729/WFS-Scheduler-2.0-User-Guide?branch=us_mplearn).

If you or the carrier can’t make or no longer need the scheduled appointment, use Scheduler 2.0 to cancel at least 24 hours before the appointment time. If you don’t reschedule or cancel within the 24-hour window, you may be charged a penalty fee.

LTL and FTL shipments that are live unloads must also arrive as pallets. Go to [_WFS shipping plans: Pallet requirements_](https://marketplacelearn.walmart.com/guides/Walmart%20Fulfillment%20Services%20(WFS)/Shipping%20to%20WFS/WFS-inbound-orders-Select-&-pack-pallets) to learn more.

### Parcel shipments

We don’t require an appointment for small parcel deliveries, regardless of whether you’re using the Walmart Preferred Carrier Program or your own small parcel carrier. These are the limits for parcel:

- Maximum size: 108" in length and 165" in length plus girth (girth = 2x width + 2x height)
- Maximum weight: 150 lb.
- Some items, such as mattress pads, may be rolled up for transit. For cylinders and tubes, use diameter instead of length or width to measure size. However, note that the fulfillment center will over-box these items in order to ship them without damages. We’ll use the over-box size to calculate storage fees.

If the shipment does not meet these requirements, you must ship as LTL or FTL instead.

## Frequently asked questions

* * *

For parcel shipments, does the 150 lb. maximum weight refer to the overall shipment or to each package?

This weight refers to the total single master case. You can send multiple 150 lb. cases, which contain smaller items that do not exceed 30 lb.

* * *

Can you help provide the NMFC code list?

We recommend that you visit the [FedEx website](https://www.fedex.com/en-us/shipping/freight-services/class-calculator.html?cmp=KNC-1001816-13-10-950-1110000-US-US-EN-NBZ0001Z01ZLTLF&gclsrc=aw.ds&ds_rl=1272157&gclid=EAIaIQobChMIrqjOoqiW6AIVQtbACh0oVAiREAAYASAAEgJyPvD_BwE) for more information. When you use a Walmart preferred carrier, we can calculate this for you. In Seller Center, look for the _Estimate my freight class_ option in the _Freight class_ field.

* * *

Will the fulfillment center do pallet exchanges with drivers to help minimize the cost of pallets?

No, we do not do this at this time.

* * *

Do you offer pickup services for shipping to your fulfillment centers?

Our [Walmart Preferred Carrier Program](https://marketplacelearn.walmart.com/guides/Walmart%20Fulfillment%20Services%20(WFS)/Shipping%20to%20WFS/Walmart-Preferred-Carrier-(WPC)-program) offers pickup for less than truckload (LTL) and full truckload (FTL) shipments.

* * *

Do I need to schedule a delivery with a WFS facility?

Delivery appointments are only required for LTL or FTL. If you’re using your own carrier, follow the steps in this guide. If you’re using a Walmart preferred carrier, we’ll take care of the delivery appointment.

* * *

Do I need insurance?

We store your items in Walmart fulfillment centers, which are fully insured.
