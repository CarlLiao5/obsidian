Resold program: Item setup

Last updated on Dec 14, 2025

Reading time: 11 min

* * *

In this guide:

- [Overview](https://marketplacelearn.walmart.com/guides/Item%20setup/Resold/resold-program-item-setup#overview)

Overview

Once you’ve been approved to list Resold items and agreed to the relevant terms and conditions, the next step is to set up these items in your Marketplace catalog. In this guide, you’ll learn about the process for setting up seller-fulfilled items for the Resold program. If you’re setting up a Walmart-fulfilled (WFS) item, visit [here](https://marketplacelearn.walmart.com/guides/walmart-pre-owned-wfs-items).

Notes:

Participation in the [Resold](https://marketplacelearn.walmart.com/guides/Item%20setup/Resold/resold-program-overview) program is by invitation only, and not available to all Marketplace sellers. If you’d like to sell Pre-Owned items through the Resold program, visit the [**Exclusives programs**](https://seller.walmart.com/growth/exclusive-programs) page in Seller Center to apply.

## Item setup

### Step 1 – Get started

Before you begin the setup process, make sure your Resold items meet the following requirements:

| Attribute | Requirements |
| --- | --- |
| **Product identifier (ID)** | - Restored: The UPC or GTIN for all restored products in any of the eligible conditions must be different than the original product ID (also referred to as a new equivalent product ID). You’re responsible for providing your own product IDs, a required attribute for item setup. <br>  <br>  <br>  <br>  The product ID and restored ID are both required during a restored item setup and should be the same for both fields. You can also provide the original product ID in the original product ID field during item setup. <br>  <br>- Pre-Owned: The UPC or GTIN for items in different conditions can be the same as the original product ID (also referred to as a new equivalent product ID). You can use the item’s original product ID. Learn more about  [product identifiers](https://marketplacelearn.walmart.com/guides/Item%20setup/Item%20setup/Choose-a-product-identifier). |
| **SKU** | SKUs should be unique to items in different conditions. You can't use the same SKU for an item in a different condition. |
| **Model number** | If your product has a model number, use a different model number for the restored item than the one used for a new item. <br>A common way of adjusting the model number is to add RB after the model number (e.g., new item model number: APL\_10997 refurbished item model number: APL-10997RB). |
| **Product title** | Walmart will add the item’s condition (such as Restored, Pre-Owned, Open Box or Remanufactured) to the beginning of the title. If you change these additions, your offer will be unpublished. For example: <br>- "Pre-Owned Primary Clothing Girl's Size 10 Dress" <br>  <br>- "Open Box Samsung Galaxy S21 5G SM-G991U1 Gray" <br>  <br>Avoid using additional condition-specific keywords in the title. |
| **Condition** | Select the item’s [condition type](https://marketplacelearn.walmart.com/guides/Item%20setup/Item%20setup/Item-condition-types) based on its level of wear, prior use (if any), appearance and overall quality. |
| **Image requirements** | Product images that show the item's condition are required for the following conditions: <br>- Remanufactured <br>  <br>- Pre-Owned: like new <br>  <br>- Pre-Owned: good <br>  <br>- Pre-Owned: fair <br>  <br>- New with defects <br>  <br>Images help set the customer's expectations about the item they will receive. Product images can’t be the same as those used in listings for the same product in new condition and must comply with our [image guidelines](https://marketplacelearn.walmart.com/guides/Item%20setup/Item%20content,%20imagery,%20and%20media/Product-detail-page:-Image-guidelines-&-requirements) and requirements. |
| **Warranty** | - Restored:<br>  <br>  Restored: premium items must include this text in the **Warranty Information** field: <br>  <br>  <br>  <br>  “This product comes with a minimum one-year warranty direct from either the manufacturer(s), a manufacturer-authorized or other qualifying seller or supplier. For more detail, visit [Walmart Restored](https://www.walmart.com/help/article/walmart-restored/1cd5cc0c3cef40c79a4b2305890b1370)."<br>  <br>  <br>  <br>  Your item may be unpublished if you fail to include this text.<br>  <br>  <br>  <br>  If you offer a warranty that’s longer than one year, you can add that to the text. You can also insert a link to your warranty in the **Warranty URL** field. <br>  <br>- Pre-Owned: <br>  <br>  A warranty isn’t required for Pre-Owned conditions unless otherwise required by law. However, you must accurately disclose to customers the warranty available (if any), or if the product is sold without any warranty. |

Pro tip

We suggest using certain IDs for each product type. Email [Restored@walmart.com](mailto:Restored@walmart.com) for more information.

### Step 2 – Choose an item setup method

You can use any [item setup method](https://marketplacelearn.walmart.com/guides/Getting%20started/Getting%20ready%20to%20sell/Item-setup-methods:-Overview) to set up your items in the Resold program. However, there are a few feature limitations for setting up the following resold product types depending on its condition:

|     |     |
| --- | --- |
| - Auto wipers <br>  <br>- Car speakers <br>  <br>- CB radios and scanners <br>  <br>- Cooktops <br>  <br>- Hand lawn edgers <br>  <br>- Humidifiers <br>  <br>- Intercoms <br>  <br>- Inverter generators | - Magazines<br>- Modems<br>- Pilates machines<br>- Pole saws<br>- Printing presses<br>- Rotary hammers<br>- VCRs<br>- Wet/dry vacuum cleaners<br>- Laptop computers |

The table below provides a guideline for setup methods of different Resold condition types:

| Method | Description |
| --- | --- |
| **Express setup** | Search results only display the new condition version along with the existing number of offers across all condition types.<br>- **Pre-Owned conditions:** Set up an offer using the original product ID and select the condition value. After they're created, New and Pre-Owned items will share a single item ID and product ID (GTIN, UPC, ISBN). Unique identifiers will now reference the SKU and condition at the offer level.<br>  <br>- **Restored conditions:** Set up an offer by searching the Restored item in the correct condition or by using the GTIN on an existing Restored item within the Walmart catalog. You must include the existing product ID value as the Restored product ID value during offer setup. |
| **Full setup** | - The template sections are grouped by their requirement level: Required, Conditional/Required for specific conditions and Optional. <br>- Include all product IDs, SKUs and required attributes.<br>- **Pre-Owned conditions**: You may use the new equivalent GTIN as the product IDs (GTIN, UPC, EAN, ISBN) and condition attribute value for item setup. The item setup references both these values to accurately associate your item with its new condition counterpart. <br>- **Restored conditions:** The product ID and Restored ID must be the same value. Add your restored ID into both the product ID and restored product ID fields. You can include the new equivalent product ID in the original product ID field during item setup. |

## Frequently asked questions

* * *

How do I find the new equivalent or original product identifier (UPC/GTIN/Item ID)?

You can find the new equivalent or original product identifier by viewing the item on Walmart.com and copying the nine-digit number at the end of the url following the forward slash (/). In this example (https://www.walmart.com/linen-tote-bag/123456789), the product identifier is 123456789.

* * *

How long will it take for my item to be published on Walmart.com?

After submitting your item, you can track its status from the [**Activity Feed**](https://seller.walmart.com/catalog/activity-feed?feedsFor=uploads) in Seller Center. Updates may take up to 24 hours. Any data errors that are detected during the updates will require extra processing time.

* * *

Are Walmart-fulfilled (WFS) items eligible for the Resold program?

Yes. You’ll use the original GTIN to set up the item, but we’ll also generate a custom fulfillment GTIN to track your Resold item through the Walmart network. The fulfillment GTIN is what’s available on the item label. Visit [_Resold at Walmart: WFS items_](https://marketplacelearn.walmart.com/guides/walmart-pre-owned-wfs-items) for more information.

* * *

Why does my Walmart-fulfilled Pre-Owned item have two GTINs?

The original GTIN is associated with a new-condition item and was licensed from GS1. We use the original GTIN for item maintenance. The fulfillment GTIN is a custom identifier created by the system when you set up a Walmart-fulfilled item. The item label has the fulfillment GTIN so we can track your Pre-Owned item.

* * *

What product types are eligible to list in the Resold program?

If you're an approved seller, you'll be eligible to set up approved product types in the item set up. If an item isn't eligible, you’ll receive and error and won’t be able to set it up. If you need assistance troubleshooting eligible items, select the _Help_ button in the Seller Center menu bar to contact Support.

* * *

Are Resold items eligible for Sponsored Search campaigns?

Yes, resold items are eligible for [Sponsored Search campaigns](https://marketplacelearn.walmart.com/guides/Getting%20started/Walmart%20Connect/Advertise-with-Walmart-Connect-sponsored-search) with some exceptions. Items in the following product types are not eligible: modems, magazines, auto wipers, VCRs, rotary hammers, intercoms, pole saws, printing presses, CB radios and scanners, wet/ dry vacuum cleaners, humidifiers, car speakers, pilates machines, cooktops, hand lawn edgers and inverter generators.

* * *

Can I enroll Resold items in Search Engine Marketing (SEM) campaigns?

Yes, resold items can be enrolled in [SEM](https://marketplacelearn.walmart.com/guides/Advertising/Search%20Engine%20Marketing%20(SEM)/Search-Engine-Marketing-(SEM):-program-overview) campaigns with some exceptions. Items in the following product types are not eligible: modems, magazines, auto wipers, VCRs, rotary hammers, intercoms, pole saws, printing presses, CB radios and scanners, wet/ dry vacuum cleaners, humidifiers, car speakers, pilates machines, cooktops, hand lawn edgers and inverter generators.

* * *

Why was my Resold item made Buy Box ineligible?

Similar to new items, Resold items are also subject to Buy Box ineligibility rules. If a Resold item is priced substantially higher than prices currently or recently offered on Walmart.com or on competing websites, but not high enough to require automatic unpublishing, we’ll remove the _Add to Cart_ button, as well as offer details such as price and shipping from the item page. In addition, Walmart may make Resold (such as Pre-Owned, Restored or Open Box) items ineligible for the Buy Box if their price exceeds that of a new item on the same product page. Exceptions may apply for certain categories, such as collectibles or some luxury brands.
