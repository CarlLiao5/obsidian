## Audio ads for Walmart Radio

Advertisers are bound by the Walmart Connect Ad Policies, including but not limited to [Prohibited Products & Services](https://advertisinghelp.walmart.com/s/guides?channel=Sponsored%20Products&article=000010872). Further restrictions may apply above and beyond our Prohibited Products & Services. All ads intended for Walmart Radio will require advance approval by the Ad Policy team.  
  
The following are **required** for Walmart Radio audio ads:

- Ads must be distinguishable from regular Walmart Radio content and Walmart-provided promotional ads.
	- Advertiser and/or product name must be clearly said within ad.
		- Language indicating the ad is sponsored must be included.
- Ads must feature only one advertiser and their product or service.
- Products must be sold in-store or on walmart.com. Services must be available in-store.
- Ads must not be disruptive to the in-store customer experience.
	- Walmart Connect reserves the right to reject or remove any ad that has potential to disturb the shopping experience or are inconsistent with Walmart brand standards.
- Music or background sounds must not be louder than dialogue/sales copy.

  
The following are **prohibited** for Walmart Radio audio ads:

- Ads implying Walmart endorsement.
- The following sound effects are prohibited:
	- Bleeps, including bleeping of profanity.
		- Emergency Alert System (EAS) tones, tornado, horns or other similar sound effects that have potential to alarm customers and imitate an official emergency signal.
		- Sound effects simulating bodily functions.
		- Sound effects resembling guns/bullets or explosions.
		- Drops or extended pauses in audio.
		- Loud or disruptive sounds.
- Copy that can be confused with store announcements.
- Copy that solicits, targets or directly addresses Walmart associates.
- URLs, phone numbers, hashtags.
- Contests, sweepstakes, special giveaways and other promotions.

## Connected TV (CTV) Guidelines

### General Guidelines

For CTV ads, we generally defer to our platform partner’s ad policies, with the noted exceptions below. Ads must comply with all policies of the platform on which the ad will air.

The following Walmart Connect Ad Policies must be adhered to at all times, even if the platform policies differ.

- [Prohibited Products & Services](https://advertisinghelp.walmart.com/s/guides?channel=Sponsored%20Products&article=000010872)
- [Targeting Policy](https://advertisinghelp.walmart.com/s/guides?channel=Sponsored%20Products&article=000010874#targeting_policy)
- [Prescription Drugs and Vaccines](https://advertisinghelp.walmart.com/s/guides?language=en_US&channel=Sponsored%20Products&article=000010875#pharm-manufacturers-resellers)
- Any ads utilizing Walmart branding within ad creative must adhere to all relevant Walmart Connect Ad Policies. See [Walmart Branding](#walmart-branding-ctv) section below for additional requirements.

### Walmart Branding

Walmart branding, including the Walmart logo, URL and voiceover callout, are not required for CTV ads. If Walmart branding is utilized in creative, with the exception of the "Available at Walmart" logo, the Vendor-Tagged Ad (VTA) process must be completed.

Additionally, ads utilizing Walmart branding are required to adhere to all Walmart Connect Ad Policies, including but not limited to:

- [Video Requirements](https://advertisinghelp.walmart.com/s/guides?channel=Display&article=000010873)
- [Category Specific Policies](https://advertisinghelp.walmart.com/s/guides?channel=Sponsored%20Products&article=000010875)
- [Ad Copy & Image Requirements](https://advertisinghelp.walmart.com/s/guides?language=en_US&channel=Sponsored%20Products&article=000010873#ad_copy_and_image_recs)

  
The following guidelines will apply to the use of Walmart branding in CTV creatives that include the below elements:

- Alcohol
	- Ads promoting alcohol on CTV must not include Walmart branding. See the full Alcohol policy [here](https://advertisinghelp.walmart.com/s/guides?language=en_US&channel=Sponsored%20Products&article=000010873#image_reqs).
- Sweepstakes/Contests:
	- Ads promoting supplier-sponsored sweepstakes/contests must not include Walmart branding. See the full Sweepstakes policy [here](https://advertisinghelp.walmart.com/s/guides?language=en_US&channel=Sponsored%20Products&article=000010873#sweepstakes).
- Prescription Drugs:
	- For branded prescription products and vaccines, Walmart logo/URL should not be included.
		- The Walmart logo/URL is allowed for unbranded vaccine ads.
		- As a reminder, all prescription drug ads require advanced review and approval by Ad Policy & Legal. See the full Prescription Drug policy [here](https://advertisinghelp.walmart.com/s/guides?channel=Sponsored%20Products&article=000010875#health_wellness).
- Sexual Wellness:
	- Ads promoting sexual wellness products must not use Walmart branding. See the full Sexual Wellness policy [here](https://advertisinghelp.walmart.com/s/guides?language=en_US&channel=Sponsored%20Products&article=000010875#sexual-wellness).
- Non-endemic:
	- Use of Walmart branding in non-endemic ads is not permitted. See the full Non-Endemic Policy [here](https://advertisinghelp.walmart.com/s/guides?language=en_US&channel=Sponsored%20Products&article=000010875#Offsite).
- Pricing:
	- Ads utilizing Walmart branding should not include specific pricing or savings claims.
		- **Exceptions:**
			- Non-endemics may use pricing for products or services unrelated to Walmart.
						- Pricing ads with Multiple Retailer Tagging is permitted, provided the item(s) being promoted is the Minimum Advertised Price (MAP).

## Social media

Walmart Connect supports social media advertising across the following platforms on the Walmart handle: Meta (i.e., Facebook and Instagram), Pinterest and TikTok. This policy only applies to media purchased via Walmart Connect and airs on Walmart social handles.

Advertisers and suppliers are bound by Walmart Connect Ad Policies and all applicable laws and regulations. Additionally, advertisers must ensure adherence to the platform policies and guidelines of social media partners.

### Requirements

**Walmart Branding**

- Walmart branding in social media ads does not need VTA-approval.
- Use of Walmart stores, bags, website, and app are allowed in social media ads.
- Social media ads that are repurposed for Sponsored Videos may not use Walmart stores, bags, website or app.
- Ads may tag other accounts (e.g., @supplier, @influencer), granted the tag does not show up as an active link that can lead to another account.

**Intellectual property, influencers and claims**

- Ads must follow all [Ad copy and specific claim and IP rights](https://advertisinghelp.walmart.com/s/guides?language=en_US&channel=Sponsored%20Products&article=000010873#ad_copy_and_image_recs) requirements.
- Any social media ads that contain celebrities and/or influencers must adhere to the FTC Guidance for Influencers and Guidelines on Endorsements.
- Any usage of testimonial in influencer content could trigger the following requests:
	- Confirming the user has actually used the product being advertised
		- Confirming the review is for the specific product being advertised
		- Obtaining a written testimonial release from the reviewer
		- Ensuring the advertiser substantiates any claim being made in the review
		- Obtaining the advertiser’s legal approval.

## Entertainment: Movies, TV shows & video games

Advertisers are bound by [Walmart Connect Ad Policies](https://advertisinghelp.walmart.com/s/guides?article=000009813&channel=Sponsored), including but not limited to the [Prohibited Content policy,](https://advertisinghelp.walmart.com/s/guides?channel=Sponsored%20Products&article=000010872) [Image Requirements policy](https://advertisinghelp.walmart.com/s/guides?channel=Sponsored%20Products&article=000010873#image_reqs) and the [Video Requirements policy](https://advertisinghelp.walmart.com/s/guides?channel=Sponsored%20Products&article=000010873#Video_requirements).  
  
Additionally, advertisers must ensure they adhere to the platform guidelines of all third-party partners.  

### General requirements

- All entertainment ads require prior approval. In-store ads, onsite ads, Walmart branded channels and ads with the Walmart logo and/or linking back to Walmart.com must feature content with a G-rated audience acceptability only, regardless of the advertised media’s actual rating. Ads for mature-rated media will be subject to increased scrutiny.
- Entertainment shown on product screens within advertisements for TVs, gaming consoles and other electronics must have G-rated audience acceptability only.
- Offsite landing pages require prior approval consistent with the [Landing Page](https://advertisinghelp.walmart.com/s/guides?channel=Sponsored%20Products&article=000010873#landing_page_recs) & [Non-Endemic policy](https://advertisinghelp.walmart.com/s/guides?channel=Sponsored%20Products&article=000010875#Offsite).

### Content guidelines

The following content guidelines apply across all entertainment categories and are not meant to be exhaustive. The Walmart Connect Ad Policy team may at any time reject or remove advertisements found to be in violation of these policies, revoke approval, suspend campaigns, require the revision of an advertisement or require pre-review prior to launch as a condition of approval at its sole discretion. We also reserve the right to update these policies at any time without prior notice.

The following applies to in-store ads, onsite ads, Walmart-branded channels and ads with the Walmart logo and/or linking back to Walmart.com:

- As stated in the General Requirements above, all ads must feature content with a G-rated audience acceptability only, regardless of the advertised media’s actual rating.
- Depictions of realistic violence (live action or animated) are prohibited. This includes, but is not limited to:
	- Bodily fluids, blood, bruises, bone, etc.
		- Death, bodily harm, self-harm or loss of consciousness as a result of violence.
		- Dark, tense verbal or body language that is indicating life-like violence or a build-up to life-like violence.
		- Life-like appearing firearms, knives, other deadly weapons or objects clearly being treated as weapons, being held, used or depicted in such a way that alludes to use.
		- Exceptions on weapon presentation may be provided for box art based on the imagery’s themes.
- Audio that references and/or encourages intense violence is prohibited.
- Depictions of children in peril are prohibited.
- Disturbing horror, creepy or grotesque imagery that is intended to allude to violence or danger is prohibited.
- Weapons that wouldn’t exist in real-life may be acceptable based on appearance.
	- Whimsical combat with a fun, friendly tone is generally permitted. Combat in E-rated video games is generally acceptable.
- All portrayals of drugs, tobacco, vaping and paraphernalia are prohibited.
- All depictions and implications of alcohol presence, consumption and/or effects are prohibited. This includes depictions of environments primarily associated with alcohol and beverages/containers resembling alcohol or alcohol packaging.
- Full, partial and implied nudity is prohibited.
	- Brief moments of contextually appropriate partial nudity (e.g., swimsuits at the beach) may be acceptable.
- Ads featuring celebrities or influencers will require prior approval.

Ads airing offsite with no Walmart logo or linkbacks to Walmart.com must adhere to the same content guidelines above. Allowances can be made for the following content:

- Direct depictions of alcohol consumption and effects remain prohibited. However, general alcohol imagery and environments implying consumption are acceptable provided the following:
	- Alcohol consumption is not being encouraged, glamorized or associated with underage usage. This includes scenes depicting or implying excessive, irresponsible or illegal consumption.
		- All individuals appear to be 21 or older.
- Life-like appearing firearms and other weapons may be portrayed in use provided the following:
	- Portrayal is not excessive.
		- Weapons are not directly pointing at or being used against a living being.
		- Weapons are not directly pointing at the camera/audience.
		- Children are not featured in the same scene as weapon usage.

### Movies

- MPA rating is required in both visual and audio for all movie ads.
- NC-17 and Unrated movies are prohibited.
- PG-13 and R-rated movie targeting must only include relevant and appropriate audiences.

### Video Games

- Advertisers must adhere to all ESRB guidelines.
- Adults Only and Unrated games are prohibited.
- Consistent with our [Prohibited Content policy](https://advertisinghelp.walmart.com/s/guides?channel=Sponsored%20Products&article=000010872), ads may not target, market toward or otherwise appeal to children.
- T and M-rated video game targeting must only target relevant and appropriate audiences.

### TV Shows

- TV-14, TV-16+, TV-MA or reasonably equivalently rated shows must only target relevant and appropriate audiences.

## Pharmacy, health & wellness

Behavioral targeting for Health & Wellness ads are restricted and limited in nature. A past purchase/behavior that infers that a customer has an underlying health condition, may not be used for any targeting. Please consult our [Targeting Policy](https://advertisinghelp.walmart.com/s/guides?channel=Sponsored%20Products&article=000010874#targeting_policy) for more information.  

### Prescription Drugs & Vaccines

- Ads for the sale of prescription drugs require advanced approval by Walmart Legal and Ad Policy.
- All advertised prescription drugs must be FDA approved.
- All ads for prescription drugs must comply with [FDA guidelines](https://www.fda.gov/drugs/information-consumers-and-patients-drugs/prescription-drug-advertising), including without limitation:
	- Product Claim ads must name a drug and discuss the associated risks and benefits. They must include:
		- The brand name AND generic name of the drug.
				- One or more FDA approved uses for the drugs.
				- The most significant risks.
				- Links to web pages with the necessary prescribing information.
		- The information provided must be consistent with the product labeling and prescribing information.
- Prescription drug ads may *not*:
	- Make claims that are false, misleading or confusing to the consumer.
		- Encourage or romanticize the use of prescription drugs.
		- Promote the direct sale of prescription drugs.
		- Make claims against other competitor brands/products.
		- Prescription drugs for Erectile Dysfunction will be allowed but require advanced approval and must follow the guidelines placed on sexual wellness products.
- Pharmaceutical companies *may* use geographic targeting.
- Please refer to our [targeting guidelines](https://advertisinghelp.walmart.com/s/guides?channel=Sponsored%20Products&article=000010874#targeting_policy).

### Pet Rx Products

Pet Rx Products are defined as medications, treatments and vaccines that are specifically formulated for pets/animals that require a valid prescription from a veterinarian.

- Advertisements require review by the Ad Policy Team, Walmart Connect Legal, and, when necessary, the Walmart Enterprise Legal Team to review claims.
- Advertisements must clearly state the product’s intended use and benefits and indicate that the product is intended for pets/animals only and not for human consumption.
- Any claims made in advertisements should be accurate, supported by scientific evidence and compliant with FDA regulations for Pet Rx advertising.
- Advertisements should not undermine the importance of consulting a veterinarian for proper diagnosis and prescription of pet/animal medications.
- For branded medications, FDA-approved Important Safety Information (ISI) must be displayed clearly and conspicuously.
- The following are prohibited in Pet Rx advertisements:
	- Medications that are FDA-approved for both human and pet/animal use.
		- Promotion of the use of Pet Rx Products for off-label or unauthorized uses.
		- Claims against other competitive products/brands.

### Pharmacies

- Ads for brick and mortar and online pharmacies are permitted in offsite media only.
- Ads for Telemedicine services that provide patient-to-provider care require advanced approval.
- Advertisers must be certified by NABP and/or LegitScript and provide all necessary documentation upon request.
- Ads must not promote the direct sale of prescription drugs without a prescription.

### Over the Counter (OTC) Medicine

- Ads must comply with all applicable laws and regulations, including those set by the FDA and FTC.
- Ads may not claim or imply Over the Counter medications:
	- Are as effective as prescription drugs.
		- Can treat, cure or reduce the risk of a disease or medical condition.
		- Have off-label uses.
		- Are safe, harmless or have little to no risks.
		- Have FDA approval.
- Ads must not overstate any health claims or exaggerate symptoms or conditions.
- Ads must include links to all relevant health information including benefits and risks in accordance with all applicable laws.
- Ads that promote newly approved over the counter medications (for example, those that have formally required a prescription) need advanced approval from your account team.

### Dietary Supplements and Vitamins

- All ads must comply with [FTC Dietary Supplements Advertising Guidelines](https://www.ftc.gov/business-guidance/resources/dietary-supplements-advertising-guide-industry).
- Ads may not claim or imply dietary supplements:
	- Can treat, cure or reduce the risk of a disease or medical condition.
		- Are as effective as prescription drugs.
		- Have off-label uses.
		- Are safe, harmless or have little to no risks.
		- Have FDA approval or clearance.
- Ads for dietary supplements and vitamins may not contain:
	- Unsafe or illegal/restricted ingredients, including but not limited to CBD, THC or other cannabinoids.
		- Illegal or restricted drug imagery and paraphernalia.
		- Unsubstantiated health claims or guaranteed results.
		- Testimonials and endorsements.
- Ads must not overstate any health claims or exaggerate symptoms or conditions.
- All claims must be substantiated per [FDA Guidance for Industry: Dietary Supplements](https://www.fda.gov/regulatory-information/search-fda-guidance-documents/guidance-industry-substantiation-dietary-supplement-claims-made-under-section-403r-6-federal-food). Substantiation may be requested as part of the review process.

### Period Products

- Ads that contain images and videos must not contain nudity, implied nudity or sexually suggestive imagery.
- Ads that contain images of adults in undergarments are allowed if used within the context of the product being advertised and are not sexual in nature.
- Ads that contain images of minors in their undergarments is prohibited.

### Home Test Kits

Advertisers and suppliers are bound by [Walmart Connect's Ad Policies](https://advertisinghelp.walmart.com/s/guides?article=000009813&channel=Display), including but not limited to the [Prohibited Content policy](https://advertisinghelp.walmart.com/s/guides?channel=Sponsored%20Products&article=000010872), and all applicable laws and regulations.  
  
Walmart Connect reserves the right to change these guidelines, rescind campaign approval and pull ads at any time without prior notice.  

#### Required

- Home test kit products must comply with all applicable laws and regulations.
- Home test kit products must not be part of any recalls.
- Consistent with our ad policy on [pricing](https://advertisinghelp.walmart.com/s/guides?channel=Sponsored%20Products&article=000010873), no exact pricing or savings claims are permitted. In the event pricing is discussed, any additional lab fees must be disclosed.

#### Prohibited

- Resellers of home test kits that are unauthorized by Walmart are prohibited.
- Products marketed for professional or prescription-only use are prohibited.
- The following types of home test kits are prohibited from all ad placements:
	- Specimen and substance drug test kits.
		- Sexual wellness-related test kits. (For advertising pregnancy or pregnancy-related tests, reach out to your account team.)
		- Forensic test kits.

#### Restricted

Depending on home test kit type, different restrictions on ad placement and targeting eligibility may apply. Contact your account team for more information.  

### Sexual Wellness

- Ads that promote condoms, lubricants, intimate hygiene and other nonprescription contraceptives are acceptable with advanced approval.
	- Ads *must*:
		- Only promote either the health benefit, product attributes or product branding of the contraceptive.
				- Use exact match keyword targeting or contextual targeting only.
		- Ads must *not*:
		- Use the Walmart logo on the ad creative (offsite).
				- Use behavioral targeting.
				- Promote casual sex or promiscuity, sexual pleasure or enhancement or increased libido.
				- Use sexually explicit or implicit content may not be utilized in the advertisement. This includes provocative imagery or phrases.
				- Include potentially offensive content in relation to pregnancy but may mention the effectiveness of the product.

### Adult Toys

All advertisements related to adult toys require prior approval from Ad Policy. Contact your account team for more information.

### Weight Loss Products and Claims

- Ads for weight loss products and services are permitted with advanced approval. Contact your Walmart Connect account team to get started.
- Any claims made by advertisers, both express and implied, must comply with the [FTC guidelines](https://www.ftc.gov/business-guidance/resources/gut-check-reference-guide-media-spotting-false-weight-loss-claims). The FTC guidelines are in place to ensure claims are not deceptive or misleading.
- Ads must not make any of the following claims that the FTC has identified as consistently false. These claims are that weight loss products:
	- Cause weight loss of two pounds or more a week for a month or more without dieting or exercise.
		- Cause substantial weight loss no matter what or how much the consumer eats.
		- Cause permanent weight loss even after the consumer stops using product.
		- Block the absorption of fat or calories to enable consumers to lose substantial weight.
		- Safely enable consumers to lose more than three pounds per week for more than four weeks.
		- Cause substantial weight loss for all users.
		- Cause substantial weight loss by wearing a product on the body or rubbing it into the skin.
- The use of weight loss buzzwords that have the intent to communicate a hack or quick-fix are prohibited. This includes but is not limited to:
	- Words such as magic, miracle, hack, detox, cleanse, quick-fix, etc.
		- Language comparing the products to prescription medications.
		- Phrases suggesting the consumer does not need to engage in other behaviors to lose weight such as "no cutting calories" or "no exercise needed."
- References to GLP-1s are prohibited, except on product packaging.
- Ads containing endorsements or testimonials should adhere to all policies related to endorsements and testimonials on our [General, Creative and Editorial Standards page](https://advertisinghelp.walmart.com/s/guides?article=000010873&channel=Sponsored). Additionally, weight loss ads must not include:
	- The use or implication of doctor endorsement or testimonials.
		- Items claiming to be endorsed by influential figures with notable programs and personalities.
		- Imagery implying that the consumer can look like a celebrity or specific person through the use of their product. However, ads may imply that a persons’ condition can improve.
- Ads for weight loss products must focus only on the positive aspects of their products.
	- They may not contain any messaging or imagery that is shaming, harmful or intended to cause the consumer to have negative feelings towards their appearance or behaviors. This includes but is not limited to:
		- Idealizing unhealthy body types or behaviors such as extreme diets and over-exercising.
				- Implying that there is an ideal physique or that the consumer should want to achieve a certain weight or look.
				- Highlighting common insecurities.
				- Using imagery such as before-and-after comparisons, altered photographs, scales, tape measurers and health conditions.
				- Mentioning BMI.
- Ads must only show supplements in full bottles or in the intended dosage.
- Ads must only feature those within the intended age range for the product.
- Ads for weight loss bracelets, nighttime weight loss aids and other products that make wishful weight loss claims are prohibited.

### Health and Medical Devices

- Ads for over the counter/non-prescription health and medical devices or tools are permitted with advanced approval.
- Ads for any devices, products or tools that require supervision from a medical professional are prohibited.
- Ads for the direct sale of prescription medical devices are prohibited (e.g., Contact Lens companies may advertise, but may not direct the consumer to a specific purchasing site or vision center).
- Ads for everyday use devices (e.g., bandages, tongue depressors) are generally allowed.
