This guide takes you through the first step in creating a new Sponsored Products manual campaign: choosing your campaign type (manual) and setting your flight dates and budget. You’ll also learn how to edit your budget and apply in-flight out-of-budget recommendations.

## Campaign type and targeting tactic

In the  *Targeting* section, select **Sponsored Products** under *Select Campaign Type* as and targeting tactic. For targeting, you have the choice between Automatic and Manual. 

- **Automatic** — Leverages Walmart Connect's AI and machine learning to target and optimize ads, which determines the right audience and placements for your products. Automatic campaigns are designed to supercharge performance, expand reach, and reduce effort—delivering more results with less effort. For more information on automatic campaigns, visit the [Create an automatic campaign guide](https://advertisinghelp.walmart.com/s/guides?article=000008379&channel=Sponsored%20Products&language=en_US).

- **Manual** — Gives you precise control as it allows you to choose exact products and keywords you want to target, with limited placements available. 
	- We recommend using manual when you are looking to:
		- Ideal for supplementing automatic campaigns to bid aggressively on top-performing terms
				- Focusing on a limited keyword set
				- With products that have a long history on Walmart

## Scheduling and budget

In the  *Scheduling & Budget* section, set your campaign duration and budget. Select a **Start date**  for your campaign. Selecting the current date (today) will launch your campaign within the hour.  Enter a **Daily budget**. You cannot launch a campaign without a daily budget set.

![image.png](https://advertisinghelp.walmart.com/servlet/rtaImage?eid=ka0KW000000oP0M&feoid=00N6100000Hxa5T&refid=0EM8Y00000DjeIJ)

*Daily Budget*  represents the most you will spend each day of the campaign. As your account will be charged per ad click, daily spend may vary. It cannot exceed your total budget, if set. 

- Walmart suppliers must set a daily budget of $50 or above.
- Marketplace sellers must set a daily budget of $10 or above.

**Note:** If your campaign does not spend all of the daily budget, the unused amount will roll over to the next day (and continue to do so if unused). However, daily spend will never exceed 2X the daily budget.   
   
For example:  

If you set a daily budget of $100 and only spend $50, the daily budget for the following day, with the addition of rollover budget, will be $150. 

OR 

If you set a daily budget of $500 and spend $100, the daily budget for the following day will be $900. If your campaign again spends $100, the daily budget for the following day will be $1000 (capped at 2X your set daily budget). 

With these settings, your campaign will default to run indefinitely until you choose to pause or end it. To set an end date and/or a total budget, expand the Additional settings section.

## End date and total budget

You can choose to set an  **End Date**  and/or a  **Total Budget**  for your campaign. An end date will stop your campaign even if there is still budget left. A total budget will end the campaign as soon as the total budget has been spent.

*Total Budget* represents the most you will spend on the campaign. As your account will be charged per ad click, the final campaign spend may be equal to or less than the total budget but will **never exceed** it. If you choose to set a total budget, you must adhere to the following minimums.

- Walmart suppliers must set a total budget of $100 or above.
- Marketplace sellers must set a total budget of $50 or above.

**Note:** If you do not set a total budget, before the campaign starts you will not be able to add one once the campaign is live.

If you set **both** an end date and total budget, an **Auto Calculate**  option will populate in the *Daily Budget* field. If you select it, the system will automatically divide your total budget by the total number of days your campaign will run, to provide an equally distributed amount. For example, if your campaign is 30 days long and you set a total budget of $6,000, selecting **Auto Calculate**  will set your Daily budget to $200.

## Campaign bidding strategy

In the Campaign bidding strategy section, choose the bidding strategy that best suits your business goals. 

**Dynamic bidding**  – This is an optimized bidding strategy designed to help Sponsored Products advertisers save time and effort, maximize conversion and optimize ad spend. With dynamic bidding, Walmart Connect’s platform adjusts cost-per-click bids up or down in real time based on the likelihood that a customer will purchase the item if the ad appears in their search. To learn more about how dynamic bidding works, visit the [Campaign bidding strategies guide.](https://advertisinghelp.walmart.com/s/guides?language=en_US&channel=Sponsored%20Products&article=000011286)

**Fixed Bidding**  – Allows you to manually control how much you bid. Advertisers enter a base bid that represents the most they are willing to pay.

## Editing live campaigns

When editing an in-flight campaign, you may see an *Out-of-budget*  or *Budget-at-risk*  tag at the top of the screen. This means your campaign may stop serving, while the status remains *Live*. These tags appear only when your campaign’s daily and/or total budget is at risk of running out before the scheduled end date or when the budget has already run out.  

For more information, please review [Out-of-budget features](https://advertisinghelp.walmart.com/s/guides?language=en_US&channel=Sponsored%20Products&article=000012383).

After you’ve selected these details, proceed to either the optional Bid Multiplier or Placement Inclusion sections (for manual campaigns).
