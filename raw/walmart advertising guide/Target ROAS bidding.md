Walmart Connect is introducing optimized bidding strategies designed to help advertisers save time, effectively manage their budget and maximize their campaign potential to help achieve their campaign performance goals. These bidding strategies are **Dynamic bidding**, **Target ROAS bidding** and **Fixed bidding**.

This article provides an overview of the Target ROAS bidding strategy, including how to use it and understand the campaign journey and best practices.

## Target ROAS bidding

Target ROAS bidding is a strategy that can help you effectively achieve their campaign ROAS goals. Using Al, our algorithm calculates the optimal bids needed to help achieve your ROAS goal, which is designed to enhance campaign efficiency by saving time and effort and supporting you in making strategic investments that can help to achieve your campaign goals.

**Important****:** A $50 per day minimum budget is required for all Target ROAS campaigns to help ensure effective optimization and campaign performance.

**Benefits:**

- **Boost performance:** Set and optimize bids in real time designed to help maximize sales and achieve your ROAS goals.
- **Save time:** No need to manually set bids and monitor performance regularly - our algorithm does it for you.
- **Smarter budget allocation:** **Direct** investment where it can help deliver better outcomes by ensuring bids are set efficiently to achieve your ROAS goals.

Target ROAS is available for automatic Sponsored Products campaigns for both Walmart suppliers and Marketplace sellers.

## How Target ROAS bidding works

### Use Target ROAS bidding for recommended existing campaigns

Target ROAS bidding is only available for existing campaigns. You can transition an existing campaign with the help of recommendations found in the Switch your bidding strategy widget on the Dashboard, All Campaigns page or within the Campaign Edit page.

A $50 per day minimum budget applies to any existing campaign that is being transitioned to Target ROAS. Additionally, you can set ROAS targets up to 20% above their 28-day average.

**Note:**Target ROAS bidding is not available for new campaigns.

![](https://advertisinghelp.walmart.com/servlet/rtaImage?eid=ka0KW000000oPGj&feoid=00N6100000Hxa5T&refid=0EMKW000008jWOg)

The *Switch your bidding strategy* recommendation shows your current campaigns that are using either Dynamic or Fixed bidding strategies, which could be driving higher sales if switched to Target ROAS bidding. Click **Apply all** to transition all those campaigns to Target ROAS or click **View recommendations** to see the suggested ROAS targets and budgets for each campaign — here you can apply recommendations individually.

![](https://advertisinghelp.walmart.com/servlet/rtaImage?eid=ka0KW000000oPGj&feoid=00N6100000Hxa5T&refid=0EMKW000008jTbS)

If you want to apply a different ROAS target than the one recommended, click on the campaign name to open the Campaign Edit page, where you can enable Target ROAS manually with the target you have in mind.

![](https://advertisinghelp.walmart.com/servlet/rtaImage?eid=ka0KW000000oPGj&feoid=00N6100000Hxa5T&refid=0EMKW000008jTbr)

For campaigns we predict can achieve potentially higher sales by using Target ROAS bidding, you will see a personalized recommendation on the Campaign Edit page, All Campaigns page and the Dashboard, including more optimal ROAS target and budget recommendations to guide you.

**Tip:** When setting your Target ROAS goal, start with your current average performance and gradually increase up to 20%, as results stabilize.

### Optimizing Target ROAS campaigns

In addition to the *Switch your bidding strategy* widget, two more widgets supporting Target ROAS campaigns have been added to your Dashboard:

- *Update ROAS target*
- *Review campaigns with paused learning*

![](https://advertisinghelp.walmart.com/servlet/rtaImage?eid=ka0KW000000oPGj&feoid=00N6100000Hxa5T&refid=0EMKW000008jTn4)

These widgets offer more insights that can help you improve the performance of your Target ROAS campaigns. For more information on optimizing your Target ROAS campaigns, visit our [Bidding strategies recommendations guide](https://advertisinghelp.walmart.com/s/guides?channel=Sponsored%20Products&language=en_US&article=000012409).

Check your campaign's reporting metrics metrics from Advertiser reports, On-demand reports and/or Custom reports once your campaign has exited the learning period. Monitor your items' health and listing quality scores to make sure items remain in-stock and with a high list quality score.

Note: Only Marketplace sellers have access to listing quality scores.

Conversion metrics like ROAS should be checked 14 days after the learning period is over to allow for sales attribution. Make sure 14-day attribution is selected to review ROAS performance for your campaigns with a minimum 14-day date range starting one day after the learning period completes. If your campaign is not showing any performance after 30 days, they will show in the learning phase paused alert widget. Try switching your bidding strategy from Target ROAS to dynamic bidding OR adding more items to your campaign.

Turn on out-of-budget and budget-at-risk notifications from the All Campaigns page as well as monitoring alerts on the Dashboard to stay up to date with your campaign’s performance.

![](https://advertisinghelp.walmart.com/servlet/rtaImage?eid=ka0KW000000oPGj&feoid=00N6100000Hxa5T&refid=0EMKW0000090h5K)

You can also view and apply Target ROAS recommendations and alerts on the All Campaigns page. Learn more in the [All Campaigns](https://advertisinghelp.walmart.com/s/guides?article=000008410&channel=Sponsored&language=en_US) article.

## Understanding the campaign journey

The following steps are based on an example scenario for Advertiser A’s Target ROAS campaign journey.  
  
**Note:** Your campaign’s timeline may vary, but these steps provide a general guideline to help you optimize your campaign’s performance.

![](https://advertisinghelp.walmart.com/servlet/rtaImage?eid=ka0KW000000oPGj&feoid=00N6100000Hxa5T&refid=0EMKW0000090srZ)

### Understanding the campaign learning phase

In the first two weeks of your Target ROAS campaign (e.g., January 1–14), the algorithm is in the **learning phase**, gathering data to optimize bids. This is a crucial period where the algorithm gathers data to analyze the performance of the campaign and how to optimize bids accordingly.

Avoid changing the ROAS target, removing top-selling products or lowering the budget during this phase.  
  
**Note:**The platform won’t optimize toward your ROAS target yet. The learning phase lasts 14–30 days.

### Evaluate campaign performance

After the learning phase, allow another two weeks for the campaign to hit your ROAS goal.

Review performance over a **14-day attribution window** (e.g., January 15–28) around **February 11**. At this stage, if our algorithm predicts that a campaign could achieve higher sales with different settings, we will provide personalized recommendations including optimal ROAS targets and budgets.  
  
**Tip****:** Start evaluating performance only after the learning phase is complete.

### Monitor ROAS and adjust campaigns

From February 11 onward, the algorithm calculates ROAS based on the full attribution window.

You can adjust your campaign by adding products, increasing the budget, or lowering the ROAS target.

Avoid increasing the ROAS target, removing top-selling products or reducing the budget. These can disrupt performance. Pausing your campaign for more than five days will restart the learning phase.

**Tip:** Monitor your campaign weekly and adjust as needed based on recommendations.

### Target ROAS performance alerts

Target ROAS performance alerts can help you understand when item-level changes may be affecting your campaign performance. Target ROAS performance can shift when changes affect your promoted items, such as item status (enabled/disable), Out-of-stock issues, Buy Box win rate changes and Unit price changes. Target ROAS performance alerts proactively surface these item-level changes directly in Walmart Connect Ad Center, across the All Campaigns page and the Campaign edit page. 

Key item-level signals for live Target ROAS campaigns are monitored and when one or more of your top-selling items is impacted, you’ll see a Target ROAS performance alert. These alerts may appear when:

- An item goes out of stock
- Buy Box win rate drops
- An item becomes disabled
- An item experiences a significant price change

Select **View** on the alert to see more details about the impacted items.  

![](https://advertisinghelp.walmart.com/servlet/rtaImage?eid=ka0KW000000oPGj&feoid=00N6100000Hxa5T&refid=0EMKW00000AEQ2f)

When you open a Target ROAS performance alert, you'll see a summary of the item-level issue detected and a downloadable file link with item-level details.

![](https://advertisinghelp.walmart.com/servlet/rtaImage?eid=ka0KW000000oPGj&feoid=00N6100000Hxa5T&refid=0EMKW00000AEQ2p)

This information helps you understand which items are contributing to performance changes. 

**Note:**Alerts are informational and may persist while the item-level issue remains. Alerts are currently available for Sponsored Products campaigns using Target ROAS. 

### Target ROAS campaign auto-revert alerts

To help prevent Target ROAS campaigns from staying indefinitely in Learning paused or continuing to run in Target ROAS when performance is not improving, Walmart Connect may automatically move underperforming campaigns to the prior bidding strategy. If a prior bidding strategy is not available, the campaign will be moved to **Dynamic bidding**.

You will receive the following campaign alerts that provide clear visibility before and after this change:

- **Warning** — Your campaign is scheduled to shift out of Target ROAS.
- **Confirmation** — Your campaign has shifted out of Target ROAS and reverted to the prior bidding strategy (or **Dynamic bidding**, if a prior strategy is not available).

After this change, the campaign will be ineligible to move back into Target ROAS for 10 days. During this period, the Target ROAS option will be unavailable and Target ROAS recommendations will not be shown for that campaign.

## Best practices

To help you achieve optimal results with your Target ROAS campaigns, we recommend that you:  

- Determine your Target ROAS goal by starting with your current average performance and gradually increasing it up to +20%, as results stabilize.
- Avoid frequently increasing your campaign's ROAS goal — this can cause the campaign to underperform or lose learnt insights, as it takes time to stabilize the campaign with a new ROAS target.
- Avoid switching from Target ROAS to fixed or dynamic bidding in-flight, to ensure optimal results.
- Avoid reducing budgets and/or disabling items for in-flight campaigns. This may restart the campaign learning phase and affect your overall campaign performance.
- Review your out-of-budget recommendations and increase your budgets to meet or exceed the $50 per day minimum.
- Continue to apply platform recommendations from Dashboard widgets, the All Campaigns page and the Campaign Edit page to your ongoing Target ROAS campaigns.
- Avoid pausing a Target ROAS campaign for more than five days; if you turn the campaign *live* again, the campaign will have to restart the learning phase.
- Use Target ROAS performance alerts to understand when item-level changes are making your current ROAS goal harder to achieve and consider adjusting your target ROAS to better align with current campaign performance.

## Frequently asked questions

At this time, Target ROAS is only available for recommended existing Sponsored Products automatic campaigns.

### Do I need to change my campaign budget after switching to Target ROAS bidding?

Yes, the minimum budget is $50 per day for Target ROAS campaigns. Make sure your budget is set to at least $50 per day to help ensure your campaign doesn't go out of budget and to comply with the platform's recommendations for optimal results.

### Will my ROAS target be optimized in the learning phase?

No, we won't be optimizing the ROAS target during the learning phase. We will start optimizing the target on day one of the optimization phase.

### How often should I change a recommendation if the Target ROAS campaign is running for a longer phase?

We only provide campaign recommendations when we see campaigns with a potential to perform even better and get incremental sales. While there isn't a specific time period for this, we recommend checking the pages at least once a week to see if the ROAS recommendations have been updated.
