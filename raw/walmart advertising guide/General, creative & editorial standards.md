## Ad copy & image requirements

This section sets forth specific requirements for the ad copy, including ad claims and images included within ad creative.  

### Ad copy, specific claim & IP rights requirements

Overview of Ad Claim and Content Rights Requirements:

- All advertising claims must be accurate, truthful and substantiated.
- Advertiser must have secured all rights, licenses and clearances necessary for the use of any third-party intellectual property or other rights in the ad creative.
- Any and all claims must be submitted/created by the advertiser (and confirmed/reviewed by advertiser legal). Walmart does not create, modify or verify product claims, except in connection with its approval rights below.
- Walmart does not create any taglines, slogans or marks for the advertiser for use in ad creative.
- Certain claims and/or types of content, as indicated below, will be subject to increased scrutiny and require prior approval by Walmart Legal and the Ad Policy team, or prior confirmation from the advertiser regarding its claim substantiation and rights clearances.
- All ads must comply with the terms of the Ad Policy, and Walmart may require the advertiser to submit proof of substantiation and rights clearances at any time in connection with its review and/or approval of any ads submitted to Walmart Connect for distribution.
- Unless claims have been previously reviewed and approved by Walmart (i.e., claims on packaging that has already been approved by Walmart), please note that claims requiring prior approval may be rejected and/or require extended review. Plan accordingly. Send all ad approval requests to your Walmart Connect account team.
- Ad Disclosures - Where necessary, ads must contain "clear and conspicuous" disclosures as required by applicable laws, rules, regulations and industry practices, including without limitation, the FTC Endorsement Guides.
- Advertisers must be accurately and clearly identified in the ad to promote a transparent user experience.

### Ads requiring prior approval

The following are ad categories that either require escalation/prior approval, or otherwise require action/certification from an advertiser:

- Advertisements making the following claims are subject to increased scrutiny and require advance prior approval. The standard SLA for a request of this type is 6 weeks. Please allot additional time in the event additional verification is required:
	- "Made in the USA" claims:
		- This category includes representations that products are built, created, crafted, processed or manufactured in the USA. These include claims that may be implied through use of U.S. symbols like the American flag.
		- "Green" or Sustainability/Environmental Claims:
		- This category includes statements that a product is biodegradable or is manufactured using an environmentally friendly process.
				- Any express or implied claims regarding an environmental attribute of a product, package or service are considered "green" or environmental claims. Examples include:
			- Environmental certifications.
						- Claims about carbon offsets.
						- Non-toxic and "free of" claims.
						- Recyclable claims.
						- Claims that a product is compostable or degradable.
						- General claims that a product is sustainable, green or natural.
				- Any statements or claims stating that a product is made out of bamboo is prohibited.
- Advertisements making the following claims are subject to increased scrutiny and require advertiser's verification, as part of the ad submission process, that it has all necessary substantiation on hand to support the claims in its ads, and has secured all necessary rights from third parties with respect to high-risk IP in its ads:
	- Use of "badges" or certifications on creative assets:
		- Any "badges" or "certifications" on creative assets that make claims relating to the product, or the endorsement or verification of the product. Any certifications used must be obtained and validated by independent and reputable third parties. Advertiser must also have the appropriate permission to use such third party certification badges in connection with their product and advertisement.
		- Performance Claims:
		- Performance claims should be accurate, truthful, substantiated and reflect the typical experience that a consumer can expect. If an ad promotes results that are atypical, the ad must then also clearly disclose the average results that a consumer should expect. Any disclosures shall adhere to the disclosure requirements below.
		- Endorsements and Testimonials:
		- Advertisements that include endorsements and testimonials require advertisers to confirm that all statements and views expressed are truthful and reflective of the endorser's actual experience.
		- Use of Celebrity Talent, Influencers, Music and other High-risk IP:
		- Advertiser must ensure it has secured all rights, licenses and clearances necessary to include any celebrity talent, music or other well-known IP (e.g., movie characters) in its ads.

### General ad creative requirements

- Ads must not contain misspellings, grammatical errors, random or excessive capitalization or special characters.
- No profanity or graphic language, express or implied. Implied profanity using acronyms, chyron, asterisks, emojis or other abbreviations is prohibited.
- No personalized ads or language that implies the user is being targeted.
	- Examples: No statements such as "We noticed you searched for 'hair loss', would you be interested in this growth serum?"
- Ad creative must not compare products to other competitor brands. This includes:
	- Company/brand names.
		- Logos.
- If the products or services being promoted in the ad require a subscription, this must be made explicit in the ad creative.
- When free products or services, special offers or discounts promoted are dependent on a purchase (for example, free shipping with the purchase of a product), you must clearly disclose the purchase requirement and any related material terms and conditions in the ad creative.

### Pricing

- The use of specific pricing and savings claims is permitted in certain scenarios:
	- Rollbacks
		- Onsite Display advertisers may use the Dynamic Rollback tool to apply the Rollback badge. Please see [here](https://advertisinghelp.walmart.com/s/guides?article=000012426&channel=Display&language=en_US) for more information.
				- For information on additional placements, please work with your Walmart Connect Account Representative.
		- Wireless
		- Wireless ads may use exact pricing and savings in ads for pre-paid plans, the sale of phones with activation of an accompanying plan, and wireless providers promoting the sale of phones only, with no accompanying plan (provided the PDP specifies the phone is locked to the carrier). The price/savings claim in the ad must match the price the customer receives on the landing page.
		- If the advertisement does not fall under one of the scenarios listed above, specific pricing and savings claims are not permitted.

### Image requirements

Ads may not depict adult or inappropriate images, including without limitation:

- Nudity, implied nudity or focus on body parts.
	- Ads featuring excessive exposed skin may only do so if relevant to the product (e.g., swimsuits, education, etc.).
		- Walmart Connect has sole discretion over relevancy and reserves the right to determine if the level of nudity/focus on skin is inappropriate.
		- No disembodiment or overemphasis on body parts; models’ full bodies (including faces) must be present in ads.
		- Any model not fully clothed must be clearly 18+.
		- Ads featuring any form of nudity must not make inappropriate references to minors, regardless of the age of the ad’s models.
		- Any product with the attributes "girl," "boy," "teen" or "tween" in the product name may not feature any form of nudity in the creative.
		- Any form of nudity that could possibly violate applicable laws.
- Sexual activity (express or implied).
- Sexual objectification.
- Obscenity.
- Violent/sexually suggestive imagery.
- Healthcare:
	- Ads depicting the usage of any healthcare related products, devices, supplements, etc., must portray the correct usage of the product.
		- Ads may only show supplements in full bottles or in the intended dosage.
		- Ads must only feature those within the intended age range for the product.
- Weight Loss:
	- Ads must contain accurate before and after photographs.
		- Ads may not display photographs that have been altered.
- Menstrual Products:
	- Ads may not display images and videos that contain nudity, implied nudity or sexually suggestive imagery.
		- Ads may display images of adults in undergarments if used within the context of the product being advertised and are not sexualized.
		- Ads may not display images of minors in their undergarments.
- Lingerie, intimates and underwear:
	- For underwear/intimate apparel products only: Images depicting adult men or women in underwear/intimates are allowed under the following conditions: breasts, pubic and buttock areas must be fully covered and visuals cannot be sexualized.
		- Sexualized photos can include photos that have been cropped, edited or presented in a such a way that places emphasis on intimate areas.
- Weapon Imagery:
	- Ads may not depict images of people holding deadly weapons. This includes guns (or realistic looking toys), paintball guns, air guns, knives, brass knuckles, etc.
		- Images of people holding craft knives, kitchen knives or knives that are one part of a multi-tool are allowed.
		- For more information on weapon imagery requirements in ads for movies, video games, or TV shows, please review the [Entertainment policy.](https://advertisinghelp.walmart.com/s/guides?language=en_US&channel=Sponsored%20Products&article=000010875#movies_tv_videogames)
- Alcohol & tobacco:
	- Ads may not depict tobacco-related products or any products that simulate tobacco smoking (e.g., e-cigarettes and vaporizers):
		- (Onsite) Ads may not depict alcohol branding or any depiction of alcohol consumption (e.g., a person holding an alcoholic beverage).
		- (Offsite) Ads may contain alcohol related imagery provided there is no Walmart logo on the ad.
		- Ads may not include images of or target minors. Ads should not include any pictures of minors, or adults who appear to be minors, or use entertainers or personalities who appeal primarily to minors (example: Santa Claus).
		- Ads may not contain illicit drug-related imagery.
		- Ads may not contain images of hemp/CBD/marijuana except for those that appear on the product packaging of topical lotions and creams.
- Controversial content:
	- Ads may not contain content revolving around hot-topic social issues (e.g., coronavirus-related).
		- Ads may not contain content that is overly sensational or that leverages fear or tragedy.
		- Ads may not feature political commentary or content.
- Tattoos:
	- Images of people with tattoos are allowed. Tattoos that are visible in an advertisement should be appropriate for general audiences (e.g., profane or offensive tattoos should be adequately covered) and in line with other image requirements (e.g., no images of smoking).

### Video requirements

Advertisers must adhere to the following guidelines, which apply to all video-based ads, including CTV:

- All content must be rated G for all audiences. The following visual and audio effects are prohibited:
	- Bleeps, including bleeping of profanity.
		- Emergency Alert System (EAS) tones, or similar sound effects that can be confused with EAS
		- Flashing visuals that have potential to affect photosensitive viewers
		- Pixelated or blurred visuals intended to cover nudity or profanity
		- Glitches, color bars or other visual effects that may cause a viewer to believe something is wrong with their TV
		- Extended pauses or drops in video/audio
- Inclusion of Walmart competitors is prohibited.

## Coupons

- Ads Promoting Coupons:
	- Walmart’s official policy is that we do not promote coupons.
		- Walmart does accept manufacturer coupons at checkout and have historically allowed WMC advertisements to link out coupons under the following conditions:
		- Display ad campaigns must never directly link to a coupon offer or page. They must also never refer to any coupon offers in the ad content.
				- The coupon must be manufacturer coupon, not Walmart specific and cannot be co-branded.
				- Preferred CTAs for coupon is "Save $X" or "Save XX¢."
				- Coupons through the Quotient-powered gallery page for digital coupons is supported.
				- Brand pages and non-Walmart media destination pages are permitted to highlight digital or printed merchant coupons, provided no PII data is collected and the advertiser is not directing the consumer to submit a Walmart receipt.
				- Display ad campaigns requiring sign-up or the submission of any customer data to receive the coupon are not permitted.
				- Display ad campaigns that wish to acquire Walmart customer information in order to access the coupon are not permitted.

## Sweepstakes, giveaways, contests & other promotions

- All sweepstakes, giveaways, contests, charitable promotions and other promotional advertising must be pre-approved by Walmart Legal and the Ad Policy team.
- The following requirements apply to all ad placements that make reference to or promote any sweepstakes or contests:
	- The following must be submitted to Walmart for advance approval (including with respect to the review of the official rules):
		- Copy of the official rules (long & short form).
				- Any purchase requirement.
				- Mockups of any materials or ad creative that reference the promotion.
				- Promotional plan (if applicable).
- For any sweepstakes or contests that are (i) exclusively marketed to Walmart customers (i.e., only promoted on Walmart channels) or (ii) have eligibility restricted to solely Walmart customers, then the official rules must name Walmart as a released party and include a statement that Walmart is in no way sponsoring or involved with the administration or fulfillment of the promotion.
- A Promotions Prize Provider/Indemnification Agreement may be required for promotions in certain circumstances (as directed by Walmart upon review of all materials).
- If Walmart Gift Cards are used as a prize it must be clear that Walmart is in no way sponsoring or involved with the administration or fulfillment of the promotion.
	- No imagery of the Walmart gift cards or Walmart logos are allowed.
- Prizes that consist of Visa Gift Cards or other non-retail store Gift Cards are permitted.
- The following disclosure is required on a brand page to the extent that such page promotes a sweepstakes or contest:
	- "Walmart is not a sponsor and is not responsible for the execution, fulfillment or administration of this promotion."
- All sweepstakes/contests must include abbreviated rules on any assets/content that refers to the sweepstakes/contests, and must link out to the official rules, which must include all necessary and applicable information (i.e., eligibility, no purchase necessary, prize information, sponsor name, etc.).
- Co-commercial ventures (i.e., promotions that state that a consumer action/purchase will result in the advertiser donating to a charity), require the advance approval of Walmart Legal and may be subject to additional requirements.

## Sweepstakes redirects

Advertisers often use offsite redirects (i.e. where clicking an ad takes you offsite) for sweepstakes and rewards with receipt uploads, as a key part of their advertising strategy. While these programs are popular, we must ensure that we have measures in place to safeguard Walmart Connect/Walmart's data and interests.  
  
**Required**  
Sweepstake and rewards/rebates campaigns redirecting off/away from Walmart.com are permitted subject to the, Walmart Connect Ad Policy, sweepstakes policy, and these requirements:

- Campaigns must receive prior approval. Contact your account team to get started.
- Redirects are only permitted for Offsite (Walmart DSP) and Onsite so long as they are nested on a brand page (e.g., no homepage placement).
- To the extent such sweepstakes or rewards/rebates programs include a receipt upload component, these campaigns can only run with approved third-party vendors where Walmart/Walmart Connect has a Data Restrictions Agreement in place. Contact your account team to learn more.
- These programs can only be promoted via display advertising, such as onsite display ads and brand pages. Brand pages may only be accessible by clicking on a display ad.
- Additional restrictions may apply.

  
**Prohibited**

- Rewards/Rebates are not permitted for single items.
- Walmart gift cards cannot be the only reward available.
- Walmart cannot administer, fulfill or sponsor the program (e.g. including by providing any portion of the prize/prizes).

## Multicultural ads

- Ads directed at English-speaking audiences may have words or phrases in non-English languages, provided they are well-known words/phrases, and are used appropriately and inclusively. Profane words or phrases are prohibited.
- Advertiser names, supplier names and product packaging are exempt from this policy.
- All non-English ads must be targeted such that the ad is directed toward an audience with a greater chance of understanding the language (e.g., targeted based on language settings of their browser or device).

## Landing page requirements

**For Ads Served Onsite:**

- All ads must remain onsite, except for the following instances:
	- Ads for sweepstakes, giveaways, contests, and other promotions are permitted to link offsite from a Brand Page.
		- Non-endemic ads are permitted to click offsite from a Brand Page.
		- Ads on post-purchase pages are permitted to link directly offsite.

**For Ads Served Offsite**:

- Ads served through the Walmart DSP are eligible to link directly to a non-Walmart.com landing page. Ads posted on Walmart social handles must link to Walmart.com landing pages.

**Walmart app**

Only post-purchase page ads may link off of the app. All other ads served in the Walmart app are **NOT** allowed to link off of the app, even to Walmart.com. Ads may use bronze pages that are built in-app.

**General Requirements**

The following guidance applies to all external landing pages:

- Approval for external landing pages must be obtained from the Walmart Connect Ad Policy team. Approval will be granted at Walmart’s sole discretion.
- Non-Walmart landing pages must follow these requirements:
	- Pages must not directly link to any competitive site, clickable or non-clickable
		- Landing pages consisting primarily of ads are not allowed
		- Third party ads on landing pages must be clearly disclosed as ads and reveal the promoter of the ad
		- Landing page must not include or link to a store locator, unless the store locator includes Walmart as one of the retailer options (if applicable).
		- Landing pages must not include elements that will create a negative user experience. This includes but is not limited to:
		- Ad copy that does not accurately and truthfully reflect the user experience.
			- Pages should not require users to perform an action to access the page unless this feature is clearly disclosed in the ad (i.e., a sign-up page that requires users to make an account or an empty page that requires users to provide an email to view the content)
						- Forcing users to go to another page (i.e., a page with no content that prompts the user to click to a different destination to view the relevant content) is not allowed
				- Pop-ups or automatic downloads
			- Certain pop-ups may be acceptable if they are relevant to the ad experience and aid the user (i.e., age gates, disclaimers, personalization factors or store locator related to the product)
				- Disabled back buttons
				- If a URL is featured in the ad, it must match the URL of the landing page
				- Page under construction, with domain issues, etc.
				- Language that does not match the ad language (not applicable to onsite campaigns)
				- General low quality (i.e., poor formatting, dead links, low quality images, nonexistent functionality, typos/grammar errors, etc.)
				- Excessively disruptive features (i.e., intense flashing images/lights, unexpected loud noises, etc.)
				- Automatically-initiated chat boxes/chat bots that dominate the user experience
				- Content irrelevant to the ad (i.e., an ad for cereal links to a landing page featuring images of a chip product)
		- Pages cannot be non-viewable in commonly used browsers (i.e., the landing page cannot be opened in browsers such as Safari, Chrome, Edge, etc.)
		- Landing pages must be mobile-friendly (not applicable to desktop-only campaigns)
- Landing pages that take a user directly to a brand’s social media page must adhere to additional rules:
	- The linked social media page must belong to the advertiser
		- Required account sign-ins/ups will be approved on a case by case basis
		- Any ads driving to other social media platforms must comply with all Walmart Connect and the respective social media platform ad policies
- Third party shoppable links are not allowed
- All advertiser landing pages that collect personal information (as such term is defined under applicable privacy laws, including, without limitation, e.g., e-mail addresses, names) must contain a privacy policy which complies with applicable laws, rules, regulations and industry standards. This also applies to other pages of an advertiser’s website that collect personal information.

**Vanity/Dedicated URLs**

- Vanity URLs are allowed with restrictions. Please contact your account team to get started. The following rules apply:
	- Branded URLs
		- Must be in all lowercase - per industry standard.
				- Only brand owners may apply their brand to the vanity URL
		- Non-Branded URLs
		- Vanity/Dedicated URLs that do not include the brand name (e.g., **walmart.com/diapersavings**) must be approved by Walmart prior to launch. The setup of all Vanity/Dedicated URLs are subject to availability and naming cannot be used for a brand page if already in use by another team.
- The process to implement a vanity URL redirect is as follows:
	- Source URL/Vanity URL i.e., www.walmart.com/BrandName.
		- Destination URL i.e., **BrandName**.
		- Vanity source and destination URLs do not need to be exactly the same, but will be set up the same unless the BPX team is otherwise instructed.

## Walmart logo

Use of Walmart IP/Logos:

- Creative which will be used outside of a Walmart-owned and operated channel (e.g., DSP offsite, social platform ads) may feature the Walmart logo if it is directing the customer back to Walmart.com.
- Any use of Walmart IP and logos must comply with all brand usage guidelines made available by Walmart, including but not limited to:
	- [VTA Guidelines](https://gecrm.my.salesforce.com/sfc/p/61000000ZKTc/a/KW000001KK8h/Bi7u8_GiyVn7pbXPqszA4iFlbbEQ_gsWWfT.gpBLBD0)
		- [Brand Center Guidelines](https://brandcenter.walmart.com/)
		- [LBC Resource Kit](https://advertisinghelp.walmart.com/s/guides?language=en_US&channel=Display&article=000012348)

The following ads may not utilize the Walmart logo:

- Ads featuring supplier-sponsored promotions (e.g., sweepstakes, contests, giveaways)
- Ads for branded prescription products and vaccines
- Non-endemic ads
- Ads for sexual wellness products

## Walmart Subscribe

The following guidelines apply to all ads featuring Walmart Subscribe messaging. All ads featuring Walmart Subscribe remain subject to all other applicable ad policies, including, but not limited to, the [Prohibited Content policy](https://advertisinghelp.walmart.com/s/guides?channel=Sponsored%20Products&article=000010872).  

### Landing pages

- All ads featuring Walmart Subscribe must link out to a browse shelf page or search shelf page with the subscribe facet activated.

### Copy

- All ads featuring Walmart Subscribe may only include one of the following pre-approved ad copy combinations verbatim. Depending on ad unit size, the full combination need not be used. No other ad copy combinations (mixing and matching) are permitted.
	- Option 1:
		- Headline: "Subscribe Today"
				- Subhead: "Plan ahead & save time."
				- CTA: "Learn more"
		- Option 2:
		- Headline: "Simplify. Subscribe."
				- Subhead: "Start subscribing today."
				- CTA: "Learn more"
		- Option 3:
		- Headline: "Subscribe & save time"
				- Subhead: "Make your planning easier."
				- CTA: "Learn more"
		- Option 4:
		- Headline: "Plan ahead & save time"
				- Subhead: "Stay stocked. Subscribe today."
				- CTA: "Subscribe"
		- Option 5:
		- Headline: "Simplify your planning"
				- Subhead: "Subscribe & save time later."
				- CTA: "Subscribe"
