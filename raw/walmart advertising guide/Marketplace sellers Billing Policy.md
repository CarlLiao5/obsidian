## Overview

This Marketplace sellers Billing Policy ("Billing Policy") applies to self-serve Sponsored Search and Onsite Display advertising purchased by a Marketplace seller through the Walmart Connect Ad Center platform, either directly through an agency or API partner. For the avoidance of doubt, this Billing Policy does not apply to Managed Serve advertising. All use of the Walmart Connect Ad Center is governed by the [Walmart Connect Ad Center Platform Terms of Use](https://advertisinghelp.walmart.com/s/guides?channel=Sponsored%20Products&article=000010104) ("TOU") and capitalized terms not otherwise defined below shall have the respective meanings set forth in the TOU. You acknowledge and agree that Walmart shall, in its sole discretion, have the right to update this Billing Policy from time to time to incorporate new terms by posting such modifications here, which such modifications shall have immediate effect.

## Billing Manager payment method setup

Only Walmart Connect Ad Center users who have access to Billing Manager can view, add and edit payment details. It will include super admin, organization admin, account admin and organization and account standard users with Billing Manager access.  

### Payment methods

The following payment methods are available for Marketplace sellers:

- Credit card
- Debit card
- Seller Balance

### How to set up a payment method

- When you first sign in to Walmart Connect Ad Center, click the link in the banner to access **Billing Manager**.
- Once in Billing Manager, open the *Payment Settings* tab to see or edit your payment methods.
- By default, your **Seller Balance** will be listed as your only and primary, payment method.

**Note:** Seller Balance is the same as your net sales.

- On this screen, you can add new payment methods and identify the priority order in which the payment methods will be applied.

### How to add a credit card or debit card as a new payment method

- In Billing Manager, click the *Payment Settings* tab.
- Click **Add a payment method**.
- Enter relevant **credit card** or **debit card** details.
- Select **card preference** (i.e., whether you want this payment method to be your primary, backup or other payment option).
- Click **Save card** to finish.

## Invoicing

- Invoice amounts are based on the ad spend in the applicable billing period, including any ad spend prior to invoice generation, but does not include ad spend incurred on the day the invoice is generated.
- Marketplace sellers using the same advertising account to run ads across different product solutions (e.g., Sponsored Search or Onsite Display) will see a consolidated invoice with line item break downs for each channel product.
- Invoices are automatically issued and available in Billing Manager at the end of the applicable billing period, unless one of the following instances occurs before the end of the period, in which case an invoice will be issued:
	- The day after you reach or exceed your Advertiser Invoice Threshold.
		- Your **Advertiser Invoice Threshold** is listed in the Walmart Connect Ad Center Billing Manager and may change from time to time based on your spending patterns in previous billing cycles.
				- **Note:** The maximum Advertiser Invoice Threshold amount is $20,000. This maximum does not impact your Organization Spend Limit.
		- The day after you reach or exceed 70% of your Organization Spend Limit.
		- Your **Organization Spend Limit** is the total amount of advertising spend that can be incurred before payment is due and ads are paused.
				- The Organization Spend Limit is set at an organization level, and it applies to all underlying advertiser accounts.
				- Once the Organization Spend Limit is reached, ads are paused and stop serving for the entire organization, including from all advertiser accounts and campaigns across Sponsored Search and Display solutions under that organization, until payment is made.
			- **Note:** This may result in a scenario in which an advertiser’s campaigns within a Walmart Connect Ad Center organization are paused without a pending invoice, because other advertisers within the same organization have pending invoices that in total are above the Organizational Spend Limit. As the billing entity, the organization is responsible for the payment of all invoices across all advertisers within its Walmart Connect Ad Center account.
		- The Organization Spend Limit is calculated based on the Marketplace seller’s performance and other Marketplace seller attributes and is automatically assigned and regularly reassessed.
		- The Organization Spend Limit is visible to all account users directly on the Walmart Connect Ad Center homepage and in Billing Manager on Walmart Connect Ad Center to users with access to it.
		- Walmart Connect reserves the right to change the Organization Spend Limit at any time with or without prior notice.

For example: Your Organization Spend Limit is $1,000 and your Advertiser Invoice Threshold is $200.

- If you spend $300 on advertising, an invoice will be issued at the end of that particular day because you exceeded your Advertiser Invoice Threshold. The invoice amount will be for the full $300 spent.
- If you spend $50 on advertising every day, on day 4 of your billing cycle, when your total amount reaches $200, an invoice will be issued because you exceeded your Advertiser Invoice Threshold.
- If you spend $10 on advertising every day during a billing cycle, an invoice will be issued at the end of the regular billing cycle for the total of $140. Note that because this spend did not reach the Advertiser Invoice Threshold, no invoices were issued mid-billing cycle.

## Payment

Payment is managed at the advertiser account level with the corresponding payment method designated in Billing Manager.

### Credit card and debit card processes

If a credit card or debit card is selected as your primary payment method for your advertiser account, then the following processes apply:

- **Automatic:** Your credit card or debit card will be automatically charged the day in which an invoice is issued, which, as a reminder, can be:
	- The day after your advertiser account meets or exceeds the Advertiser Invoice Threshold
		- The day your organization reaches or exceeds 70% of the Organization Spend Limit
		- At the end of the regular billing cycle
		- In the event your primary payment method is a debit or credit card and it fails, then your backup payment method (which will automatically be your Seller Balance) will be charged at the end of your Marketplace billing cycle.
- **Manual:** On the Billing Manager home screen, a **Pay Now** button is available to immediately pay with a credit card or debit card for any invoices that failed automatic payment or those in "Pending" status.

**Note:** A credit or debit card cannot be used for invoices totaling $50,000 or more. For invoices over $50,000, Seller Balance is the only method for payment.

### Seller Balance processes

If you have Seller Balance as your primary payment method, then the following processes apply:

- All invoiced amounts will be deducted at the end of your Marketplace billing cycle, unless the organization reaches or exceeds 70% of the Organization Spend Limit.
	- In the event the organization reaches or exceeds 70% of the Organization Spend Limit, the advertiser accounts that have a credit or debit card as a backup payment method will be automatically charged. Note, the accounts will be charged in order of the highest unpaid dues and the process will continue until the organization’s outstanding balance is below 50% of the Organization Spend Limit.
- If at the end of the regular Walmart Connect billing cycle, your Seller Balance is not sufficient to pay the unpaid balance, then the backup payment method(s) of each advertiser account, if available and as applicable, will be automatically charged for its respective outstanding balance(s).
- Once a successful payment is made, it will be reflected in the Billing Manager, and your advertiser account outstanding balance will be adjusted accordingly.

## Ad serving

- If the Organization Spend Limit is reached, ad serving for the entire organization (i.e., all underlying advertiser accounts and campaigns) will automatically pause.
- Ads will resume serving once your organization’s outstanding balance is lower than the Organization Spend Limit.
	- **Note:** Even if your ads are paused, you may still be charged for any clicks received on ads that have already been served.

## Frequently asked questions

### Can my Organization Spend Limit be increased?(click to expand)

Increases are granted on a case-by-case basis and at Walmart Connect’s sole discretion. To request an increase to your Organization Spend Limit, reach out to [Walmart Connect](https://advertisinghelp.walmart.com/s/contact?language=en_US).

### I am not able to access Billing Manager, how do I request access? (click to expand)

Only Walmart Connect Ad Center users with super admin, org admin, account admin and org and account standard with Billing Manager access can access Billing Manager. If you are a Walmart Connect Ad Center user with Billing Manager and are receiving an access denied error message when you try to access the platform, please ensure you have the correct account selected in the upper left dropdown menu. If you are still not able to access Billing Manager, please reach out to the [Walmart Connect team](https://advertisinghelp.walmart.com/s/contact).

### How can I review my invoices? (click to expand)

Walmart Connect Ad Center users with Billing Manager access can download issued invoices for the advertiser accounts for which they have access to.

### Can I add the same credit card or debit card to multiple different advertising accounts? (click to expand)

Yes, the same credit card or debit card can be used for multiple different advertising accounts.

### Will I receive a notification when I am close to reaching my Organization Spend Limit? (click to expand)

Yes, a widget in Walmart Connect Ad Center notifies advertisers when they log in to the system and an email is sent to users with Billing Manager access when the organization is about to reach the Organization Spend Limit and has invoices due to be paid.

### Will I receive a notification when I exceed my Organization Spend Limit? (click to expand)

Yes, a widget in Walmart Connect Ad Center notifies advertisers when they log in to the system and an email is sent to users with Billing Manager access when the organization account reaches the Organization Spend Limit and there are invoices due to be paid.

### Why has my Organization Spend Limit decreased or increased from prior periods? (click to expand)

There could be multiple reasons for the decrease or increase in your Organization Spend Limit, for example:

- If your GMV has decreased or increased recently
- If you do or do not pay the total balance of unpaid invoices

### How can I leverage the Pay Now option to pay my pending invoices? (click to expand)

On the Billing Manager home screen:

- If the payment method for a particular invoice is Seller Balance, then use the payment method dropdown to select a card. The *pay now* feature will be activated. Then, you can select **pay now** and follow the prompts to pay the invoice.
- If the payment method for a particular invoice is credit card or debit card, the pay now feature will already be enabled. You can either use the auto populated card or use the **payment method** dropdown menu to select a different card. Then select **pay now** and follow the prompts to pay the invoice.

**Note:** It is not possible to make an immediate payment using Seller Balance.

### How can I leverage the Pay Now option to pay my failed or refunded invoices? (click to expand)

On the Billing Manager home screen:

- If the payment method for a particular invoice is Seller Balance, then use the payment method dropdown menu to select a card. The **pay now** feature will then be activated. Then, you can select **pay now** and follow the prompts to pay the invoice.
- If the payment method for a particular invoice is credit card or debit card, then you must select a different card to enable **pay now**. Once enabled, select **pay now** and follow the prompts to pay the invoice.

**Note:** It is not possible to make an immediate payment using Seller Balance.

### What happens if the payment made through my credit card or debit card fails? (click to expand)

If the payment made via your credit card or debit card fails, we will attempt to charge the same card every 24 hours. You have the option to visit Billing Manager and use the *pay now* feature to manually pay with an alternative payment method or you can add a new card as an account payment method. The new card will be automatically charged at the end of the day. We will also send an email notification for any unsuccessful payment.

### How can I access my invoices in Billing Manager? (click to expand)

To access your invoices in Billing Manager, Walmart Connect Ad Center users with access to Billing Manager should follow these steps:

- Log in to **Billing Manager** and go to the *P* *ayment Dashboard* tab.
- Search for an invoice using the available filter options: Billing period from, billing period to, ad credit, payment status, payment method, invoice ID.
- From the returned list of invoices, click **see details** for the invoice you are interested in reviewing.
- Click **download PDF** to export the invoice.
	- You can also view transaction history to see the log of failed, pending and successful transactions for an applicable invoice by clicking **transaction history**.

### How can I access my receipts in Billing Manager? (click to expand)

Once payment has been made, Walmart Connect Ad Center users with Billing Manager access should follow these steps to access your payment receipts in Billing Manager:

- Log into the **Billing Manager** and go to the *P* *ayment Dashboard* tab.
- Search for paid invoices using the available filter options.
- Click **see details** and then **download PDF** to export the receipt.

### If I’ve exceeded my Organization Spend Limit, when will my ads start serving again? (click to expand)

In order to promptly resume serving your ads, you must make a payment with a credit card or debit card using the pay now feature to bring your outstanding balance below the Organization Spend Limit. Once the payment is processed, ads will resume serving — generally within one to two hours. Otherwise, it may resume if your outstanding balance falls below the assigned Organization Spend Limit after automatic deduction from your Seller Balance at the end of the Billing cycle.

### What if my organization runs Walmart Connect advertising as both a Marketplace seller and Walmart supplier? How will my billing be impacted? (click to expand)

This Billing Policy applies only to advertising purchased by a Marketplace seller through the self-serve Walmart Connect Ad Center platform. If you also have advertising accounts where you operate as a Walmart supplier, your billing will not be impacted by this Billing Policy.

### As a Walmart supplier, can I access Billing Manager and pay for my invoices using a credit or debit card? (click to expand)

No, at this time only Marketplace sellers advertising through the self-serve Walmart Connect Ad Center platform can access Billing Manager and pay for their invoices using a credit or debit card.

### Where can I find more information on the different levels of account access? (click to expand)

Learn more about [levels of account access here.](https://advertisinghelp.walmart.com/s/guides?article=000009334&language=en_US)
