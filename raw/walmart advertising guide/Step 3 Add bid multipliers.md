This article explains what bid multipliers are and how to apply them.  
  
If you're creating Automatic or Manual campaigns, you will have the option to set bid multipliers.

## Bid multipliers

Bid multipliers give you additional opportunities to optimize your advertising campaigns. This bidding feature helps you manage your Sponsored Products campaign by enabling you to increase your bids on prime placements and platforms, while keeping your base bid low.  
  
**Note:** Bid multipliers are optional, but highly recommended.

## Setting and using bid multipliers

There are two types of bid multipliers, Placement and Platform.

### Placement multiplier

This control allows you to set bid modifiers starting at 0% for various placements, depending on your campaign type. When your ads are eligible to serve in these placements, the bid percentages you set will help you better compete for these placement opportunities.  
  
Example:

- **Starting bid:** $0.75
- **Search In-grid Multiplier:** 200%
- **Resulting bid for Search In-grid ad position**: $0.75 \* (1+200%) = $2.25

### Platform multiplier

This control allows you to set bid multipliers starting at 0% for desktop, app and mWeb (mobile web) platforms. When your ads serve on these platforms, the bid percentages you set will help you better compete for these platform opportunities.  
  
Example:

- **Starting bid**: $0.20
- **mWeb Multiplier**: 90%
- **Resulting searches on Mobile**: $0.20 \* (1+90%) = $0.38

  
If you use both Placement and Platform multipliers, your bid multipliers will be added together.  
  
Example:

- **Starting bid:** $1.00
- **Buy Box Multiplier:** 50%
- **Desktop Multiplier:** 150%
- **Resulting bid for Buy Box position on Desktop**: \[$1.00 \* \[1+(50% + 150%)\] = $3.00

  
If bid multiplier = 0% or blank, your bid will remain the same as the bid entered at item or keyword level.  
  
**Note:** There is no maximum bid multiplier amount, so please make sure you understand and are comfortable spending the multiplier amount you are setting.
