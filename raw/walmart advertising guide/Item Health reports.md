This article provides an overview of the item health reports including definitions, how listing quality works, how to view listing quality score and modify an item listing.  
  
The Item Health report enables advertisers to see the health of their sponsored items and take corrective actions as needed, to improve item performance and reach their desired campaign goals.  
  
To view your report, navigate to the *Reports* tab and select **Item Health**.  

![](https://advertisinghelp.walmart.com/servlet/rtaImage?eid=ka0KW000000oP3o&feoid=00N6100000Hxa5T&refid=0EMKW000009HXSa)

  
This report provides a streamlined way of viewing various aspects of your advertised Sponsored Search items within your Automatic and Manual campaigns. It includes signals such as item availability, Buy Box win rate, item page view, whether an item is a primary variant, etc.  
  
This information can help you to run high-performing campaigns by helping you select healthy items from your catalog to advertise.

  
![image.png](https://advertisinghelp.walmart.com/servlet/rtaImage?eid=ka0KW000000oP3o&feoid=00N6100000Hxa5T&refid=0EM8Y000009B0qF)

  
**Note:** This report only shows enabled items that have been live for at least three days.

  
Item Health report columns and definitions:

- **Primary variant**: Whether an item is a primary variant. The primary variant was previously known as the "base item."  All variations within the variant group will be mapped to the primary variant.  It is important to note that with the launch of variant bidding, all variants, including the primary variant, can be promoted and displayed within Sponsored Search campaigns.
- **Item status**: Whether an item is published or unpublished.
- **Product detail page views**: Number of times the product detail page for this item was visited today or in last 7 days.
- **Item availability**:Percentage of time the item was in stock today or in the last 7 days.
- **Win rate**: Percentage of time the item won the Buy Box today or in the last 7 days.
- **Listing quality** (for Marketplace sellers only): A score for advertisers that provides insight into how their items are performing based on variables that affect a customer’s purchase decisions.

## How listing quality works

The listing quality score helps advertisers discover items that will perform well in their Sponsored Search campaigns and helps them prioritize those items since they’ll have a higher chance of conversion.  
  
A listing quality score is based on the variables that contribute to purchase and is determined by the following first-party data:

- **Content & discoverability** – Content is the first thing a customer sees, so it must be accurate. The accuracy of various attributes (item name, description, key features, images) influences how discoverable an item is on Walmart’s site and app. For additional information on these attributes, please review the [Content quality guide and best practices](https://marketplace.walmart.com/wp-content/uploads/2020/09/WMP_listing_quality_optimization_guide.pdf).
- **Offer** – Based on your item’s price, shipping speed and whether it is available to purchase.
	- **Price** – How competitive your item’s overall price is.
		- **Shipping speed** – How fast the seller can deliver the item. Strive to offer expedited shipping on most items.
		- **Published and in stock** – How often your item is published and in stock for customers to purchase. This metric is based on the last seven days.
- **Ratings & reviews** – Measures an item based on the number of customer ratings and reviews it has.

### View listing quality score and modify an item listing

Hover your cursor over the percentage in the *Listing Quality* column to see the score breakdown.  

  
Strive to keep your listing quality scores above 60%. I tems with scores at 60% or below are less likely to convert.  
  
If your listing quality score is below 60% and you want to investigate underlying data for the corresponding Item ID, select **strengthen your Listing Quality score** to view that specific item’s listing quality page on Seller Center. You can make optimizations to your item from the Seller Center *Listing Quality & Rewards* dashboard.   
  
**Note:** If you have multiple email accounts for Seller Center, you may not land on the correct listing quality page after clicking the link. Use the same Walmart Connect Ad Center login credentials on Seller Center to view the correct listing quality page.  
  
Changes will take about 24 hours to reflect on the Item Health report. 

### Best practices

**Note:** You must have access to the Seller Center in order to view an item’s Listing Quality page. If you do not have access to Seller Center, please make a request.  

The listing quality data reported on the Item Health report may vary slightly from what is being reported on Seller Center, due to the difference in data refresh rates. The Seller Center data updates in real-time, whereas the Item Health report data updates once a day.  
  
[Click here](https://sellerhelp.walmart.com/s/guide?article=000009387) for general tips on how to improve your listing quality score.
