## Brand term targeting

Brand term targeting helps increase your brand’s visibility by empowering you to bid on your competitors' brand.  

![](https://advertisinghelp.walmart.com/servlet/rtaImage?eid=ka0KW000000oP0Q&feoid=00N6100000Hxa5T&refid=0EMKW0000090zfG)

  
This feature is available for automatic campaigns and exact-match keywords for manual campaigns via Sponsored Products(Search In-grid only), Sponsored Brands and Sponsored Videos.  

### Brand term targeting for brand offense (i.e., bidding on a competitor’s brand)

You can enable this feature in Walmart Connect Ad Center by, following the instructions for your campaign type:

- For *existing automatic campaigns*, select the checkbox and Walmart will decide which branded keywords to target for your campaigns.
- For *new automatic campaigns*, brand term targeting is automatically enabled.
- For *manual exact-match keyword campaigns*, bid on competitive branded keywords that you choose. The checkbox is not required.

To disable, follow the instructions for your campaign type:

- For *automatic campaigns*, deselect the checkbox for any automatic campaigns.
- For *exact-match keyword campaigns*, remove branded keywords for targeting.

### Brand term targeting for brand defense (i.e., bidding on your own brand)

To enable, follow the instructions for your campaign type:

- Brand term targeting for brand defense is automatically enabled for *automatic* *campaigns*. The checkbox is not required.
- For *manual exact-match keyword campaigns*, bid on your own branded keywords.

To disable, follow the instructions for your campaign type:

- Pause your *automatic campaign* as this is automatically enabled on this campaign type.
- For *manual exact-match keyword campaigns*, remove your own branded keywords from targeting.

## Exclusions to brand term targeting

Walmart private labels are currently not eligible to participate in brand term targeting. Walmart private labels are restricted from bidding on other advertiser's brand terms and advertisers are restricted from bidding on Walmart private label brand terms.

  
You can download the brand term targeting best practices [here](https://gecrm.my.salesforce.com/sfc/p/#61000000ZKTc/a/KW000001FxvQ/Z4jsIOrbIDc4AT8rKKdMxMYiQv6UF9nvRNlWBRghZew).

## Complementary targeting

Complementary targeting allows Walmart Connect to serve ads to customers as they search for products related to yours on the Walmart site and app. This strategy can help increase product visibility, drive consideration among high-intent customers and expand reach via automatic campaigns.

For campaigns that have complementary targeting enabled, relevant items will be displayed in a carousel on the related item page.   

**Example:** If a customer searches for coffee filters, different types of coffee products, such as ground coffee could be shown to the customer on the item page carousel as complementary items.

![](https://advertisinghelp.walmart.com/servlet/rtaImage?eid=ka0KW000000oP0Q&feoid=00N6100000Hxa5T&refid=0EM8Y00000EO0E7)

Complementary targeting is enabled for new campaigns by default. To enable complementary targeting for existing campaigns, open the campaign setup page and select **Complementary targeting** under the Expanded targeting box. To disable, deselect the checkbox.

![](https://advertisinghelp.walmart.com/servlet/rtaImage?eid=ka0KW000000oP0Q&feoid=00N6100000Hxa5T&refid=0EMKW0000090zhg)

## Frequently asked questions

### What are complementary products?

Complementary products are items that pair well with the product being browsed. For example, printer ink is complementary to a printer, just as coffee filters are to ground coffee.

### Can I modify an existing campaign to include complementary products?

Yes, you can edit any existing campaign to include complementary targeting by checking the relevant option.

### Is complementary targeting available via API?

Yes, complementary targeting is available via API.
