Overview of Sponsored Products

  
  
This article provides an overview of Sponsored Products on Walmart's site and app, how it works and how cost is calculated.  

## What are Sponsored Products?

Sponsored Products help customers discover relevant products as they search and browse Walmart.com, mWeb (Mobile Web), and the Walmart App. They are ads that appear on prominent, high-traffic spots such as homepage, browse, search, item and stock up pages. Sponsored Products drive discovery and purchase among Walmart customers searching for your products.

![Sponsored Products](https://advertisinghelp.walmart.com/servlet/rtaImage?eid=ka0KW0000002UeJ&feoid=00N6100000Hxa5T&refid=0EM4M000003Y37H)

**Key benefits:**

- Boost product sales
- Increase share of wallet
- Expand product visibility
- Grow & protect market share
- Maximize profitable SKUs
- Optimize ad strategy through robust reporting

Using Automatic targeting for a Sponsored Products campaign leverages Walmart Connect's AI and machine learning to target and optimize ads, which determines the right audience and placements for your products. Automatic campaigns are designed to supercharge performance, expand reach, and reduce effort—delivering more results with less effort.

## How do Sponsored Products work?

Sponsored Products are cost-per-click (CPC) ads that promote individual product listings on Walmart’s site and app. When customers click on the Sponsored Product ad, they are taken to the advertised product’s detail page.

The promoted products are selected based on the winner of an advanced second price auction for each ad placement. The winner of the auction is based on the product’s relevancy to a customer’s search query on the Walmart site or app and the amount the advertiser is willing to spend on the ads (i.e., their bids and budget.)

  
To have your product promoted via Sponsored Products ads, you must meet the following eligibility criteria:

- Product is in stock.
- Product is winning the Buy Box.
- Product is published.

  
**Note:** All variant types are now eligible for Sponsored Products campaigns if they meet the above requirements.  

### How is a product’s relevancy determined?

Walmart determines a product’s relevancy to a customer’s search query based on various factors, including:

- **Customer intent:** When customers search for specific keywords or phrases on Walmart’s site or app, the algorithm evaluates those terms to detect any implied or explicit intent. It also recommends popular products based on various signals, such as what other customers searched for or purchased after using similar search terms. This approach delivers a more personalized and intuitive shopping experience by highlighting relevant results for Walmart customers.
- **Item page content quality:** When a customer searches on Walmart’s site and app, the algorithm scans product detail pages for keywords within the customer query and ranks the product results based on their relevancy and eligibility for Sponsored Search placements. Content and listing quality scores provide advertisers with insights into which product detail pages need optimization and how well those listings are performing.
- **Item-level customer engagement:** Increased customer engagement such as clicks and purchases for a particular product further boosts a product’s relevance in search results, helping surface the items that resonate most with customers. A product’s longevity in the Walmart ecosystem strengthens its relevancy.

### What other factors determine an auction winner?

Bid price works in conjunction with relevancy.

- **Bid price:** The amount the advertiser is willing to spend when a shopper clicks on the Sponsored Product ad for their advertised product.
- **Relevancy:** The more relevant a product is to the customer’s search query, the less the ad is likely to cost the advertiser, when other factors remain constant. On the other hand, the higher and more competitive the advertiser’s bid is, the more likely the ad is to be displayed while it is relevant to the customer’s search query.

## How much do Sponsored Products cost?

Sponsored Products are sold on a cost-per-click basis in Walmart's advanced second-price auction. This model means the advertiser only pays when a customer clicks on its advertised product.

To determine the price the advertiser will pay, the advertiser sets a maximum bid they are willing to pay per click, but only pay what is needed to win the auction for the ad placement.  

Relevancy + bid price determines the auction winner. The advertiser must also meet daily budget minimums and bid minimums to compete in the auction, as outlined below.

**Budget minimums:**

- For first-party suppliers: the **daily budget** must be **$50 or above**. If you choose to set a **total budget**, it must be **$100 or above**.
- For Marketplace sellers: the **daily budget** must be **$10 or above**. If you choose to set a **total budget**, it must be **$50 or above.**

**Bid minimums:**

- For manual campaigns, bids cannot be lower than **$0.30**.
- For automatic campaigns, bids cannot be lower than **$0.20.**

When a customer searches for a product on Walmart’s site or app, a real-time auction is run to determine which Sponsored Search ads will appear along with the organic product listings, and in what ad placements across the Walmart site and app. As explained above, the auction will consider a product’s relevancy (with respect to the nature of the customer’s search query), the advertiser’s maximum bid price, and competition from other advertisers for the same ad placement, to dynamically establish the final cost-per-click price for each winning ad placement. Certain premium ad placements may be subject to a minimum reserve price, which ensures that the right consideration is given to the value advertisers place on their ads appearing in such placements.

### How reserve prices impact your CPC

Your actual CPC is calculated based on your relevancy and bid, including the competition from other advertisers, and any reserve prices set by Walmart.

Even if your ad is the only one eligible to show (for example, if there are no other competing bids), where there is a reserve price for the applicable ad placement, your CPC may be higher than the minimum required bid for that placement to meet the reserve price. But you will never pay more than your maximum bid for the placement.

In highly competitive categories, or during peak seasonal moments, even the most relevant products may experience elevated pricing due to the nature of competition for premium placements.

## Eligibility requirements

In order to advertise with Sponsored Products, you must be a Walmart Supplier or Marketplace Seller.

- As a Walmart Supplier, you will create and use a Walmart.com account to access the platform.
- As a Marketplace Seller, you will use your Seller Center credentials.

  
For details on how to apply to be a Walmart Supplier, visit [supplier.walmart.com](https://supplier.walmart.com/home).  

For details on how to apply to be a Marketplace Seller, visit [marketplace.walmart.com](https://marketplace.walmart.com/).  
 

**Item requirements:**

- Item is in stock
- Advertiser wins the buy box for that item on the item page
