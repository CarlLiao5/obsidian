This article provides an overview of the Keywords Planner tool, including how to use it, selecting relevant keywords and what to do if no keywords populate.

## About Keywords Planner

The Keyword Planner tool generates a list of keywords for your selected products. It incorporates historical search traffic and terms associated with the selected items. It shows how frequently a specific item appears when customers search using that specific keyword, and how frequently that keyword was used to search on Walmart.com.

## How to use Keywords Planner

Under the *Tools* heading in the left-hand navigation, click **Keywords Planner**.  

![image.png](https://advertisinghelp.walmart.com/servlet/rtaImage?eid=ka0KW0000002UfH&feoid=00N6100000Hxa5T&refid=0EM8Y00000DAXyg)

Select **Add items** in the page that loads.  

  

**Note:** You can also use the CSV template option to bulk upload multiple items.

Enter your item ID in the search box and click the **search icon** (🔍).  

If your item is synced with the Sponsored Search platform, your item title appears in the dialogue box. If you see "no items found for given search" in the results, it means that your item is not synced with our platform. In this instance, [create a support case](https://advertisinghelp.walmart.com/s/contact).  

  
**Note:** If you enter a variant item ID in the search box, the primary variant appears in the results. The Keyword Planner tool is only designed to pull the primary variant of a variant group at this time.  
  
Once your search results appear, select the radio button to the right of the item details and then click **Add x items for keyword suggestions**.  

  
Your keyword list is populated with the most popular keywords related to the selected items, based on historical data. You can export this list in CSV format and use it in your manual campaigns. We recommend bidding on these keywords as broad-match types so your campaigns can capture the widest customer reach.  
  
**Note:** Remember that phrase and exact match type can narrow your target audience. Exact *match* is the most specific, followed by *phrase* and *broad*.  

The table provides data for the following:

- **Normalized query** — A group of similar raw queries (e.g., "motor bicycles").
- **Raw query** — The keywords that customers type into Walmart (e.g., "motorbike,” "motorized bike,” "motor bicycle").
- **Item keyword frequency bucket** — The frequency that the item shows up when customers search for the raw query.
- **Traffic keyword frequency bucket** — The frequency the raw query was searched by customers.

## Selecting relevant keywords

Not all keywords in the results will be relevant to your campaign needs. It is important to review the results and choose the ones that are best.  
  
Here are some reasons why keywords listed may not be relevant to your item(s):

- The item taxonomy may be mapped incorrectly. Check the item taxonomy and map a correct taxonomy for your item.
- The item's Product Type may be mapped incorrectly. In this case, please check the product type of your item and make changes accordingly. Please refer to [Add multiple items: Full setup](https://sellerhelp.walmart.com/seller/s/guide?article=000007680&language=en_US) in Seller Help for more information.
- Even if you have the correct taxonomy and product type, your item may have irrelevant keywords populated if the Global Product Type is mapped incorrectly.

If you have any issues mapping the taxonomy, product type or global product type, create a support case with Partner Support for [Sellers](https://sellerhelp.walmart.com/s/contact?language=en_US) or [Suppliers](https://supplierhelp.walmart.com/s/contact).

## Troubleshooting no keyword suggestions

If there are no suggested keywords populated for your item(s), review the troubleshooting details below for next steps.  
  
Newly published items may take time to start populating with keywords. We recommend running your items in an automatic campaign to increase their visibility. There are several ad slots available for items to serve as Sponsored Search ads. Adding an item to an automatic campaign allows our system to place your items in relevant placements. For a guide on jumpstarting new items, [click here to download](https://sforce.co/3ZvVufY).  
  
The more places on Walmart your item appears, the more visibility is improved, which can lead to more clicks, sales and reviews, which helps increase relevancy. Placing your items in automatic campaigns also trains our system to associate more keywords with your items, which generates more suggested keywords for your item.  
  
If your item has been in the system for longer than 60 days and you still cannot find any suggested keywords populated for your item, [create a support case](https://advertisinghelp.walmart.com/s/contact).
