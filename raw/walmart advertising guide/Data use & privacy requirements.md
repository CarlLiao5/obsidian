## Advertiser-created naming conventions

**There are instances in which advertisers provide a naming convention or can otherwise customize a particular field, title or name** through one of our advertising platforms (i.e., choosing a name for a custom audience for ad targeting). Wherever Walmart Connect provides the ability to define/customize content or naming conventions, advertisers must do so in a way that does not refer to, or infer the presence of, sensitive personal information or interests. In addition, naming conventions must not violate any portion of our Ad Policy, [health and wellness policies](https://advertisinghelp.walmart.com/s/guides?channel=Sponsored%20Products&article=000010875#health_wellness), [targeting guidelines](https://advertisinghelp.walmart.com/s/guides?channel=Sponsored%20Products&article=000010874#targeting_policy), our terms or any applicable laws or regulations.

Examples of content which may be named/customized include:

<table><tbody><tr><td colspan="1" rowspan="1"><ul><li>Custom audiences/data elements (DSP)</li><li>Manual shelf names</li><li>Keywords</li><li>Item sets</li><li>Custom locations</li></ul></td><td colspan="1" rowspan="1"><ul><li>Creatives/creative folders</li><li>Campaign names</li><li>Tags</li><li>Ad groups</li></ul></td></tr></tbody></table>

  
Naming conventions should:

- Be relevant to the product and brand being advertised.
- Be easy to read (i.e., no symbols, emojis, hyperlinks).

  
Naming conventions must not refer to, or otherwise infer the presence of, sensitive personal information or interests, such as, without limitation:

- Children (or individuals under 18).
- Race, ethnicity, national origin, citizenship or immigration status, gender identity, disability, veteran status, sexual orientation, religion, protected class or sexual interests.
- Sensitive physical health, mental health or genetic data including medical diagnoses/conditions, medical treatment, or health status.
- Identifying information particular to any individual, household, or device.
- Anything otherwise personal, sensitive, predatory, discriminatory, offensive, hateful or harassing in nature.

## Targeting policy

Personalized advertising enables advertisers to apply information from users’ past online behavior to provide a more relevant and catered present day experience. This in turn can increase return on investment for advertisers since people are more likely to engage with content to which they can relate.  
  
That said, we understand that it is extremely important to handle user data with the utmost care and consideration. Walmart Connect’s ad targeting policies are designed to protect our users and maintain their trust as they engage with personalized ad experiences across all our product offerings.  
  
In addition to adhering to these policies, advertisers are responsible for ensuring that all ad targeting is legal and complies with all applicable local and regional regulations. Note that these policies apply to first and third party data.  

### Prohibited ad targeting practices

Walmart Connect does not allow the following for segment/item set names, or for items contained within item sets:

- Naming targeting segments/item sets in a manner that violates our [Advertiser Created Naming Conventions](#advertiser-naming-conventions) policies.
- Sharing or inferring private or sensitive personal information, or personally identifiable information.
- Targeting children.
- Targeting users in a geographic location where the product is illegal or restricted.
- Targeting on products, services or content that our [Ad Policies prohibit](https://advertisinghelp.walmart.com/s/guides?article=000010872).
- Creating an item set that results in targeting users based on their past search, browse and/or purchase behavior related to a sensitive interest category. Sensitive Interest Categories against which ad targeting is prohibited are listed below:

## Invalid traffic policy

Walmart strives to maintain the highest standards of traffic quality in order to protect our advertisers from invalid traffic (IVT). Invalid traffic is typically generated by bots or other non-human sources to a webpage, which inflates ad metrics. Walmart strictly prohibits establishing or continuing partnerships with individuals or organizations who deploy IVT with malicious intent. Walmart vets business partners (advertisers and third-party vendors) for involvement in fraudulent IVT before business relationships are established and performs periodic IVT investigations.

### For Display

Walmart employs internal filtration techniques to detect and filter out known invalid traffic. Additionally, we utilize [HUMAN](https://www.humansecurity.com/platform/), which is a third-party cybersecurity company that helps safeguard the digital customer experience and is accredited by the Media Rating Council. We utilize HUMAN to further identify invalid traffic not blocked during upfront filtration.

## Collection of personal information

### 3rd party collection of personal information on external landing pages

All external advertiser landing pages that collect personal information (e.g., e-mail addresses, names) must contain a privacy policy or notice. This also applies to other pages of an advertiser's website that specifically collect personal information. When redirecting from a Walmart site, the look, feel and experience of the landing pages needs to be distinctive from Walmart and clear to the user they have moved out of the Walmart user experience. An interstitial is required any time a user is leaving Walmart.com, not just when personal information is being collected. The interstitial should read, "You are now leaving Walmart.com and being directed to an external page" or something similar.
