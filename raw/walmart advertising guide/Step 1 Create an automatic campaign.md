## Campaign type and targeting tactic

In the *Targeting* section, select **Sponsored Products** under *Select Campaign Type* as and targeting tactic. For targeting, you have the choice between Automatic and Manual.  

- **Automatic** — Leverages Walmart Connect's AI and machine learning to target and optimize ads, which determines the right audience and placements for your products. Automatic campaigns are designed to supercharge performance, expand reach, and reduce effort—delivering more results with less effort.
	- We recommend using automatic targeting when you are looking to:
		- Run an always-on campaign to maximize visibility
				- Launch new products
				- Expand reach beyond your existing customer base
				- Save time with minimal in-flight optimization
		- Automatic campaigns also offer expanded options, like complementary targeting and Target ROAS, that are unavailable within manual campaigns.
- **Manual** — Gives you precise control as it allows you to choose exact products and keywords you want to target, with limited placements available. For more information on setting up a manual campaign, visit [Creating a manual campaign guide.](https://advertisinghelp.walmart.com/s/guides?article=000012414&channel=Sponsored%20Products&language=en_US)

## Scheduling and budget

  
In the *Scheduling & Budget* section, set your campaign duration and budget. Select a **Start date** for your campaign. Selecting the current date (today) will launch your campaign within the hour. Next, enter a **Daily budget.** You cannot launch a campaign without a daily budget set.

  
![](https://advertisinghelp.walmart.com/servlet/rtaImage?eid=ka0KW000000oP0L&feoid=00N6100000Hxa5T&refid=0EMKW00000939zj)

*Daily Budget* represents the most you will spend each day of the campaign. As your account will be charged per ad click, daily spend may vary. It cannot exceed your total budget, if set.

- Walmart suppliers must set a daily budget of $50 or above.
- Marketplace sellers must set a daily budget of $10 or above.

  
We recommend a minimum daily budget of $100 to avoid running out of budget. If your campaign does not spend all of the daily budget, the unused amount will roll over to the next day (and continue to do so if unused). However, daily spend will never exceed 2X the daily budget.  
  
For example:

If you set a daily budget of $100 and only spend $50, the daily budget for the following day, with the addition of rollover budget, will be $150.

OR

If you set a daily budget of $500 and spend $100, the daily budget for the following day will be $900. If your campaign again spends $100, the daily budget for the following day will be $1000 (capped at 2X your set daily budget).

With these settings, your campaign will default to run indefinitely until you choose to pause or end it. To set an end date and/or a total budget, expand the *Additional settings* section.  

### Additional settings

Expand Additional Settings to set an **End Date** and/or a **Total Budget** for your campaign, though we recommend an always-on approach as you can pause or end your campaign at any time without setting an end date or total budget. An end date will stop your campaign even if there is still budget left. A total budget will end the campaign as soon as the total budget has been spent.

![](https://advertisinghelp.walmart.com/servlet/rtaImage?eid=ka0KW000000oP0L&feoid=00N6100000Hxa5T&refid=0EMKW0000093A1p)

*  
Total Budget* represents the most you will spend on the campaign. As your account will be charged per ad click, the final campaign spend may be equal to or less than the total budget but will **never exceed** it. If you choose to set a total budget, you must adhere to the following minimums.

Walmart suppliers must set a total budget of $100 or above.

Marketplace sellers must set a total budget of $50 or above.

**Note**: If you do not set a total budget, before the campaign starts you will not be able to add one once the campaign is live.  

If you set ***both*** an end date and total budget, an **Auto Calculate** option will populate in the *Daily Budget* field. If you select it, the system will automatically divide your total budget by the total number of days your campaign will run, to provide an equally distributed amount. For example, if your campaign is 30 days long and you set a total budget of $6,000, selecting **Auto Calculate** will set your Daily budget to $200.

  
![](https://advertisinghelp.walmart.com/servlet/rtaImage?eid=ka0KW000000oP0L&feoid=00N6100000Hxa5T&refid=0EMKW0000093A3H)

**Note**: Make sure you have enabled Out-of-budget and budget-at-risk notifications from the All Campaigns page to get near-real time budget alerts.

## Expanded targeting

The expanded targeting section provides options that can help extend your campaigns reach. Choose between brand term targeting and/or complementary targeting. You can have both enabled on the same campaign.

**Brand term targeting** helps increase your brand’s visibility by empowering you to bid on keywords associated with your brand and those of your competitors. It is available for **automatic campaigns** and exact-match keywords for manual campaigns via Sponsored Products (Search In-grid only), Sponsored Brands and Sponsored Video.

**Complementary targeting** allows Walmart Connect to serve ads to customers as they search for products related to yours on the Walmart site and app which can help increase product visibility, drive consideration among high-intent customers and expand reach. It is only available for **automatic campaigns.**

To learn about expanded targeting, visit the [Expanded targeting guide](https://advertisinghelp.walmart.com/s/guides?language=en_US&article=000012133).

![](https://advertisinghelp.walmart.com/servlet/rtaImage?eid=ka0KW000000oP0L&feoid=00N6100000Hxa5T&refid=0EMKW0000093A9e)

## Campaign bidding strategy

In the Campaign bidding strategy section, choose the bidding strategy that best suits your business goals.  

**Dynamic bidding** – This is an optimized bidding strategy designed to help Sponsored Products advertisers save time and effort, maximize conversion and optimize ad spend. With dynamic bidding, Walmart Connect’s platform adjusts cost-per-click bids up or down in real time based on the likelihood that a customer will purchase the item if the ad appears in their search. To learn more about how dynamic bidding works, visit the [Dynamic bidding guide](https://advertisinghelp.walmart.com/s/guides?language=en_US&article=000012447).

**Target ROAS** – This bidding strategy helps advertisers effectively achieve their campaign ROAS goals. Using AI, Walmart’s algorithm calculates the optimal bids needed to achieve your ROAS goal, thus enhancing campaign efficiency by saving time and effort and supporting you in making strategic investments to achieve your campaign goals. To learn more about how target ROAS works, visit the  [Target ROAS bidding guide](https://advertisinghelp.walmart.com/s/guides?language=en_US&article=000012446).

**Note:** You cannot create a new campaign using the Target ROAS bidding strategy. You can only apply Target ROAS bidding to an existing campaign that is recommended by the platform.

  
**Fixed bidding** – Allows you to manually control how much you bid. Advertisers enter a base bid that represents the most they are willing to pay.  

![](https://advertisinghelp.walmart.com/servlet/rtaImage?eid=ka0KW000000oP0L&feoid=00N6100000Hxa5T&refid=0EMKW0000093A5I)

  
Once you have entered all the necessary information, proceed to either the *Bid Multiplier* section (optional) or *Placement Inclusion* section (for Manual campaigns).  

Ready to continue your campaign setup? Continue to [Add bid multipliers](https://advertisinghelp.walmart.com/s/guides?language=en_US&article=000008409).

## Editing live campaigns

When editing an in-flight campaign, you may see an *Out-of-budget* or *Budget-at-risk* tag at the top of the screen. This means your campaign may stop serving, while the status remains *Live*. These tags appear only when your campaign’s daily and/or total budget is at risk of running out before the scheduled end date or when the budget has already run out.  
  
For more information, please review [*Out-of-budget features*](https://advertisinghelp.walmart.com/s/guides?channel=Sponsored%20Products&article=000012383).
