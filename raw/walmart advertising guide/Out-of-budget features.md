## Out-of-budget features

Out-of-budget features are available across all Sponsored Search campaigns, including Sponsored Products, Sponsored Brands and Sponsored Videos, using daily and/or total budgets. Out-of-budget features help identify and troubleshoot two key budget-related issues:

1. Your campaign has used up all its daily and/or total budget and is now *out-of-budget*.
	- Your ads are not serving, and we recommend you take action and add budget so your campaigns can resume serving and potential customers can see your ads.
2. Your campaign is operating at a suboptimal budget, which has caused it to use up its daily budget at least once in the past seven days and is now *budget-at-risk*.
	- Your ads are currently serving but are at risk of running out-of-budget. Customers will no longer see your ads once you run out-of-budget. We recommend that you increase your budget to ensure your ads serve all day. You can also view *Avg. Cap-out-Time* to understand the average time when you're running out of campaign budget.
		- Out-of-budget features include alerts, recommendations and key metrics and insights, available throughout the Walmart Connect Ad Center.

## Benefits of out-of-budget and budget-at-risk features

The out-of-budget and budget-at-risk features enable you to:

- Identify missed opportunities and help prevent potential missed sales from campaigns running out-of-budget unintentionally. You can view estimated missed impressions and estimated missed clicks to help make an informed decision and then use the data-driven recommendations of daily and total budget to quickly adjust.
- Make informed decisions regarding ad spend with personalized and actionable recommendations for adjusting budgets and avoiding losing potential customers.

## How to use out-of-budget features

Out-of-budget features are available throughout the Walmart Connect Ad Center for Sponsored Search. You can view out-of-budget features on the Sponsored Search Dashboard, the All Campaigns page, and on the Campaign edit page. You can also sign up for out-of-budget email alerts on the All Campaigns page and view key related metrics throughout the Walmart Connect Ad Center.

### Dashboard and recommendations page widgets

When you have campaigns that have run out of daily and/or total budget, an alert widget will appear on the Dashboard and Recommendations page with information including budget recommendations.

There is one widget for campaigns using total budget, and another widget for campaigns using daily budget. A campaign will appear in both widgets if the daily and total budget have been spent.

You can quickly get your campaigns running again by applying the recommended budgets across all out-of-budget campaigns using the **Apply all** button.

![](https://advertisinghelp.walmart.com/servlet/rtaImage?eid=ka0KW000000oPDQ&feoid=00N6100000Hxa5T&refid=0EMKW00000AE3ao)

If you would like to review the details of the out-of-budget campaigns and their budget recommendations, click **View recommendations** and a pop-up will appear. You can take several actions:

- Apply all budget recommendations in bulk using the **Apply all** button
- Apply budget recommendations individually for each campaign using the **Apply** button
- Update budgets manually for each campaign in the *Current total budget* column

After you have applied the budget recommendations or increased your budget, your campaigns will resume serving in near-real time.

If you do not see a total and/or daily out-of-budget alert widget on the Dashboard, that means you do not have any campaigns that are out-of-budget.

When you open the Campaign edit page for a campaign that has run out-of-budget or is at risk of running out-of-budget, you will see an alert tag at the top of the page.

To view your budget and make changes, click **View budget** at the top of the page.

Apply the suggested daily and/or total budgets provided in the Scheduling & Budget section by clicking **Apply**.

### Email alerts

You can sign up to receive email alerts when your campaigns run out-of-budget or are labeled as budget-at-risk.

To change your out-of-budget email notification preferences (opt-in or opt-out), use the **email** icon in the top right corner of the All Campaigns page. Select to either **Receive Out-of-budget email notifications** or **Do not receive Out-of-budget email notifications** and click **Change**.

### Key metrics and insights

You can view all the following data points as part of out-of-budget features:

- Campaign Status: The current state of the campaign’s budget, including Out-of-budget and Budget-at-risk.
- Sugg. Daily Budget: Suggested daily budget is a recommended daily amount to spend on the campaign to minimize the possibility of running out -of -budget, and missing out on impressions and clicks. The recommendation is based on factors that may include campaign performance, time of day, performance of similar campaigns and historical impressions granted.
- Sugg. Total Budget: Suggested total budget is a recommended total amount to spend on the campaign to minimize the possibility of running out -of -budget, and missing out on impressions and clicks. The recommendation is based on factors that may include campaign performance, time of day, performance of similar campaigns and historical impressions granted.
- Est. Missed Impressions: Estimated missed impressions is the estimated range of impressions a campaign might have received if it had not run out -of -budget. This estimate is based on factors such as current campaign performance, time of day, performance of similar campaigns and historical impressions generated. This is an estimate, not a guarantee of actual impressions. Some campaigns may not have enough data to provide this estimate.
- Est. Missed Clicks: Estimated missed clicks is the estimated range of clicks a campaign might have received if it had not run out-of-budget. This estimate is based on factors such as current campaign performance, time of day, performance of similar campaigns and historical impressions generated. This is an estimate, not a guarantee of actual clicks. Some campaigns may not have enough data to provide this estimate.
- Avg. Cap-out Time: Average cap-out time is the average time of day when the campaign ran out-of-budget within the chosen time window. For example, if you choose a 30-day window, the average cap-out time will be the average of all the times this campaign ran out-of-budget in the last 30 days.

### Out-of-budget scenarios

**I do not see the recommended budget for my out-of-budget/budget-at-risk campaign with the daily remaining budget = zero after 24 hours.**

This is because your campaign may have a rollover budget that is still being spent. The recommendations will be provided once the rollover budget is also exhausted.  

## Frequently asked questions

### When do the out-of-budget features show up in the Walmart Connect Ad Center?

Some out-of-budget features, including alerts, appear in near-real time on the Walmart Connect Ad Center when a campaign’s daily and/or total budget has been spent. Other out-of-budget features, such as recommendations and key metrics or insights are refreshed every 24 hours.

### Where can I view out-of-budget features in the Walmart Connect Ad Center?

Out-of-budget features are integrated throughout the Walmart Connect Ad Center experience for Sponsored Search. You can view out-of-budget features on the Dashboard, the All Campaigns page and the campaign edit page. You can also sign up for out-of-budget email alerts on the All Campaigns page and view key related metrics.

### What happens if I spend my total budget before my campaign's end date?

Your campaign will continue to stay in Live status until the campaign end date is reached, however your ads will not serve until you increase your daily budget.

### When does the Out-of-budget tag get removed from a campaign?

The Out-of-budget tag is removed from a campaign when you increase your daily and/or total budget above its current level. We recommend applying our suggested budget values to help ensure your ads continue to serve.

### What is the meaning of the Budget-at-risk tag on the All Campaigns and campaign edit pages?

The Budget-at-risk tag is used to identify campaigns that have gone out of budget at some point in the past seven days and are currently operating with a suboptimal budget. This means the campaign is at risk of going out of budget again, and we recommend you increase your budget(s) to the suggested value(s).

### Why don’t the metrics for my out-of-budget campaigns on the Attributed data tab and the Near-real time data tab match?

Some of the out-of-budget metrics may not match between the tabs because accepting the suggested budget for a campaign means you’ll see updated metrics on the Near-real time tab before the Attributed tab.

### Why is the total remaining budget still at zero, even after a few minutes of updating the total budget on the Near-real time data tab on the All Campaigns page?

The total remaining budget value takes about 15 minutes to update on the Near-real time data tab. Meanwhile the out-of-budget label is removed as soon as total or daily budget is updated. The budget-at-risk label goes away as soon as budget is greater than or equal to the recommended budget.

### Why don’t I see recommendations for my campaigns that have out-of-budget or budget-at-risk labels, even after 24 hours?

Because your campaign has rollover budget (cannot be more than 2X daily budget) that is still being spent. You will see recommendations after the rollover budget gets exhausted. ### Why do I see suggested total and suggested daily budget for some campaigns but not for all of them?

If a campaign's daily budget runs out, you’ll receive only a daily budget recommendation. You would receive both types of budget recommendations if both budgets ran out and you had defined both types of budgets at the time of campaign setup. 

### Why do I see only a suggested total budget but not a suggested daily budget in the out-of-budget metrics?

If you did not assign a daily budget to your campaign when you created it, your campaign will only receive a recommendation on the suggested total budget.

### Why do I see only a suggested daily budget but not a suggested total budget in the out-of-budget metrics?

This can happen because either your campaign went out of budget when your daily budget ran out (but your total budget is not yet exhausted), or you did not assign a total budget to your campaign when it was created.
