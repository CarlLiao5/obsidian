## Overview

This article contains content and products that must not be featured in any ads purchased through Walmart Connect.  

### Prohibited content

- Advertising that targets or otherwise is appealing to, or otherwise collects any type of information from, children.
- Content depicting children in adult or dangerous situations. For example, ads must not show children close to, or handling, firearms or other weapons, or in situations which may contravene established child safety best practices (such as children in vehicles without their seatbelts fastened or cycling without helmets), or without adult supervision in places or situations where they cannot safely look after themselves (such as swimming pools, crossing busy roads or around dangerous equipment).
- Religious advocacy or disparagement.
	- Mockery, aggression or irreverence toward any religion or religious institutions and the icons/symbols that represent them are strictly prohibited.
- Symbols of the Occult Satan, satanic worship, 666, pentagrams and related symbols, non-literary witchcraft references and/or elements of paganism.
- Political Content/Content Concerning Controversial Social Issues.
	- Support or opposition of any person in an elected or appointed political office, political party, political group, political organization, political committee, political ideologies, political campaign, or political agenda is prohibited.
		- Any content influencing or appealing for a vote, political action, political change, political protest, political demonstration, political donation, political fundraising or political movement is not allowed.
		- We generally do not allow content that promotes any sort of social agenda. This includes content building social awareness, supporting political campaigns or advocating for any sort of social/political change. For example, statements such as "what are you doing to help support the cause?" will result in rejection. However, certain instances of social agenda-related content may be approved on a case-by-case basis. To seek approval for this sort of content, advertisers should reach out to their account manager.
- Deceptive, false or misleading content.
- Content that promotes practices that can result in the physical or mental harm of customers.
	- Antisocial behavior. Content that involves a strong depiction of illegal, reckless or problematic behavior without negative consequences, punishment or proper resolution; elements that are highly imitable and portray activity that is a risk to personal health.
- Content that encourages, glamorizes or depicts excessive consumption of drugs or alcohol.
- Content depicting illegal drugs, drug use, drug paraphernalia, drug addiction or symbolic drug-related gestures that may refer to drug use or the symbols that represent illegal drugs.
- Exploitative or emotionally disturbing content including:
	- Sensitive events such as natural disasters, human-caused disasters, incidents of mass trauma and/or casualties, or the death of public figures.
		- Content that portrays abused humans or animals.
		- Content that includes visuals of hunting.
		- Threatening, abusive, or harassing content.
		- Obscenities or content that is otherwise controversial, defamatory, libelous, illegal, or invasive of another's privacy.
		- Gross or tasteless images, dialogue or overall themes that may be considered insensitive, crude, irreverent, or shocking.
- Content that advocates or discriminates against, or disparages or demeans, a protected group (whether based on race, color, national origin, religion, disability, sex, sexual orientation, disability, age or any similar or another category). This includes content containing negative stereotypes or demeaning images or dialogue that exploits, objectifies or casts any of the aforementioned groups in a pejorative light.
- Profanity, including direct curse words and situations of implied profanity such as:
	- Blurred or obscured letters.
		- Suggestive dialogue.
		- Innuendos and double entendres.
		- Physical representations such as hand gestures.
		- References to offensive bodily noises, reproductive organs, or scatological usages.
		- Obscene gestures.
- Violence. Intense, graphic, excessive, realistic or simulated violence - sometimes even cartoon violence - including strong descriptive dialogue and/or gestures that allude to violence against people, animals, authority figures or one’s self. Content containing extensive and detailed instructions in the use of harmful devices or weapons, or which describe imitable techniques.
- Full or partial nudity, revealing clothing, excessive skin and the display of undergarments (except as explicitly permitted herein).
- Pornography or explicit sexual content. This includes depiction of or desire for sexual activity, sexual gestures, symbolic sexual references, sexual dialogue or innuendo.
- Content that infringes the intellectual property, privacy, publicity, or other legal rights of any person or entity.
- Creatives must not compare products or make references to other competitor brands.
- References to GLP-1s outside of a product's packaging.
- PFAS, PFOA, PFOS, PTFE Free or similar claims.
- Emergency Alert System (EAS) tones or similar sound effects that can be confused with EAS.
- 1-900 phone numbers.
- Spam.

### Prohibited products & services

The following products and services cannot be promoted:

- Any products that are prohibited from being sold at Walmart.
- Tobacco-related products and any products that simulate tobacco smoking (e.g., e-cigarettes, vaporizers and nicotine pouches).
- Illegal or recreational drugs and associated products and services.
- Drug test aids.
- Pill presses, counterfeit dyes and tableting or encapsulating machines.
- Counterfeit goods.
- Ingestible CBD products.
- Gambling, which includes but is not limited to:
	- Online gambling and gambling apps.
		- Sweepstake casinos.
		- Horse racing.
		- Lotteries.
- Weapons, ammunition or explosives.
	- Firearms, firearm parts, accessories and ammunition.
		- Paintball guns, airsoft guns, BB Guns.
		- Knives intended for use as a weapon or defense.
		- Allowed: kitchen knives, craft knives, multi-purpose knives, knives that are one part of a multi-tool.
				- Not Allowed: Camping knives, hunting knives, machetes and other knives that could reasonably be used as a weapon.
		- Fireworks, pyrotechnics and other explosives.
		- Allowed: snappers.
		- Electronic control devices, such as tasers and stun guns.
		- Pepper spray.
- Weapon licenses, certifications and safety courses.
- The following home test kits:
	- Specimen and substance drug test kits.
		- Sexual wellness-related test kits.
		- Forensic test kits.
- Adult-Oriented Products and services, which includes but is not limited to:
	- Sexual enhancers/products aimed at increasing sexual performance or drive.
		- Adult publications, magazines, websites, apps, phone lines, etc.
- Sites or services that promote affairs, infidelity, escorts, prostitution services, mail-order brides and other similarly situated practices.
- Restaurants promoting scantily clad service workers.
- Permanent tattoo and body modification (e.g., branding) products and accessories (e.g., tattoo ink).
	- Exceptions: Temporary/non-permanent tattoos, temporary tattoo ink, care products (e.g., skin creams, care kits).
- False legal documentation (i.e., drivers licenses and passports).
- Counterfeit Goods.
- Products or services principally used to infringe the intellectual property rights of others, such as those designed to bypass copyright protection mechanisms, or to sell counterfeit products and ads which include false testimonials, endorsements, or reviews. (i.e., peer to peer file sharing services).
- Malware, spyware, or any products and services that promote hacking or the wrongful access of personal information or devices.
- Artificial metric boosters and email/information aggregators.
- Cheating services (e.g., essay writing, test taking).
- Solicitation for legal services such as attorneys and class actions.
- Live animals.
	- Exceptions can be made for non-profit foster and adoption services for pets.
- Artifacts (e.g., fossils, remains, relics, tribal items).
- Psychics, mediums, tarot card readings, astrological predictions, and other similarly situated practices.
- Unsanitary products (e.g., expired food, used cosmetics, etc.).
- Funeral items (e.g., caskets, urns).
- Hazardous chemicals, compounds, or contaminated materials.
- Loose gemstones or jewelry that does not comply with applicable laws and regulations.
- Plants or plant seeds subject to any government restrictions.
- Products that have been recalled or are currently subject to a recall.
- Services that make unrealistic promises.
- Financial services that are generally considered predatory are prohibited. This includes:
	- Bail Bonds.
		- Cash or Paycheck Advances.
		- Short-term high interest loans such as payday loans, including:
		- Loans with interest rates above 36%.
				- Loans with repayment required in fewer than 60 days.
		- Penny stocks and bidding fee auctions.
		- Predatory mortgage services such as reverse mortgages and foreclosure relief.
		- Services that promise to change your credit history or score for a fee.
		- Services that claim to recover stolen money or disputed funds.
		- Services that facilitate the buying and selling of specific stocks and bonds.
		- Services that charge upfront fees for uncertain results.
		- Get-rich-quick schemes, multi-level-marketing businesses, and pyramid schemes.
		- Tied-selling practices.
- Bullion.
- Cryptocurrency.
- Solicitation for clinical trials and research participation.
- For profit medical donation services.
- Cosmetic surgeries and esthetician services that require medical supervision.
- UV tanning services and equipment.
